SECTION 02 — CLINICAL PRESENTATION

Clinical Manifestations & Grades of Ascites

❖ Symptoms

▸Progressive abdominal distension
▸Abdominal discomfort, shortness of breath
▸Weight gain
▸Cirrhosis: confusion, GI bleeding (decompensation)
▸Malignant: weight loss
▸Heart failure: dyspnea, orthopnea, peripheral edema
▸Chylous: diarrhea, steatorrhea, malnutrition, nausea, fever, night sweats

❖ Signs

▸Flank dullness, shifting dullness
▸Stigmata of liver cell failure: spider angioma, palmar erythema, abdominal wall collaterals, jaundice, muscle wasting, gynecomastia, leukonychia, parotid enlargement
▸Heart failure: JVD, pulmonary congestion, peripheral edema
▸Chylous: LE edema, lymphadenopathy, cachexia, abdominal masses

❖ Grades of Ascites (International Ascites Club)

Grade 1Mild — detectable only by ultrasound
Grade 2Moderate — moderate symmetrical abdominal distension
Grade 3Large / gross — marked abdominal distension
