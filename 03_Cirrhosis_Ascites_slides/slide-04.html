SECTION 01 — ASCITES BASICS

Pathogenesis of Cirrhotic Ascites

❖ Key Factors

✔Increased total body sodium and water
✔Splanchnic arterial vasodilatation due to clinically significant portal hypertension (HVPG > 12 mmHg)

❖ In Cirrhosis

✔Hepatic dysfunction & increased sinusoidal portal pressure signal the kidney to retain excess sodium and fluid
✔Portal hypertension localizes excess fluid to the peritoneal cavity rather than the periphery

Pathophysiology Pathway

Cirrhosis → Portal hypertension → Reduced albumin → ↓ Oncotic pressure → Reduced aldosterone metabolism → ↑ Aldosterone → Activation of RAAS → Underfilling of circulation → Reduced renal blood flow → Splanchnic vasodilatation → Lymph formation exceeds lymph return → Salt and water retention → Ascites
