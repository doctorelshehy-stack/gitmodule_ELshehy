SECTION 05 — DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

Other Types of Ascites

3) Chylous Ascites

▸Macroscopically turbid / milky (high triglycerides)
▸Causes: lymphangiectasia, lymphoma, cirrhosis (infrequent)
▸Other: retroperitoneal surgery, pancreatitis, sarcoidosis, TB, abdominal trauma
▸Mechanism: hydrostatic hypertension → spontaneous lymphatic rupture

4) Biliary & Pancreatic Ascites

▸Green ascitic fluid (bilirubin >plasma)
▸Suspected after liver biopsy or biliary surgery
▸Exudate (protein >3 g/dL)
▸High pancreatic enzymes → chronic pancreatitis

5) Cirrhotic Hydrothorax

▸5% of cirrhosis patients
▸Pleural effusion (usually right-sided) without pulmonary/pleural disease
▸Mechanism: ascites passes through diaphragmatic defects into pleural space
▸Risk of spontaneous bacterial empyema

Key: SAAG ≥1.1 = portal hypertension. SAAG <1.1 = malignancy, TB, pancreatitis, nephrotic syndrome. Total protein helps classify exudate vs transudate.
