SECTION 05 — DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

Differential Diagnosis of Ascites

1) Malignant Ascites

▸Macroscopically bloody in only 10% of patients
▸Total protein >3 g/dL
▸SAAG <1.1 (low gradient)
▸High LDH and cholesterol
▸Cytology: 60–90% accurate
▸Immuno-cytochemical techniques help differentiate malignant vs mesothelial cells
▸Laparoscopic biopsy may be needed if cytology negative

2) Post-sinusoidal Portal Hypertension

Causes: CHF, constrictive pericarditis, IVC obstruction, Budd-Chiari syndrome

▸SAAG <1.1 (low gradient)
▸LDH and cholesterol not increased
▸Heart failure / Budd-Chiari: usually easy clinical diagnosis
▸Constrictive pericarditis: often lacks CHF symptoms
