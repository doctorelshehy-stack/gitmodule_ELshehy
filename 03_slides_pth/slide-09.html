Approach to Upper GI Bleeding

History — Key Questions

  • Abdominal pain, dyspepsia
  • Hematemesis, hematochezia, melena
  • Features of blood loss: shock, syncope, anemia
  • Features of underlying cause: jaundice, weight loss
  • Medications: NSAIDs, aspirin, corticosteroids, anticoagulants
  • Epistaxis or hemoptysis (rule out non-GI source)
  • Past history: previous UGIB, DM, CAD, CKD, liver disease, COPD
  • Previous abdominal surgery

Examination

General

Vital signs — pulse, BP, respiratory rate, temperature

Shock Assessment

Cold extremities, tachycardia, hypotension, chest pain, confusion, delirium, oliguria

Cirrhosis Stigmata

Palmar erythema, spider nevi, purpura, ecchymosis

Mass / Tumor Signs

Nodular liver, abdominal mass, lymphadenopathy
