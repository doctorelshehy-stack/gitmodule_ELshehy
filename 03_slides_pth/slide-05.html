Clinical Consequences of Portal Hypertension

Major Clinical Consequences

  1. GI bleeding — ruptured gastroesophageal varices
  2. Portal hypertensive gastropathy & colonopathy
  3. Ascites
  4. Hepatorenal syndrome
  5. Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis (SBP)
  6. Hepatopulmonary syndrome
  7. Porto-pulmonary hypertension
  8. Hepatic encephalopathy

Focus: Gastrointestinal Bleeding

UGIB vs LGIB

  • Upper GI (UGIB): from upper esophageal sphincter to ligament of Treitz
  • Lower GI (LGIB): from ligament of Treitz to anal canal (mid-GI + colonic)
  • UGIB is 4× more common than LGIB
  • Higher incidence in males

UGIB Classification

  • Variceal: complication of ESLD / portal HTN
  • Non-variceal: peptic ulcer, erosions, Mallory-Weiss, etc.
