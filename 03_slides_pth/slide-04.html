Portal Hypertension — Definition & Etiology

Definition

Portal hypertension (PH) is a clinical syndrome with pathological increase in portal venous pressure. The normal portal pressure gradient (portal vein to IVC) is 1–5 mmHg. PH is defined when the gradient rises above 10–12 mmHg.

Etiology — Three Categories

1. Prehepatic

  • Portal / splenic vein thrombosis
  • Congenital portal vein stenosis
  • Extrinsic compression
  • Arteriovenous fistula

2. Intrahepatic

  • Liver cirrhosis (most common)
  • Schistosomiasis
  • HCC, Amyloidosis
  • Congenital hepatic fibrosis

3. Post-hepatic

  • Budd-Chiari syndrome
  • IVC thrombosis
  • Constrictive pericarditis
  • Tricuspid valve disease
