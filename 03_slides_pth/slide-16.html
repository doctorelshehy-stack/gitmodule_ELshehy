Key Takeaways

  • ✦ Portal HTN — gradient >10–12 mmHg; prehepatic, intrahepatic (cirrhosis), or post-hepatic causes
  • ✦ UGIB — 4× more common than LGIB; variceal (~20% of PH) and non-variceal (PUD most common)
  • ✦ Resuscitation — crystalloids, blood (Hb 7–9), PPI high-dose, vasoactive drugs for variceal
  • ✦ Esophageal varices — EBL is first-line; sclerotherapy alternative; repeat EBL for eradication
  • ✦ Gastric varices — histoacryl injection (not banding); balloon tamponade, TIPS, surgery as rescue
  • ✦ Non-variceal — thermocoagulation, clips, sclerosants; surgery if endoscopy fails

"Systematic evaluation, prompt resuscitation, and timely endoscopic intervention save lives in upper GI bleeding."
