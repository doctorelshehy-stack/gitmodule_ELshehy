Non-Variceal Upper GI Bleeding

Most Common Causes

  1. Peptic ulcer disease (most frequent)
  2. Mucosal erosive disease of upper GIT
  3. Mallory-Weiss syndrome
  4. Upper GIT malignancies

Management Goals

  1. Hemodynamic resuscitation (same as variceal)
  2. No role for somatostatin/terlipressin in non-variceal bleeding
  3. Stop bleeding via endoscopic therapy
  4. Endoscopic risk stratification (Forrest classification)

Endoscopic Treatment Options

Thermocoagulation

Heat probe or argon plasma coagulation to cauterize bleeding vessels.

Metallic Clips

Mechanical compression of bleeding vessel — effective for visible vessels.

Sclerosant Injection

Epinephrine should NOT be used as sole agent. Combine with other sclerosant.

Surgery

Indicated if bleeding cannot be adequately controlled by endoscopy.
