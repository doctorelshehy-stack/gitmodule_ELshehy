Management of Acute UGIB — Three Goals

Goal 1

Initial Evaluation

  • Hemodynamic status: pulse, BP, anemia
  • History & clinical examination
  • Assess co-morbidity
  • Avoid multi-organ failure
  • Lower mortality

Goal 2

Resuscitation

  • Volume replacement: crystalloids
  • Blood transfusion: target Hb 7–9 g/dL
  • PPI therapy: high-dose IV bolus + infusion (80 mg → 8 mg/h × 72h)
  • Vasoactive drugs: octreotide, terlipressin (if variceal)
  • Nasogastric lavage: confirm bleeding, improve visualization

Goal 3

Identify & Control

  • Endoscopic therapy ASAP after resuscitation
  • Acute variceal hemorrhage occurs in ~20% of PH patients
  • Esophageal band ligation (preferred)
  • Sclerotherapy (if banding unavailable)
  • Gastric varices: histoacryl injection
  • Balloon tamponade / TIPS as rescue
