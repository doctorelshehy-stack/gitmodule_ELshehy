Management of Esophageal Varices

Endoscopic Band Ligation (EBL)

Preferred endoscopic therapy for acute esophageal variceal bleeding

  • Superior to sclerotherapy in all major outcomes
  • Secondary prevention: repeat every 2–4 weeks until varices eradicated

Endoscopic Sclerotherapy

Injection of sclerosant (e.g., 5% ethanolamine oleate) into the varix or submucosa

Complications:

  • Transient fever, mucosal ulceration
  • Esophageal stricture, dysphagia
  • Chest pain, pleural effusion
  • Esophageal perforation, mediastinitis

Key Points

  • EBL is the first-line therapy
  • Sclerotherapy is reserved when banding is unavailable
  • Vasoactive drugs (octreotide, terlipressin) should be started before endoscopy if variceal bleeding suspected
  • Endoscopy should be performed ASAP after resuscitation
  • Secondary prophylaxis with serial EBL reduces rebleeding
