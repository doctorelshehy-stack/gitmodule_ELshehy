Symptoms of Acute Viral Hepatitis (Part 2)

Sometimes, after 3 to 10 days, the following develop:

  • Dark urine
  • Pale stool
  • Jaundice (yellowish discoloration of the skin and whites of the eyes) - sometimes accompanied by itching
  • The liver may be enlarged and tender

Clinical course: Most of the initial symptoms usually disappear within a week, and people feel better even though the jaundice may worsen.

Fulminant Hepatitis

Rarely develops. Severe hepatitis with signs of liver failure. Can occur in HAV, but more likely in HBV (especially with HDV co-infection).

Recovery

People usually recover in 4 to 8 weeks, even without treatment. However, some people infected with HBV or HCV develop chronic infections.

Feature Detail
Prodrome 3-10 days: poor appetite, malaise, N/V, fever
Icteric phase Dark urine, pale stool, jaundice, itching
Convalescence 4-8 weeks; symptoms resolve
