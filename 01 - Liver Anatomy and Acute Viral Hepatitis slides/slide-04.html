Segmental Anatomy of Liver (Recent Classification)

The liver is divided into functional right and left lobes by a line passing from the left of the gallbladder fossa to the left of IVC — Cantlie's line — creating Couinaud's segments.

There are eight segments:

Left Lobe

Segments I, II, III, IV

Right Lobe

Segments V, VI, VII, VIII

Segment I is the caudate lobe of the liver and has independent supply of portal and hepatic veins that directly joins the IVC.

Couinaud's segments of the liver. Segments I-IV (left lobe), V-VIII (right lobe), separated by Cantlie's line. Segment I (caudate) drains directly into IVC.
