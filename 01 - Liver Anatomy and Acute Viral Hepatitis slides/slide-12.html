Laboratory Diagnosis: HAV & HBV

Hepatitis A (HAV)

IgM Anti-HAV

  • Positive at the time of onset of symptoms
  • Results remain positive for 3-6 months after the primary infection

Anti-HAV IgG

  • Appears soon after IgM
  • Generally persists for many years

Hepatitis B (HBV)

Serology

  • HBsAg (Hepatitis B surface antigen)
  • HBcAb IgM (IgM antibody to core antigen)

HBV Viral DNA

  • Most accurate marker of viral DNA
  • Detected by PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction)

Key point: Serological markers for HBV allow differentiation between acute infection (HBsAg +, HBcAb IgM +) and chronic infection (HBsAg + for >6 months).
