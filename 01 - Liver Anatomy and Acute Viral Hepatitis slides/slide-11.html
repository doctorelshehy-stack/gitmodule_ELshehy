Diagnosis of Acute Viral Hepatitis

Initial Evaluation

  • Suspect based on history and physical examination.
  • Liver tests: measure liver enzymes and other liver-produced substances.
  • Blood tests detect viral components or specific antibodies.

Suspecting Fulminant Hepatitis

Doctors suspect fulminant hepatitis if:

  • People are very ill and develop jaundice very quickly
  • Mental function quickly deteriorates
  • Abnormal blood tests of coagulation (PT and INR)

Specific Laboratory Tests by Virus:

HAV

IgM anti-HAV • IgG anti-HAV

HBV

HBsAg • HBcAb IgM • HBV DNA

HCV

Anti-HCV • HCV core Ag • HCV RNA

HEV

Anti-HEV IgM • Anti-HEV IgG • PCR
