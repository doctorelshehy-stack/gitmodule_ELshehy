Definition of Acute Viral Hepatitis

Acute hepatitis is a term used to describe a wide variety of conditions characterized by acute inflammation of the hepatic parenchyma or injury to hepatocytes resulting in elevated liver function indices.

In general, hepatitis is classified as acute or chronic based on the duration of the inflammation and insult to the hepatic parenchyma.

< 6 months

Duration of inflammation

ACUTE HEPATITIS

Normalization of LFTs

≥ 6 months

Persistent inflammation

CHRONIC HEPATITIS

Persistently abnormal LFTs

If the period of inflammation or hepatocellular injury lasts for less than six months, characterized by normalization of the liver function tests, it is called acute hepatitis.
