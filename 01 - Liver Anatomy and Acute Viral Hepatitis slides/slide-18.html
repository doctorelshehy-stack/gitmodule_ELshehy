Summary & Key Takeaways

Key Takeaways

01 Liver Anatomy

Couinaud's 8 segments; Cantlie's line divides functional right/left lobes; Segment I (caudate) drains directly to IVC.

02 Definition

Acute = inflammation <6 months with LFT normalization. Caused by 5 hepatotropic viruses.

03 Symptoms

Flu-like to fulminant liver failure; prodrome 3-10 days; jaundice; recovery 4-8 weeks; chronic risk with HBV/HCV.

04 Diagnosis

Serology (IgM/IgG), antigens, PCR for viral DNA/RNA. HAV: IgM anti-HAV. HBV: HBsAg, HBcAb, DNA. HCV: RNA by PCR. HEV: IgM/IgG, PCR.

05 Prevention

Vaccines for HAV & HBV; HEV vaccine in China only; immune globulin for post-exposure; hand hygiene; safe sex; needle safety.

06 Treatment

Supportive care for most; hospitalization for severe; ICU + antivirals for fulminant HBV; liver transplantation for refractory cases.
