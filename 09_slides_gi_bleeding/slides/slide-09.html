Diagnosis — Imaging Studies

Radionuclide Scans & Angiography

  • Used in hemodynamically unstable patients
  • Performed with or without preceding radionuclide scan
  • Also performed if colonoscopy fails to identify bleeding site
  • Allows super selective embolization as therapeutic option

Angiography is both diagnostic and potentially therapeutic through embolization.

Helical CT Scan

Used when routine workup fails to determine the cause of active GI bleeding.

Criteria for establishing bleeding site:

  1. Vascular extravasation of contrast medium
  2. Contrast enhancement of bowel wall
  3. Thickening of the bowel wall
  4. Spontaneous hyperdensity of peri-intestinal fat
  5. Vascular dilatations
