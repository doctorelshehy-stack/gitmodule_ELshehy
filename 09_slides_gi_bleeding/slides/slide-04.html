Definition & Overview

Definition: LGIB generally means bleeding that originates distally from the ligament of Treitz. It includes mid and colonic bleeding.

Contributes to approximately 20–30% of all GI bleeding cases.

Acute LGIB

Lasting less than 3 days. May result in hemodynamic instability due to abrupt blood loss and cardiovascular system instability.

Chronic LGIB

Slower blood loss. Usually presents with:

  • Anemia (iron deficiency)
  • Mild tachycardia
  • Mild shortness of breath
  • Positive fecal occult blood loss
  • Intermittent melena or bleeding per rectum

LGIB is a frequent cause of hospital admission and a factor in hospital morbidity and mortality. It is distinct from upper GI bleeding in epidemiology, management, and prognosis.
