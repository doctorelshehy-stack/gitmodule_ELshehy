Presentation by Etiology & Age

Young Patient (Infectious/Idiopathic Colitis)

  • Fever
  • Dehydration
  • Abdominal cramps
  • Hematochezia

Older Patient (Diverticular/Angiodysplasia)

  • Painless bleeding
  • Minimal symptoms
  • Mild and intermittent bleeding

Ischemic Colitis

  • Abdominal pain
  • Varying degrees of bleeding
  • CHF, Atrial fibrillation, CRF

Massive LGIB (≥65 years)

  • SBP < 90 mmHg
  • Hb ≤ 6 g/dL
  • Maroon stools or bright red blood per rectum

Colon carcinoma rarely causes significant LGIB. Massive lower GI bleeding usually occurs in patients aged 65+ with multiple medical problems.
