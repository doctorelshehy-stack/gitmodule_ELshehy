Key Takeaways

LGIB Definition

Bleeding distal to ligament of Treitz, contributes to 20-30% of GI bleeding. Distinct from UGIB in epidemiology, management, and prognosis.

Most Common Causes

Colonic diverticula and angiodysplasia. Presentation varies by anatomical source and patient age.

Diagnosis

Colonoscopy is first-line for stable patients. Angiography for unstable. Helical CT when routine workup fails.

Management

3 components: Resuscitation → Localization → Intervention. Surgery when conservative measures fail.

Mid-GI Bleeding

Causes differ by age: <40y (Meckel's, tumors, Crohn's) vs >40y (angiectasia, NSAIDs, celiac, hemobilia). Diagnosis via push endoscopy, double balloon, capsule endoscopy.
