Section 7 • Diarrheal Diseases

Acute Infectious Diarrhea — Viral & Bacterial

1. Acute Viral Gastroenteritis

Self-limited. Norovirus, Rotavirus, Adenovirus, Astrovirus.

Rotavirus:

  • Winter months. Primary infection in infancy → moderate/severe. Adolescent → mild.
  • Invades upper small intestine → villous damage, secondary transient disaccharide deficiency.
  • Clinical: Vomiting 3-4 days, diarrhea 7-10 days.
  • Treatment: Supportive + probiotic (lactobacillus GG) shortens duration.

2. Salmonella Food Poisoning

  • S. Enteritidis & S. typhimurium. Source: poultry, egg products.
  • IP: 8-48 hours.
  • Clinical: Fever + chills, nausea/vomiting, cramping, grossly bloody diarrhea 3-5 days. Reactive (post-infective) arthritis (Reiter's syndrome).
  • Tx: TMP-SMX, ampicillin, Quinolones (if not self-limited).

3. Campylobacter

  • C. jejuni/C. coli. Uncooked poultry.
  • Diarrhea (bloody ~10%), abdominal pain.
  • Post-infection: Guillain-Barre, Reiter's.
  • Tx: Ciprofloxacin 500mg bid 5-7d. Azithromycin 500mg ×3d.

4. Shigellosis (Bacillary Dysentery)

  • Groups: Sh. dysenteriae, flexneri, boydii, sonnei.
  • Severity: sonnei (mild) → flexneri (severe) → dysenteriae (fulminant, death within 48h).
  • Clinical: Fever, chills, abdominal cramps, bloody/mucoid diarrhea, headache, malaise.
  • Tx: ORS/IV fluids. Ciprofloxacin 500mg bid ×3d. Azithromycin 500mg day1, 250mg ×4d for resistant strains.

5. Travelers' Diarrhea & E. coli

  • Causative: ETEC, EAEC, Campylobacter, Shigella, Giardia, non-cholera Vibrio.
  • E. coli serotypes: EPEC, ETEC, EIEC, EHEC (O157:H7), EAEC.

EHEC (O157:H7)

  • Source: contaminated water/milk, undercooked hamburgers, lettuce, apple juice.
  • Clinical: Abdominal pain + bloody diarrhea without fever. Enterotoxins affect bowel + distant organs (glomerular, heart, brain).
  • Antibiotics NOT recommended (↑ risk of HUS complication).
