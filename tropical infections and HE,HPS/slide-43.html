Section 7 • Diarrheal Diseases

Approach to Acute Diarrhea & Amoebic vs Bacillary

Approach (from flowchart)

History & physical → likely noninfectious → evaluate/treat accordingly.
Likely infectious: mild/moderate/severe → fluid/electrolyte replacement → observe → resolves or persists.
If fever ≥38.5°C, bloody stools, ↑ fecal WBCs, immunocompromised/elderly → stool microbiology → pathogen found → specific treatment. No pathogen → empirical treatment + further evaluation.
If no red flags → antidiarrheal agents → observe.

Fecal WBCs

Present: C. difficile, Crohn's, UC, Shigellosis, Salmonellosis, Invasive E. coli, Yersinia.
Absent: Giardiasis, Amebiasis, Viral enteritis, Toxigenic E. coli, Microscopic colitis, Drug-induced.

Indications for Evaluation

Profuse diarrhea with dehydration. Bloody stools. Fever ≥38.5°C. Duration >48h without improvement. Recent antibiotics. Community outbreaks. Severe abdominal pain in >50y. Elderly (≥70y). Immunocompromised.
