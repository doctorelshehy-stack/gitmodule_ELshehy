Section 3 • Brucellosis

Complications & Diagnosis

Complications

A. Musculoskeletal

Spondylitis (lumbar), paravertebral abscess, large joint arthritis, synovitis, bursitis, osteomyelitis

B. Neurological (Neurobrucellosis)

Meningism, meningoencephalitis, neuroretinitis, hydrocephalus

C. Cardiopulmonary

Endocarditis (aortic, even healthy valves), myocarditis, thrombophlebitis, DVT, bronchitis, bronchopneumonia

D. Liver & Spleen

Granulomatous hepatitis, abscess, cholecystitis, hypersplenism

E. Reproductive

Epididymoorchitis

Diagnosis

I. Laboratory

  • CBC: Leucopenia, neutropenia, relative lymphocytosis, variable anemia
  • LFT: Elevated enzymes, elevated ALP if granulomatous hepatitis

II. Bacteriological

  • Blood culture (during fever spike, before antibiotics)
  • Bone marrow culture (may be +ve when blood -ve)
  • Pus/tissue culture, CSF culture (meningitis)

III. Serological

  • Rose Bengal Test: Rapid screening
  • STAT: Titer ≥1/160 supports diagnosis. 4-fold change over 4-12 weeks strongly supports
  • Coomb's test: Measures IgG only
  • ELISA/RIA: IgM and IgG
  • PCR: When isolation difficult or serology inconclusive
