Section 4 • Schistosomiasis

Species, Life Cycle & Pathology

Species

  1. S. Mansoni
  2. S. Haematobium
  3. S. Japonicum

Life Cycle (from diagram)

  1. Eggs: S. mansoni (B), S. japonicum (A) in feces; S. haematobium (C) in urine
  2. Eggs hatch → miracidia → penetrate snail tissue
  3. Sporocysts in snail → cercariae released into water (Infective stage)
  4. Penetrate skin → lose tails → schistosomulae → circulation → portal blood → mature adults
  5. Paired adults migrate to mesenteric venules (A,B) or venous plexus of bladder (C)

Pathology — 3 Stages

1. Invasion

  • Cercarial dermatitis (swimmer's itch)
  • Cercarial pneumonitis (eosinophilic pneumonitis)

2. Ova Deposition & Granuloma

  • S. haematobium: urinary system (ureter, bladder, seminal vesicles, prostate)
  • S. mansoni: large intestine (descending colon, rectum)
  • Granuloma: ova + epithelioid cells, eosinophils, lymphocytes, giant cells → fibrous tissue
  • Soluble antigen leaks → Ag-Ab complexes → immune complex disease

3. Repair by Fibrosis

Granulomatous tissue replaced by fibrosis. Clinical manifestations depend on affected organ.
