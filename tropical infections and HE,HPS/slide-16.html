Section 2 • Enteric Fever (Typhoid)

Chronic Salmonellosis, Vaccines & Treatment

Chronic Salmonellosis

  • In patients with active Schistosomal infection
  • Recurrent bacteremia + prolonged fever
  • Organism penetrates schistosome, multiplies in ceca → nidus for salmonella showers
  • Treatment: Anti-typhoid drugs 10-14 days + Praziquantel 40 mg/kg single dose on 2nd apyrexial day

Vaccines

Ty21a (Live attenuated)

Oral, 4 doses. Min age 5. 50-80% protection. Boost 5 years.

VI Capsule Antigen

IM, 1 dose. Min age 2. 50-80%. Boost 2 years.

General Measures

  • Good nursing, sanitary disposal, high-calorie soft diet
  • Fluid/electrolyte balance, no purgatives
  • Monitor vital signs

Anti-Typhoid Drugs

  • 3rd Gen Ceph: Cefotaxim/Cefoperazone 1gm q8-12h or Ceftriaxone 2gm. 5-7 days post-fever.
  • 4th Gen Ceph: Cefepime 1-2gm q8-12h. 5-7 days post-fever.
  • Fluoroquinolones: Ciprofloxacin 500-750mg q12h, Norfloxacin 400mg q12h, Levofloxacin 500-750mg/day. 10-14 days. Not in pregnancy/lactation/<16yr.
  • Azithromycin: 10 mg/kg (max 500mg/d). For uncomplicated typhoid in children 4-17y.

Carrier Treatment & Surgery

Co-Trimoxazole/Amoxycillin/Ampicillin 1 month. Ciprofloxacin/Norfloxacin 1 month + Probencid. Cholecystectomy if medical treatment fails. 3 negative stool/urine cultures before discharge. Surgery: Intestinal Hge (if conservative fails), perforation, cholecystectomy.
