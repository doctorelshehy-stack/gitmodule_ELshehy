Section 4 • Schistosomiasis

Urinary & Intestinal Bilharziasis

1) Urinary Bilharziasis

  • Urethral: Stricture, abscess, fistula
  • Bladder: Wall thickening/contraction, cystitis cystica, stones, cancer bladder
  • Ureteric: Stricture → hydroureter, hydronephrosis, renal failure. Calculi, secondary infection → pyoureter, pyonephrosis
  • Genital: Nodularity/thickening of spermatic cord. Female: vulva, vagina, uterus, tubes, ovaries (NOT a cause of infertility)

2) Intestinal Bilharziasis

  • Colonic polyposis: Multiple sessile/pedunculated polyps in descending colon & rectum. Core of connective tissue + ova, covered by normal mucosa (never malignant)
  • Bilharzioma: Rare, massive ova deposition → large mass in descending colon (may mimic malignancy)
  • Hepatosplenic: Ova embolized through portal tributaries → portal fibrosis → portal hypertension

Collateral pathways (from image): Caput medusae, esophageal varices, hemorrhoids, retroperitoneal collaterals.

Hepatic Schistosomiasis — Key Concept

Delayed hypersensitivity (T-lymphocytes). Mesenchymal not parenchymal disease. Portal tracts thickened, portal vein tributaries obstructed → portal hypertension. Granuloma → fibrous tissue → periportal fibrosis.
