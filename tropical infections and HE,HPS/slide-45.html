Section 7 • Diarrheal Diseases

Chronic Infectious Diarrhea & SIBO

Chronic Diarrhea — Causative Agents

Bacteria (rare)

SIBO, Salmonella, Shigella, Yersinia, Campylobacter, E. coli (enteroinvasive), C. difficile

Parasites

Giardia, Cryptosporidium, Entamoeba histolytica, Strongyloidiasis

Viruses (Immunocompromised)

Norovirus, CMV, HIV

SIBO — Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth

  • Malabsorption of fats & carbohydrates due to altered gut flora.
  • Normal: lactobacilli, enterococci, gram+ facultative anaerobes.
  • SIBO: replaced by colonic bacteria (coliforms, Bacteroides, Clostridium) → ↑ amidase/protease → altered bile salt absorption → impaired micelle production (fat malabsorption) + ↓ villous enzymes (carbohydrate malabsorption).

SIBO Predisposing Factors

  • ↑ GI pH: PPI therapy, gastrectomy, atrophic gastritis
  • GI stasis/delayed emptying: structural (stenosis, bypass) or functional (pseudo-obstruction, diabetic enteropathy)

SIBO Investigations

  • Gold standard: Small-bowel aspiration + quantitative culture (>10⁵ CFU/ml in proximal jejunum). Complex, not routine.
  • Most applied: ¹⁴C/¹³C D-xylose breath test (positive = ¹⁴C in exhaled breath).
  • Hydrogen breath test: Lactulose challenge. >20ppm H₂ in first 90 min = early peak. False + with rapid transit. False - with non-H₂ producing flora.
  • Therapeutic antibiotic trial when high pre-test probability.
