Section 3 • Brucellosis

Definition, Organism & Classification

Definition

Systemic zoonotic & occupational disease transmitted from animals (cattle, sheep, goats) to humans by Brucella bacteria. Also called Mediterranean fever or undulant fever.

Brucella Organism

  • Gram negative cocco bacilli, intracellular aerobic
  • Non-spore forming, non-motile, non-capsulated

Classification (4 pathogenic species)

  • B. melitensis — important human pathogen
  • B. abortus — important human pathogen
  • B. suis — important human pathogen
  • B. canis — lesser, mild disease

Source & Mode

Source: Animal reproductive organs, shed in urine, milk, semen, placental fluid.
Mode: Ingestion of infected milk/products or direct contact with infected animals.
Risk groups: Veterinarians, slaughterhouse workers, farmers, shepherds, lab workers.
