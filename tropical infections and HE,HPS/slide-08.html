Section 1 • Hepatic Encephalopathy

Prevention, Treatment & Differential Diagnosis

Primary Prevention

  • Pre-emptive lactulose after TIPS
  • Avoid constipation, psychoactive drugs, excessive diuretics
  • Prophylaxis against variceal bleeding & SBP

Secondary Prevention

  • Lactulose compliance → 2-3 BM/day
  • Avoid sedatives/hypnotics in hospital
  • Avoid dehydration from diuretics/lactulose

Treatment

Lactulose: 1st line, non-absorbable disaccharide. Side effects: cramping, diarrhea, flatulence.
Rifaximin: 2nd line for refractory HE. Broad-spectrum antibacterial, minimal absorption.
General: 35-45 kcal/kg/day, protein 1-1.5 g/kg, high fiber.

Differential Diagnosis

Metabolic: Hypoxia, hyponatremia, azotemia, diabetic coma.
Intracranial: Tumors, hemorrhage, meningitis, dementia.
Toxins: Alcohol, hypnotics, tranquilizers, heavy metals.

Hospital management: Airway/breathing/circulation. Evaluate for infection/bleeding/renal failure. CT head for focal signs. NG lactulose 30 cm³ q1-2h until BM. Haloperidol for agitation. Monitor for hypernatremia. Add Rifaximin if no improvement.
