Section 1 • Hepatic Encephalopathy

Pathogenesis & Precipitating Factors

Pathogenesis

  • Nitrogenous substances from gut affect brain function via systemic circulation (decreased hepatic function or portal-systemic shunts).
  • Produce alterations of neurotransmission → motor dysfunction, extrapyramidal symptoms.
  • GABA increased in CSF.
  • Other CNS toxins: endogenous benzodiazepine-like compounds, manganese, oxygen free radicals, opioid peptides, nitric oxide.

Precipitating Factors

  • High protein intake
  • Infections
  • GI bleeding
  • Diuretic overdose
  • Electrolyte disorder
  • Constipation or Diarrhoea

Key insight: Nitrogenous compounds (especially ammonia) from gut adversely affect brain function, gaining access via decreased hepatic function or portal-systemic shunts.
