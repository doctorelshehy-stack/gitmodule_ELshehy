Section 5 • Malaria

Species & Life Cycle

Species

  • P. falciparum — most severe, most deaths
  • P. vivax
  • P. ovale
  • P. malariae

400 million infected/year, >1 million die annually. 25,000 international travelers/year infected. Mortality principally in infants in endemic areas.

Life Cycle (from diagram)

  • Sporozoites from female Anopheles mosquito → liver → reproduce asexually → merozoites → infect RBCs
  • Merozoites → trophozoites → feed on hemoglobin → multiply 6-20 fold every 48h (P. malariae 72h) → RBCs rupture
  • Some → gametocytes (long-lived sexual forms) → taken up by mosquito
  • P. vivax/ovale: Hypnozoites remain in liver → cause disease 3 weeks to >1 year later
  • P. falciparum: Cytoadherence, rosetting, agglutination → sequestration in vital organs (central to pathogenesis, not seen in other malarias)

Drug targets from diagram: Hypnozoite: 8-aminoquinolines, Primaquine. Hepatic schizont: Atovaquone-proguanil, Primaquine. Blood stage: Quinoline derivatives, Antifolates, Antimicrobials, Artemisinin derivatives. Gametocyte: Primaquine.
