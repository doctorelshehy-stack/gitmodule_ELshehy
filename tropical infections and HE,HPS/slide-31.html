Section 5 • Malaria

Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis

  • Gold standard: Asexual forms on peripheral blood smears
  • Thick smear: Detects parasitemia as low as 0.001%
  • Thin smear: Detects <0.05%
  • If high suspicion + negative → repeat q12-24h for 2 days
  • Other: normochromic normocytic anemia, elevated inflammatory markers, thrombocytopenia
  • Antibody-based tests used with increasing frequency

Chloroquine-Sensitive Strains

P. vivax, P. malariae, P. ovale:
Chloroquine: 10 mg base/kg stat → 5 mg/kg at 12, 24, 36h (or 10 mg/kg at 24h, 5 mg/kg at 48h)
Primaquine: 0.5 mg base/kg/day for 14 days to prevent relapse (P. vivax)

P. falciparum — ACT (from image)

Uncomplicated: Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT) worldwide recommended.

  • Artesunate 4 mg/kg/d ×3d + Sulfadoxine/Pyrimethamine single dose
  • Artesunate 4 mg/kg/d ×3d + Amodiaquine 10 mg base/kg/d ×3d
  • Artemether-lumefantrine 1.5/9 mg/kg bid ×3d with food
  • Artesunate 4 mg/kg/d ×3d + Mefloquine 24-25 mg base/kg
  • Dihydroartemisinin + Piperaquine

Special Groups (from image)

  • Pregnancy 1st tri: Quinine + Clindamycin
  • Pregnancy 2nd/3rd tri: ACTs recommended
  • Lactating: ACTs (avoid dapsone, tetracyclines, primaquine)
  • Uncomplicated P. vivax: ACTs + Primaquine (or Chloroquine 25 mg base/kg over 3 days + Primaquine 0.25 mg base/kg ×14d)
  • Severe malaria: Adults: Artesunate IM/IV. Children: Artesunate IM/IV, Quinine, or Artemether
