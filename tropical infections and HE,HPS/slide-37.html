Section 6 • Fever & FUO

FUO — Definition, Classification & Causes

FUO Definition: Fever documented by healthcare provider, lasting ≥3 weeks, temperature >38°C on most days, cause not identified after 3 weeks of outpatient evaluation or 1 week of inpatient evaluation.

4 Categories

1. Classic FUO

2. Health care associated FUO

3. Immune deficient FUO

4. HIV-related FUO

Causes of FUO

A. Infectious

Bacterial: Salmonella, Brucellosis, TB, meningococcal. Spirochaetal: Leptospirosis, relapsing fever, syphilis. Parasitic: Bilharziasis (Katayama), Babesiosis, Malaria. Fungal: Blastomycosis, Histoplasmosis. Rickettsial: Q fever, typhus, RMSF. Viral: CMV, HIV, hepatitis. Local: Dental/subphrenic/hepatic abscess, tonsillitis, mastoiditis, UTI, diverticulitis.

B. Non-Infectious

Immunological: RA, SLE, Behcet. Neoplastic: Leukemia, Lymphoma, Wilms tumor. Metabolic: Gout, Porphyria. Endocrine: Thyrotoxicosis, Addison's. Misc: Liver cirrhosis, FMF, Sarcoidosis, Whipple's, Factitious fever.

Fever + eosinophilia: Katayama fever, Fascioliasis, Trichinosis, Filariasis, Hydatid disease.
