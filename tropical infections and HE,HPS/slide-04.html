Section 1 • Hepatic Encephalopathy

Definition & Classification

Definition: HE is a chronically incapacitating syndrome of neuropsychiatric symptoms, developing in patients with acute and chronic liver dysfunction once other brain diseases are excluded.

Classification (by type of liver dysfunction):

Type A

Acute liver failure

Type B

Porto-systemic bypass
No intrinsic hepatocellular disease

Type C

Cirrhosis & portal hypertension

Etiology: Accumulation of toxins due to liver dysfunction. Major factors: reduced hepatic function/mass + porto-systemic collaterals. Ammonia is the most frequently implicated toxin.
