Section 6 • Toxoplasmosis

Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis

  • Culture: Difficult, only specialized labs
  • Serology: Primary method. IgM, IgG, and antibody avidity levels help determine timing of infection. (IgM can persist >1 year)

From serology diagram: Acute infection: IgM positive, IgG positive later, Low avidity. Chronic infection: IgG positive, IgM negative/positive, High avidity.

Treatment

  • Immunocompetent (lymphadenopathy only): No treatment needed
  • Severe disease (eye involvement): Sulfadiazine 2-4 g daily + Pyrimethamine 25 mg daily + Folinic acid for 4 weeks
  • Congenital: Oral pyrimethamine, sulfadiazine, and folinic acid daily for 1 year
  • Pregnancy: Spiramycin alone or with sulfadiazine. Infected infants treated from birth
