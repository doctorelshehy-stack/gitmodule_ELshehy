Section 4 • Schistosomiasis

Clinical Features by Stage

1. Stage of Invasion

  • Cercarial dermatitis: Severe itching at penetration site
  • Cercarial pneumonitis: 4-6 weeks, patchy lung congestion, eosinophilic sputum, high eosinophilia

2. Katayama Syndrome (Acute)

  • Common: S. japonicum > S. mansoni > rare in S. haematobium
  • In immigrants/visitors first exposure
  • 4-8 weeks post-infection: Fever, urticaria, eosinophilia, arthralgia, myalgia, cough, wheezes, diarrhea, abdominal pain, hepatosplenomegaly
  • Dx: Eosinophilia, serology (ELISA), repeated urine/stool

3. Granuloma Formation

S. haematobium:

  • Terminal hematuria, dysuria, frequency, precipitancy, hemospermia

S. japonicum & mansoni:

  • Dysentery, tenesmus, anal mucosa prolapse, anal fistula/fissures

Dysentery: inflammatory disorder → severe diarrhea with mucus and/or blood, abdominal pain, tenesmus, fever.

4. Stage of Fibrosis

Results in Urinary bilharziasis, Intestinal bilharziasis, and Hepatosplenic bilharziasis (detailed next). Portal hypertension develops when portal pressure >12 mmHg.
