Section 4 • Schistosomiasis

Hepatic Schistosomiasis — Stages & Complications

Clinical Presentation (5 Stages)

1. Hepatomegaly

Liver enlarged, tender due to congestion from ova embolization.

2. Hepatosplenomegaly

Spleen enlarged (hyperplasia + portal HTN). Liver still enlarged.

3. Huge Splenomegaly

Liver shrunken, firm, nodular, not tender. Spleen hugely enlarged.

4. Ascites

Due to portal HTN + hypoalbuminemia + huge splenomegaly.

5. Hepatic Cell Failure & Coma

Terminal: compression of hepatocytes by fibrosis/ascites + reduced portal flow → ischemic damage.

Complications

  1. Huge splenomegaly: Hypersplenism → monocytopenia/pancytopenia. Splenic pain, thrombosis. Easy rupture. Divarication of recti, abdominal hernias.
  2. Esophageal varices: Rupture → fatal hematemesis
  3. Ascites complications: SBP, TB peritonitis
  4. Schistosomal glomerulopathy (nephrotic syndrome): Autoimmune → Ag-Ab deposition in glomerular vessels & Bowman's capsule
  5. Chronic salmonellosis
