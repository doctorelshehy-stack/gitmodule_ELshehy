Section 2 • Enteric Fever (Typhoid)

Diagnosis

I. Laboratory

  • CBC: Leucopenia (neutropenia + relative lymphocytosis), variable anemia
  • LFT: Febrile albuminuria, enzymes/bilirubin normal or slightly elevated

II. Bacteriological

Standard: Culture isolation.

  • First 7-10 days: Blood culture (85-90% +ve) or bone marrow culture
  • Third week onward: Fecal, Urine, Tissues, Pus, Bile (Duodenal Aspirate) culture

III. Serological — Widal Test

  • Measures agglutinating antibodies against H and O antigens
  • Agglutinin appears end of 1st week, peaks 2nd-3rd week
  • Diagnostic titer: O-Ab ≥1/80 (non-endemic) or ≥1/160 (endemic)
  • 4-fold rising titer in 2 samples 1 week apart = diagnostic

Other Serologic Tests

  • IHAT, Indirect fluorescent VI-Ab, Indirect ELISA, Monoclonal Ab
  • PCR: Sensitivity 82.7%, specificity 100% (not widely available)

Widal Limitations: Single test little relevance in endemic areas. False +ve with prior vaccination/infection. Cross-reactivity. Cannot distinguish current vs past infection. False -ve with early antimicrobial treatment.
