Section 2 • Enteric Fever (Typhoid)

Complications

A. GIT

Typhoid ulcer • Intestinal hemorrhage • Perforation • Paralytic ileus • Hepatitis • Liver abscess • Cholecystitis

B. Cardiopulmonary

Myocarditis • Pericarditis • DVT with pulmonary embolization • Bronchitis/Bronchopneumonia

C. Hematological

Anemia • Bone marrow suppression • Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome

D. Renal

Urine retention • Febrile albuminuria • Cystitis • Nephritis • Nephropathy • Bacilluria

E. Neurological

Meningism/meningitis • Delirium → coma (Typhoid Encephalopathy) • Psychosis • Brain abscess • Peripheral neuritis

F. Musculoskeletal

Periostitis • Osteomyelitis • Arthritis (hip) • Bone abscess • Zenker's degeneration

G. Relapse

Symptoms return ~1 week after antibiotic treatment finishes.

H. Typhoid Carrier

Asymptomatic source of infection. Convalescent or chronic (>6 months). Fecal or urinary.
