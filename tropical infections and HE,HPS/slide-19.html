Section 3 • Brucellosis

Pathogenesis & Clinical Picture

Pathogenesis (3 steps)

  1. Enters body → regional LNs → bloodstream (primary bacteremia) → RES (liver, spleen, LNs, BM). Intracellular location protects from defenses.
  2. Multiply in ER → released into blood (secondary bacteremia = end of incubation) with hemolysins & cell necrosis.
  3. Cycle repeats with bacteremia/fever undulation until diagnosis/treatment, death, localization or chronicity. Inflammation, abscess or granuloma formation.

I. Subclinical

Asymptomatic. Serology -ve/low. Blood culture -ve. PCR -ve.

II. Acute (<3mo) / Subacute (3-12mo)

Abrupt/insidious. Malaise, headache, weakness, fatigue, anorexia, muscle/joint pain, spondylitis. Undulant fever/rigors. Lymphadenopathy, splenomegaly, hepatomegaly. Dyspepsia, constipation, diarrhea, vomiting.

III. Relapsing (~10%)

Recurrence of symptoms. Greater in inadequately treated. Even in properly treated due to intracellular location.

IV. Focal (Localized)

Any organ: CNS, CVS, Musculoskeletal, Liver, Spleen, Genitourinary.

V. Chronic (>1yr)

Fever in 50%. Blood culture -ve. Serology may be -ve.
