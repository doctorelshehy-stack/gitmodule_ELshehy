Section 6 • Fever & FUO

FUO — Approach, Investigations & Treatment

A) History

  • Duration, spikes per day, timing, returns to normal?
  • Travel history (typhoid, malaria, TB, RMSF)
  • Animal exposure (cat-scratch, rat-bite, leptospirosis)
  • Unpasteurized dairy (brucellosis)
  • Rashes, conjunctivitis, mucosal changes, arthritis
  • Cough, weight loss, lymphadenopathy

B) Physical Exam

  • Rectal temp, RR, HR, BP
  • Pharynx, tympanic membranes, chest auscultation
  • Dental exam (caries, periodontal disease)
  • New cardiac murmur (rheumatic fever, endocarditis)
  • Generalized lymphadenopathy (EBV, CMV, toxo, HIV)
  • Joint exam, skin rashes

C) Investigations

Level 1

  • ESR, CBC with differential, Metabolic panel (BUN, Cr, LFT, LDH, uric acid)
  • Urinalysis + culture, CXR + tuberculin test
  • EBV + CMV serology, Viral culture (if warranted)

Level 2

  • Echocardiogram (Kawasaki, SLE pericardial effusion)
  • Abdominal/pelvic CT (occult abscesses/granulomas)
  • Bone scan (osteomyelitis, discitis)
  • Serology: ANA, dsDNA, complement (if SLE suspected)

Level 3

  • Bone marrow aspiration (infiltrative process or culture for brucellosis, TB, fungus)
  • Biopsy of skin lesions or lymph nodes
  • Endoscopy + biopsy

Treatment

Symptomatic + specific according to cause: antibiotics for bacterial, antituberculous for TB, surgical drainage for pus, praziquantel + steroids for Katayama fever.
