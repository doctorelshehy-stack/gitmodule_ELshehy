Section 1 • Hepatopulmonary Syndrome

HPS — Definition, Etiology & Clinical Diagnosis

Definition: Increased alveolar-arterial gradient on room air with evidence of intrapulmonary vasodilators (IPVDs) in patients with liver disease, without intrinsic lung disease.

Etiology

  • Why some cirrhotics develop HPS is unclear
  • Can occur in noncirrhotic portal hypertension & ischemic hepatitis
  • No correlation between hypoxemia degree and Child-Pugh score

History

  • Chronic liver disease symptoms: fatigue, confusion, abdominal distension, ankle swelling, GI bleeding
  • Shortness of breath worsened by standing upright

Physical Exam

  • Stigmata of CLD
  • Normal heart/lung exam
  • Spider nevi more numerous in HPS
  • Central cyanosis if very low O₂ sat
