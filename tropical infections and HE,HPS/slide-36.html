Section 6 • Fever & FUO

Fever — Definition, Types & Patterns

Definition

  • Normal body temperature: 36.6-37.2°C
  • Regulated by thermosensitive neurons in preoptic/anterior hypothalamus
  • Fever: Controlled increase above hypothalamic set point. Rectal >38°C
  • Hyperpyrexia: >40°C
  • Circadian rhythm: lower in early morning
  • Skin temp 0.4°C lower than oral; Rectal/eardrum 0.4°C higher than oral
  • Oral temp >37.8°C = fever

Types of Fever (Patterns)

  • Intermittent: Touches normal for few hours/day. Seen in malaria, acute pyelonephritis, boils.
  • Remittent: Fluctuates >1.5°F in 24h without touching normal.
  • Continuous: Does not touch normal, fluctuates <1.5°F/day. Seen in enteric fever, bacterial endocarditis, viral pneumonia.
  • Relapsing: Periods of fever alternating with normal temperature.

Why fever spikes at night?

Cortisol is higher during day → suppresses immune activity. When cortisol goes down at night, immune system is more active → fevers spike at night.

Danger levels: 39.4°C (103°F) → may indicate serious illness. >41°C (105.8°F) → damaging to organs. 44°C (111.2°F) → almost certainly death. Max survival record: 46.5°C (115.7°F).
