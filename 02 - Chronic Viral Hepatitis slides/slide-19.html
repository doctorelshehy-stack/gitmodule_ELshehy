HCV — Prevention & Treatment

Prevention & Treatment of Chronic Hepatitis C

Prevention

Only general prophylaxis is possible:

  • Blood, tissue, and organ screening
  • Safe injection practices
  • Infection control in healthcare settings

No active or passive immunizing agent (vaccine) is available.

Treatment — Past vs. Present

Past (Interferon-based)

  • Interferon based therapy
  • Low efficacy against the most common HCV genotypes
  • Treatment often was not curative
  • Patients often experienced significant side effects

Present (DAAs)

  • Direct Acting Antivirals (DAAs)
  • Curative (95%) for most patients and most genotypes
  • Provide improved patient quality of life
  • Fewer side effects
  • Shorter treatment duration (8–24 weeks)

Key breakthrough: The advent of DAAs revolutionized HCV treatment, transforming it from a difficult-to-treat infection with poor outcomes to a highly curable disease with minimal side effects.
