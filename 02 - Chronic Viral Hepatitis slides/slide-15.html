Chronic Hepatitis C — Causative Agent & Epidemiology

HCV — The Causative Agent & Epidemiology

Causative Agent

  • Hepatitis C virus (HCV) — an RNA virus
  • Genus Hepacivirus in the family Flaviviridae
  • In addition to replicating in the liver, the virus can multiply in lymphocytes
  • There are 6 genotypes
  • Genotype 4 is the most common cause in Egypt

Epidemiology

180

Estimated million persons worldwide chronically infected with HCV

HCV Genotypes

6 genotypes worldwide. Genotype distribution varies geographically and influences treatment response.

Egypt Specific

Genotype 4 is the predominant genotype in Egypt, historically linked to past schistosomiasis treatment campaigns using parenteral antimony.
