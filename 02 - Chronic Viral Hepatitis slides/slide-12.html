Chronic Hepatitis B — Prevention

Prevention of Chronic Hepatitis B

General Prevention

  1. Blood safety strategies — quality-assured screening of all donated blood and blood components.
  2. Safe injection practices.
  3. Safer sex practices — e.g., using condoms.
  4. Screening for HBV in high-risk groups:
• Household & sexual contacts of HBsAg-positive persons
• Persons who have ever injected drugs
• Sexual risk (e.g., multiple partners)
• Individuals infected with HCV or HIV
• Patients undergoing renal dialysis
• All pregnant women
• Persons needing immunosuppressive therapy

Specific Prevention

1. Vaccination

Highly effective recombinant vaccines are available. Given by injection into muscle. Routine vaccination recommended for all children and adults at high risk.

2. HBIG

Hepatitis B Immune Globulin given within 48 hours of exposure. Also given to neonates whose mothers are HBsAg and HBeAg positive.
