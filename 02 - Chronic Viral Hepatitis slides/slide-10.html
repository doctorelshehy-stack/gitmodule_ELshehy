HBV — Diagnosis: Serology Markers

Diagnosis of Chronic Hepatitis B — Serology

Marker Clinical Significance
HBsAg First marker to appear in blood after infection. Disappearance of HBsAg and appearance of anti-HBs signals recovery from HBV infection and non-infectivity.
Anti-HBs (HBsAb) Appears after HBsAg disappears. Signals recovery and immunity.
Anti-HBc (IgM) IgM anti-HBc appears shortly after HBsAg becomes detectable. HBcAg alone does NOT appear in serum. May persist for 3–6 months or longer.
Anti-HBc (IgG) Also appears during acute hepatitis B but persists indefinitely.
HBeAg Appears concurrently with HBsAg or soon afterwards. Indicates viral replication and infectivity. Persistence beyond 3 months indicates increased likelihood of chronic hepatitis B.

Acute Infection Profile

HBsAg +, IgM anti-HBc +, HBeAg +

Chronic Infection Profile

HBsAg + >6 months, IgG anti-HBc +, HBeAg + (active replication)
