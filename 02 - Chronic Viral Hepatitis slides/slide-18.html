Chronic Hepatitis C — Diagnosis

Diagnosis of Chronic Hepatitis C

1. Serology

HCV Antibody (Anti-HCV)

  • ELISA used to diagnose hepatitis C infection.
  • Not useful in the acute phase as it takes at least 4 weeks to appear.
  • Cannot distinguish acute from chronic infection.

HCV Core Antigen

  • An EIA for HCV antigen is available.
  • Used in the same capacity as HCV-RNA.

2. PCR — HCV RNA

Most accurate marker for HCV infection.

  • Detects viral RNA
  • Confirms active infection
  • Used to monitor treatment response
  • Sustained virologic response (SVR) = cure

3. Liver Chemistry Tests

Key tests: AST, ALT, P.T. (prothrombin time).

4. Liver Biopsy

Used to determine:

  • Grade — degree of inflammation
  • Stage — degree of fibrosis

Note: Anti-HCV by ELISA is the initial screening test. Positive results are confirmed by HCV RNA PCR. HCV core antigen can be used as an alternative to PCR in some settings.
