HBV — Diagnosis: PCR, Biopsy & Others

Diagnosis of Chronic Hepatitis B — Other Methods

2. Liver Chemistry Tests

Key tests include:

  • AST (aspartate aminotransferase)
  • ALT (alanine aminotransferase)
  • ALP (alkaline phosphatase)
  • Total Bilirubin

3. Histology

Immunoperoxidase staining is used to detect HBV antigens in liver tissue.

4. HBV Viral DNA (PCR)

Most accurate marker for HBV infection. Detected by PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction). Quantifies viral load.

5. Liver Biopsy

Used to determine:

  • Grade — degree of inflammation
  • Stage — degree of fibrosis

Summary of HBV Diagnostic Approach: Serology (HBsAg, anti-HBs, anti-HBc, HBeAg) → Liver chemistry (AST, ALT, ALP, bilirubin) → HBV DNA by PCR → Liver biopsy for grade & stage.
