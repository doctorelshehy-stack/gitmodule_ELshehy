Fistula — Classification & Treatment

Classification (by Anatomy)
Inter-sphincteric (45%)
• Penetrates through internal sphincter only
Trans-sphincteric (30%)
• Passes through both internal and external sphincters
Supra-sphincteric (20%)
• Through internal, then superiorly above external
Extra-sphincteric (5%)
• Rectum to perineum, lateral to both sphincters
Evaluation
• MRI (>90% sensitivity — most sensitive)
• Endoanal ultrasound
• Complete rectal exam
• Assess baseline fecal continence
Treatment Modalities
1) Fistulotomy:
• Open tract, possibly divide sphincter
• 90% healing for simple fistulas
2) Seton Placement:
• Complex fistulas, two-stage technique
• Stage 1: sepsis control
• Stage 2: fistulotomy
3) Endorectal Advancement Flap:
• Close internal opening
• Debride tract
• Mobilize anorectal mucosa over defect
4) LIFT:
• Ligation of inter-sphincteric fistula tract
• For simple and complex fistulas
5) Fibrin Plug & Glue:
• Collagen matrix blocks internal opening
6) Photodynamic Therapy (PDT):
• Light energy + photosensitizers
• Photooxidative damage to target tissue
