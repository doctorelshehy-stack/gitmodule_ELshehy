Oral Cavity Neoplasm

Anatomy & Incidence
• Extends from lips to junction of soft palate + circumvallate papillae
• Common sites: floor of mouth, lateral tongue, retromolar trigone
• M > F, predominantly elderly (>60 years)
• Female incidence increasing (younger patients)
Risk Factors
• Tobacco, HPV, EBV, Alcohol
• Plummer-Vinson syndrome, areca nut
• Poor nutrition
Pre-malignant Lesions
Leukoplakia: White patch/plaque that cannot be characterized clinically or pathologically
• Smooth, wrinkled, fissured, or nodular surface
Erythroplakia: Bright red plaque that cannot be characterized clinically or pathologically
• Irregular outline, may coexist with leukoplakia
~90% are Squamous Cell Carcinoma
• Lateral tongue, oropharynx, floor of mouth
Spread
• Direct: fascial planes, periosteum as barriers
• Perineural invasion → poor prognosis
• Angioinvasion → distant metastases (oral tongue)
Clinical Features (>4 weeks)
• Oral swelling, sore tongue
• Jaw/facial swelling
• Unexplained tooth mobility
• Mouth ulceration, dysphagia
• Painless neck lump, trismus
TNM Staging (T)
• T1: <2 cm | T2: 2-4 cm | T3: 4-6 cm
• T4: invades adjacent structures
Stages
• Stage 0 (Tis, N0, M0)
• Stage I (T1, N0, M0) → Stage II (T2, N0, M0)
• Stage III (T3/N1, M0)
• Stage IV (T4/N2-N3/M1)
