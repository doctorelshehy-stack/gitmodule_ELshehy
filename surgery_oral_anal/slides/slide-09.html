Malignant Salivary Tumors

Pathology
Mucoepidermoid Carcinoma (commonest)
• From duct epithelium
• Columnar (mucoid) + squamous (epidermoid) cells
Adenoid Cystic Carcinoma (Cylindroma)
• Commonest malignancy of minor salivary glands
• Slow growth, peri-neural spread (painful)
• Swiss-cheese pattern: myoepithelial + epithelial cells
Acinic Cell Carcinoma: Serous acini, parotid only
Adenocarcinoma: Bad prognosis
Spread
• Direct: mandible, masseter, facial nerve (parotid)
• Lymphatic: parotid/submandibular → upper deep cervical
• Blood: RARE, LATE (lung, bone)
Clinical Features
• Slow growing, well circumscribed
• Pain radiating to ear, worse with mastication
• Facial nerve paralysis (suspicion of malignancy)
Signs:
• Firm/hard, irregular, nodular, ill-defined
• Infiltration of skin/vessel/nerve/LN
• Cervical LNs: stony hard, mobile → fixed
Suggest Malignancy
• Pain, rapid growth, nerve weakness
• Paresthesia, cervical lymphadenopathy
• Fixation to skin/muscles
Staging (T)
• T1: <2cm | T2: 2-4cm | T3: 4-6cm
• T4a: skin/jaw/ear/nerve | T4b: skull/arteries
Treatment
• En bloc surgical excision
• Superficial tumor → lateral lobectomy (preserve facial nerve)
• Deep lobe → total parotidectomy
• High-grade → elective neck dissection
• Facial nerve: preserve if possible, repair if injured
• Postop RT: high-grade, perineural invasion, extra-glandular
• Inoperable → palliative resection + RT
