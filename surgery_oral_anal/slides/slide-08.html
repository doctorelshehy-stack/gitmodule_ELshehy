Pleomorphic Adenoma

Pathology
Incidence:
• Commonest salivary tumor
• More common in male
• More in superficial parotid
• Malignant transformation: 2-3% (after >10 years)
Macroscopic:
• Firm, irregular, lobulated, encapsulated
• Tumor cells penetrate capsule (incomplete)
• Multi-centric — enucleation has high recurrence
Microscopic:
• Epithelial cells in sheets + duct-like structures
• Blue-stained mucinous matrix (pseudo-cartilaginous)
Clinical Features
Symptoms:
• Painless, slowly growing swelling
Signs:
• Irregular shape, firm, freely mobile
• Not attached to skin/muscle/bone
• Elevating lobule of the ear
• NO cervical LN or facial nerve involvement
Investigations:
• CT (deep parotid tumors)
• FNAC
Warthin's Tumor:
• 2nd most common benign parotid
• Hot spot on ⁹⁹ᵐ Tc scan (high mitochondrial content)
• No malignant potential → may be monitored
Treatment
Parotid (Benign):
• Superficial parotidectomy
(excise all tissue superficial to facial nerve)
• Total conservative parotidectomy
(deep lobe tumors, recurrent)
Submandibular (Benign):
• Submandibular sialoadenectomy
• Preserve 3 nerves:
— Cervical + mandibular facial nerve
— Lingual nerve
— Hypoglossal nerve
Key: Excision with safety margin
• Lower recurrence than enucleation
