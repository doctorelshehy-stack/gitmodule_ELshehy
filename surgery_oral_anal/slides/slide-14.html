Perianal Abscess & Fistula

Perianal Abscess
Etiology:
• Obstruction/infection of glandular crypts
• Crohn's disease (complex, recurrent)
Presentation:
• Dull aching → throbbing anal pain
• Fever, chills, constipation/diarrhea
• Purulent discharge (if draining)
• Erythema + induration on exam
Treatment:
• Timely incision and drainage
• Antibiotics alone: INADEQUATE
Complications:
• Sepsis, Recurrent abscess
• Fistula formation
Fistula-in-Ano
Definition: Epithelial-lined tract connecting anal canal to perianal skin
Etiology: Glandular obstruction → abscess → fistula
Simple:
• Single subcutaneous tract
• <30% external sphincter involvement
• Lowest recurrence/complication rates
Complex:
• >30% external sphincter
• Multiple tracts, recurrent
• Associated with Crohn's, radiation
Goodsall Rule:
• Anterior opening → straight radial tract
• Posterior opening → curved tract to posterior midline
