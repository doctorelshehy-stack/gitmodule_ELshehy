Oral Cancer — Treatment

Investigations
• Incisional biopsy (all cases)
• MRI (investigation of choice)
— Not distorted by dental restorations
— Excellent soft-tissue visualization
• CT: bony invasion, thorax/abdomen
• FNAC: cervical lymph nodes
• US: adjunct to FNAC
• OPG: dental assessment, bony invasion
General Principles
• Primary surgery is standard
• Surgery preferred if bone invaded
• Bulky disease → surgery + postop RT
Tongue Cancer:
• T1 (<2cm): 30% occult metastasis
• → Selective neck dissection or RT
• 2-cm margin in all planes
• Advanced: major resection + reconstruction
• T4: total glossectomy if crosses midline
Reconstruction:
• Small defects: primary closure / secondary intention
• Larger defects: radial forearm flap
(microvascular anastomosis)
