Anal Canal — Anatomy

Key Structures
Anorectal Ring:
• Demarcation between anal canal and rectum
• ~5 cm from anus
• Puborectalis muscle forms sling posteriorly
Anal Canal:
• Length: ~4 cm (2/3 above, 1/3 below dentate line)
• Completely extraperitoneal
• Anoderm: sensitive keratinized squamous epithelium
Pectinate Line:
• Transition: proctodeum below, gut above
• Anal valves at inferior-most ends of anal columns
Anal Columns & Cushions
• 6-10 longitudinal mucosal folds (Morgagni)
• Anal cushions at 3, 7, 11 o'clock positions
• Contain branches of superior rectal artery/vein
• When prominent → internal hemorrhoids
Muscles
• Internal sphincter: smooth muscle, 2.5-4 cm
• External sphincter: skeletal muscle from levator ani
• Levator ani + coccygeus: pelvic diaphragm
Blood Supply & Drainage
• Portosystemic venous connection
• Superior rectal artery + middle/inferior rectal arteries
• Lymphatics → superficial inguinal lymph nodes
Perianal Lesions (Clock Position)
• Anal canal skin (anoderm): fissure, hematoma, fistula opening, skin tag
• Anal canal mucosa: internal hemorrhoids, cancer
• Perianal skin: external hemorrhoids, cancer
