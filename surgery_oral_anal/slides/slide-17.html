Anal Neoplasm & Pilonidal Disease

Anal Neoplasm
Most common: Squamous cell carcinoma
• HPV infection is major etiology
Other neoplasms:
• Basal cell carcinoma
• Melanoma, Adenocarcinoma
Pre-malignant lesions:
• Bowen's disease → precursor to SCC
• Paget's disease → precursor to adenocarcinoma
Pilonidal Disease
Overview:
• M > F (2:1), ages 15-30
• Sacro-coccygeal region
• Acute: abscess under tension
• Chronic: intermittent discharge from sinus(es)
Predisposing factors:
• Obesity, deep gluteal cleft
• Hairy thick skin, poor hygiene
• Prolonged sitting
Clinical Features:
• Infected sinus with pits in midline
• Hair debris exiting from pits (easily removed with forceps)
Pilonidal Treatment
1) Simple Incision:
• Resect pits, abrade cavity
2) Phenol Treatment:
• Antiseptic + keratolytic properties
• Curettage first, then inject phenol
3) Excision without closure:
• Resect all/part of infected sinus
• Aim to avoid recurrence
4) Excision with closure:
• Primary intention healing
• Techniques:
— Direct midline suture
— V-Y or Z-plasty advancement flaps
— Rhomboid transposition flap
