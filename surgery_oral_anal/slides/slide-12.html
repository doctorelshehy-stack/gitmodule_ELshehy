Hemorrhoids — Overview

Definition & Theories
• Clusters of vascular tissue, smooth muscle, connective tissue
• Located in 3 anal cushions: left lateral, right anterior, right posterior
Theories:
• Anorectal varices
• Sliding anal canal lining (cushion deterioration)
Contributing factors:
• Advancing age, strenuous lifting
• Straining with defecation, prolonged sitting
Classification
Internal (above dentate line):
• Painless bleeding
• Bright red blood on stool
• Prolapse, mucus discharge
External (below dentate line):
• Pain when thrombosed
• Perianal fullness, irritation
Grading
• Grade I: No protrusion, may bleed
• Grade II: Protrude with defecation, reduce spontaneously
• Grade III: Protrude, manual reduction needed
• Grade IV: Permanently prolapsed
Key Symptoms:
• Painless bleeding (most common)
• Tissue prolapse
• Pain (thrombosed/strangulated)
