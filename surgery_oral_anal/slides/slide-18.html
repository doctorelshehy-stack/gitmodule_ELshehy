Summary — Oral Cavity & Anal Canal

Oral Cavity
• Sialadenitis: antibiotics, stone removal, salivary substitute
• Oral manifestations: GERD, IBD, Celiac, Gardner, Behçet
• Oral cancer: 90% SCC, MRI is investigation of choice
• Tongue cancer: 2-cm margin, selective neck dissection
Salivary Glands
• Parotid: 70% of tumors, 80% benign
• Pleomorphic adenoma: commonest, enucleation = high recurrence
• Mucoepidermoid: commonest malignant
• Adenoid cystic: peri-neural spread, painful
Anal Canal
• Anal fissure: posterior midline 75%
• Acute: GTN/nifedipine | Chronic: sphincterotomy (gold standard)
• Hemorrhoids: Grade I-IV, band ligation / hemorrhoidectomy
• Abscess: timely incision & drainage (antibiotics alone inadequate)
Fistula
• Inter-sphincteric 45%, Trans-sphincteric 30%
• Simple: fistulotomy (90% healing)
• Complex: seton, advancement flap, LIFT, fibrin plug
Rectal Prolapse & Pilonidal
• Prolapse: laparoscopic rectopexy or perineal procedures
• Pilonidal: excision with/without closure, phenol, flaps
