Anal Fissure (Fissure-in-Ano)

Definition & Sites
• Linear/oval tear in anal canal
• From below dentate line to anal verge
Positions:
• Posterior midline: 75% (M=F)
• Anterior: 24% (female after delivery)
• Atypical: 1% (Crohn's disease)
Acute vs Chronic
Acute:
• Anal pain, spasm
• Bleeding with defecation
Chronic (>6-8 weeks):
• Exposed internal sphincter fibers
• Hypertrophied anal papilla (proximal)
• Skin tag / sentinel pile (distal)
Treatment
Non-operative (Acute):
• High-fiber diet, fluids, laxatives
• GTN topical (relax sphincter)
• Nifedipine topical (CCB)
• Topical local analgesics
Operative (Chronic):
• Lateral internal sphincterotomy
— Gold standard
— Divide 1-2 cm of internal sphincter
• Advancement flaps
• Fissurectomy + sphincterotomy
Crohn's:
• Decrease diarrhea, bulk stool
• Topical medications
