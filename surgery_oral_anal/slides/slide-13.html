Hemorrhoids — Treatment

1) Conservative
• Lifestyle & dietary modification
• Oral fluid intake
• Reduce fat consumption
• Avoid straining
• Regular exercise
• Topical local anesthetics
2) Office-based (Internal)
• Rubber band ligation
• Sclerotherapy
• Infrared coagulation
Goals: decrease vascularity, reduce redundant tissue
3) Surgical
Indications:
• Failed conservative measures
• Symptomatic Grade IV
• Strangulated internal
• Symptomatic Grade III
Types:
• Surgical hemorrhoidectomy
• Stapled hemorrhoidopexy
• Doppler-guided HAL
Crohn's Disease Consideration
• Distinguish hemorrhoids from hypertrophic skin tags (Crohn's)
• Skin tags: tender, associated with ulceration
• Keep treatment conservative — avoid surgery (poor wound healing)
