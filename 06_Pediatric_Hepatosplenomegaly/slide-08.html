Splenomegaly — Definition & Evaluation

Definition

Spleen is normally palpable in 30% of newborns and in 15% of children below 6 months.

Splenomegaly = spleen palpable more than 1 cm below the costal margin in a child more than 6 months of age.

Evaluation

• Splenomegaly is evaluated by ultrasound

• Plotted into special age-correlated charts

• Splenomegaly with minimal hepatomegaly can be due to: infections, portal hypertension, infiltrative disease, disorders of immune regulation, hemolytic disorders

Classification by Size

Massive

Extends below umbilicus or into pelvis

Moderate

2–4 cm below costal margin

Mild

1–2 cm below costal margin
