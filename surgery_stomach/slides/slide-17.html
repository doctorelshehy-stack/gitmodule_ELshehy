Carcinoma — Diagnosis

Diagnostic Modalities
Gastroscopic Biopsy (Gold Standard)
• Flexible upper endoscopy
• Multiple biopsies around ulcer crater
• Note size, location, morphology
Barium Radiography
• Double-contrast: 90% accuracy
• Cannot distinguish benign from malignant
CT Scan
• Detects intraluminal tumors
• Thickened gastric wall
• Direct invasion assessment
• Enlarged lymph nodes
Additional Studies
PET Scan
• Detecting metastatic disease
• Measuring response to chemotherapy
Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS)
• Staging assistance
• Gastric wall invasion depth
• Local nodal status
Laparoscopy
• Recommended with lymphadenopathy on CT
• Tumors at GEJ or diffuse
Tumor Markers
• CEA, CA199, CA72-4, α-fetoprotein
• Not useful for diagnosis (15-60% elevated)
• Used for: recurrence detection, residual tumor
