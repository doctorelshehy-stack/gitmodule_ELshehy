Gastric Carcinoma — Overview

Incidence
• 2nd cause of cancer mortality worldwide
• Adenocarcinoma: 95% of all gastric cancers
• US/Europe: low risk areas
• Asia (Japan/China): high risk areas
• Men more affected, incidence ↑ with age
Etiology
1) H. pylori infection
2) Pernicious anemia
3) Achlorhydria
4) Chronic gastritis
5) History of caustic injury/previous surgery
6) Adenomatous polyps
7) Diet: spirits, excessive salt, ↓ antioxidants
Site
• West: proximal stomach most common
• Japan: distal cancer still predominates
Gross Pathology
• Ulcerated carcinoma (25%): Deep ulcer, everted edges
• Polypoid carcinoma (25%): Intraluminal tumors
• Superficial spreading (15%): Mucosa/submucosa
— Better prognosis
• Linitis plastica (10%): All layers involved
— Early spread, poor prognosis
Histology
Intestinal type
• Well differentiated, glandular
• More common in high-incidence areas
• Older patients
Diffuse type
• Poorly differentiated, signet-ring cells
• Younger patients
• Associated with ABO blood group "A"
• Much worse prognosis
