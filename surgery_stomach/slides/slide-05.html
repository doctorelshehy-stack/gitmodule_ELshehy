Atrophic Gastritis

Definition
• Histopathologic entity characterized by:
— Chronic inflammation of gastric mucosa
— Loss of gastric glandular cells
— Replacement by intestinal-type epithelium
— Pyloric-type glands and fibrous tissue
Causes (Two Types)
1) H. pylori-associated
• Multifocal: antrum + corpus/fundus
• Often asymptomatic
• ↑ Risk of gastric carcinoma
2) Autoimmune
• Restricted to corpus/fundus
• Loss of parietal cell mass
• Anti-IF antibodies → pernicious anemia
Pathophysiology
• H. pylori lodge in mucous layer
• Infection usually acquired in childhood
• Bacterial toxic products → epithelial damage
• Intestinal metaplasia develops
• Corpus atrophy → hypochloridria
• ↑ Serum gastrin → neuroendocrine tumors
Complications
• Gastric ulcers, adenocarcinoma, polyps
• Pernicious anemia
• Malabsorption with megaloblastic changes
• Neurologic manifestations:
— Demyelination, axonal degeneration
— Peripheral nerves, spinal cord, cerebrum
Associated Conditions
• Thyroid diseases, type 1 DM
• Addison disease, myasthenia gravis
• Vitiligo, chronic spontaneous urticaria
