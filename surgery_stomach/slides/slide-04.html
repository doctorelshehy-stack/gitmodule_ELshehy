Gastritis — Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis
• Based on clinical history
• EGD and biopsy of gastric mucosa
• H. pylori investigations
Autoimmune Gastritis Diagnosis
1) Antiparietal and anti-IF antibodies
2) Achlorhydria (basal and stimulated)
3) Hypergastrinemia
4) Low serum B12 (<100 pg/mL)
5) Schilling test: abnormal, corrected by IF
Treatment — Acute
• Remove irritant
• Antacids, H2 antagonists, PPI
• Anti-nausea medications
• H. pylori: antibiotics for up to 2 weeks
Treatment — Chronic
• Eradication of causative infection (H. pylori)
• Pernicious anemia: vitamin B12 injections
• Severe changes: annual upper endoscopy
Prognosis
• Most improve quickly once treatment begun
Remember:
• Painless hematemesis → portal hypertension
• Painful hematemesis → Mallory-Weiss
• Chronic gastritis → ↑ risk of gastric cancer
