Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass

RYGB — Procedure
• Short vertical lesser curvature-based gastric pouch
• Roux limb: retro- or antecolic
• Biliary limb kept short (to reduce vitamin/mineral deficiencies)
• Roux limb length: 100-150 cm
• Bowel continuity restored by Y jejuno-jejunostomy
Advantages
• Effective weight loss
• Improvement in comorbidities
• Well-established procedure
Considerations
• More complex than sleeve
• Risk of marginal ulcers
• Nutritional monitoring required
One Anastomosis Gastric Bypass
• Mini-gastric bypass, longer gastric pouch
• Only one anastomosis (antecolic loop gastrojejunostomy)
• Without Roux-en-Y configuration
Risks: Biliary reflux, marginal ulcers
Bilio-pancreatic Diversion
• Combined restrictive + malabsorptive
• Most effective for weight loss
• Higher risk of nutritional deficiencies
