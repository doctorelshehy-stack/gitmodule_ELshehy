Gastric Outlet Obstruction

Common Causes
1) Gastric cancer
2) Pyloric stenosis from peptic ulceration
Clinical Features
• Long history of peptic ulcer disease
• Pain may become unremitting or disappear
• Vomiting: unpleasant, no bile, food from days ago
• Weight loss, unwell, dehydrated
• Distended stomach visible
• Succussion splash audible
Management
1) Rehydrate with IV isotonic saline + K+
2) Empty stomach (wide-bore tube or lavage)
3) Endoscopy, contrast radiology, biopsy
4) Exclude malignancy
5) Surgical: gastro-jejunostomy ± antrectomy
— Preferred over pyloroplasty
Endoscopic Treatment
• Balloon dilatation
• May result in perforation
• May require multiple sessions
• May not be successful long-term
Key Point:
Always exclude malignancy with biopsy before treating obstruction as benign pyloric stenosis.
