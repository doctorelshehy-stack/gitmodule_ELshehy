Peptic Ulcer Disease — Overview

Most common cause of upper GI bleeding worldwide
Definition
• Discontinuity in mucosa extending through muscularis mucosa
Sites
• Duodenum (especially proximal)
• Stomach (especially lesser curvature)
• Lower esophagus (in GERD)
• Jejunum (after gastrojejunostomy or ZE)
• Meckel's diverticulum (ectopic gastric mucosa)
Pathophysiology
• Balance between acid secretion and mucosal defense
• Injury when balance is disrupted
Aggressive Factors
• NSAIDs, H. pylori, alcohol
• Bile salts, acid, pepsin
• Allow back diffusion of H+ ions
Defensive Mechanisms
• Tight intercellular junctions
• Mucus and bicarbonate
• Mucosal blood flow
• Cellular restitution
• Epithelial renewal
Complications
• Perforation → peritonitis
• Bleeding (up to 15%)
• Gastric outlet obstruction
• ↑ Risk of gastric cancer (H. pylori)
