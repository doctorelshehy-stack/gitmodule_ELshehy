Dumping Syndrome

Definition
• Rapid emptying of gastric content into small bowel
Early Dumping Syndrome
• 15 min to 1 hour after a meal
• Rapid release of hyperosmolar food
• Rapid shift in extracellular fluid
• Systemic hypotension
• Release of vasoactive hormones (bradykinins)
Symptoms — Early
• Nausea, vomiting, epigastric fullness
• Abdominal cramping and diarrhea
• Palpitation, diaphoresis
• Relieved by lying down
Late Dumping Syndrome
• 1 to 3 hours after a meal
• Carbohydrates absorbed quickly
• Blood sugar level rises
• Hyperinsulinemia
• Consequent hypoglycemia
Symptoms — Late
• Fainting, tremor, prostration
• Decreased consciousness
• Relieved by food
Key Difference:
• Early: relieved by lying down
• Late: relieved by food
