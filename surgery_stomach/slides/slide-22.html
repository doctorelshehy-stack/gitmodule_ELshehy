GIST

Overview
• Commonly occur in the stomach
• Arise from interstitial cells of Cajal
• May be benign or malignant
• Biological behavior unpredictable
Malignancy Predictors
• Size > 5 cm
• Mitotic index >10 per 50 fields
Spread
• Peritoneal and liver metastases most common
• Lymph node spread extremely rare
Diagnosis
• Mucosal ulceration → bleeding
• Incidental finding at endoscopy
• Endoscopic biopsy: may be uninformative
— Unless tumor has ulcerated
Treatment
• Surgery: primary treatment if resectable
• Larger tumors:
— May require gastrectomy/duodenectomy
— Lymphadenectomy not required
— Imatinib for 3-6 months before surgery
• Advanced metastatic GIST:
— Imatinib dramatically improved prognosis
— Liver metastasis resection still important
