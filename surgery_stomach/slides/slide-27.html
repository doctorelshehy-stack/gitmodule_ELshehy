Summary — Stomach

Key Points
• H. pylori: most common cause of PUD and chronic gastritis
• NSAIDs: second most common cause of PUD
• Triple therapy: PPI + Clarithromycin + Amoxicillin
• Post-treatment testing mandatory
• Perforation: board-like rigidity, free gas under diaphragm
• Gastric carcinoma: 2nd cause of cancer mortality worldwide
Clinical Pearls
• Graham's patch for perforated ulcer
• Truncal vagotomy + antrectomy for bleeding
• Dumping: early (lying down) vs late (food)
• GIST: imatinib before surgery for large tumors
• Sleeve gastrectomy: current standard for bariatric surgery
• BMI ≥ 40: surgical indication
Remember: PUD remains the most common cause of upper GI bleeding worldwide. Early detection and treatment of H. pylori can prevent complications including gastric cancer.
