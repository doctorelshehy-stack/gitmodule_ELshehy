PUD — Diagnosis

History
• Duration and severity of symptoms
• Weight loss
• Medications (NSAIDs)
• Smoking and drinking habits
• Family history
Alarm Features → Prompt Referral
• Bleeding or anemia
• Early satiety, unexplained weight loss
• Progressive dysphagia/odynodysphagia
• Recurrent vomiting
• Family history of GI cancer
Physical Examination
• Uncomplicated PUD: few, nonspecific findings
• Epigastric tenderness (usually mild)
• Succussion splash (if obstruction)
H. pylori Investigations
Non-invasive Tests
• IgG antibodies (less sensitive)
• Urea breath test: detects urease activity
• Fecal antigen: more accurate than antibodies
Invasive Tests
• Rapid urease test (diagnostic of choice)
• Histopathology from biopsy
Special Studies
• Fasting serum gastrin if suspect ZES
• Multiple ulcers, distal to bulb
• Refractory to medical therapy
