Obesity Management

1) Behavioral Treatment
• Monitor and modify food intake
• Increase physical activity (60-90 min/day)
• Low-fat diet rich in complex carbs
• Eat breakfast daily
• Weigh frequently (at least weekly)
2) Pharmacotherapy
• BMI ≥ 30, or BMI ≥ 27 with obesity-related condition
(hypertension, type 2 DM, dyslipidemia)
Orlistat
• Inactivates gastric/pancreatic lipases
• Reduces fat absorption by ~30%
• Side effects: cramps, gas, diarrhea, oily spotting
Lorcaserin
• Serotonin 2C receptor agonist
• Reduces appetite, promotes satiety
3) Endo-luminal Management
• Space-occupying devices (intra-gastric balloon)
• Gastric restrictive methods (transoral gastroplasty)
• Malabsorptive procedures (DJ bypass liner)
• Regulating gastric emptying (Botox injections)
• Aspiration therapy
Intra-gastric Balloon
• Endoscopic placement
• Creates feeling of satiety
Indications:
• BMI >35: refused or high-risk for surgery
• BMI 29-35: no surgical indications
Disadvantages
• Stomach discomfort, cramping, nausea
• Acute pancreatitis
• Regurgitation, heartburn
• Balloon perforation
• Intestinal obstruction
