Section 1 • Hepatopulmonary Syndrome

Investigations for HPS

Initial Tests

  • Arterial oxygen saturation on room air: Value < 96% suggests HPS in absence of cardiopulmonary disease in cirrhotic patients
  • Arterial blood gas on room air: Demonstrates hypoxemia (typically <80mmHg). Elevated alveolar-arterial oxygen gradient (usually >20mmHg) due to ventilation perfusion mismatch

Severe disease:

  • Room air PaO2 < 60 mmHg
  • Shunt fraction > 20%

Imaging Techniques

  1. Chest X-ray: To exclude significant pulmonary disease or pleural effusion
  2. High resolution CT chest: Shows dilated peripheral pulmonary vessels and increased pulmonary artery to bronchus ratio
  3. Contrast echocardiogram: Demonstrates right to left shunt. Agitated saline or indocyanine green produces microbubbles. Appearance in right then left atrium/ventricle: if within 3 beats = intracardiac; 3-6 beats = intrapulmonary
  4. Pulmonary angiography: Invasive, limited experience in HPS. Useful to exclude conditions that mimic HPS such as pulmonary embolization

Nuclear scanning: Technetium-labeled macroaggregated albumin demonstrates right to left shunt (labeled albumin should not cross pulmonary bed).
