Section 2 • Enteric Fever (Typhoid)

Definition, Organism & Transmission

Definition

Enteric Fever (Enterica) is an acute enteric infectious disease leading to life threatening systemic illness caused by salmonella enterica.

Two subspecies:

  • Serovar typhi causing Typhoid fever
  • Serovars Para typhi A, B, and C causing Paratyphoid fever A, B, C

Salmonella Enterica

  • Gram negative short thick bacilli, motile by flagella
  • Facultative anaerobes and resistant to bile salts
  • H2S producing, non-lactose fermenting, non-spore forming organism

Sources of Infection

Primary source: Urine or feces of cases or carriers.

Secondary source: From contaminated food, water, fingers or flies.

Mode of Infection

Ingestion of contaminated food or water by organism in urine or feces of case or carrier either directly or by flies.

Pathogenesis (3 Steps)

  1. Bacilli survive gastric acidity, attack mucosa at terminal ileum via bacterial Ag. Taken by lymphatics to Peyer's patches & mesenteric LNs, engulfed by lymphocytes & macrophages.
  2. Bacilli reach blood via thoracic duct (primary bacteremia = incubation period). After multiplication in liver, spleen, bone marrow, LNs → spill into blood again (secondary bacteremia = clinical phase).
  3. Bacteria shed from gall bladder with bile, re-infect Peyer's patches & lymphoid follicles of ileum → inflammation and ulceration.
