Section 3 • Brucellosis

Complications & Diagnosis

Complications

A. Musculoskeletal

Spondylitis (usually lumbar) • Paravertebral abscess • Arthritis of large joints • Synovitis & bursitis • Osteomyelitis of long bones

B. Neurological (Neurobrucellosis)

Meningism & meningoencephalitis • Neuroretinitis • Hydrocephalus

C. Cardiopulmonary

Endocarditis mostly aortic valve (even healthy valves) • Myocarditis • Thrombophlebitis & DVT • Bronchitis & bronchopneumonia

D. Liver & Spleen

Granulomatous hepatitis • Abscess formation • Cholecystitis • Hypersplenism

E. Reproductive System

Epididymoorchitis

Diagnosis

I. Laboratory Diagnosis

  • CBC: Leucopenia due to neutropenia with relative lymphocytosis, variable anemia
  • LFT: Some elevation of liver enzymes, elevated ALP if granulomatous hepatic affection

II. Bacteriological Diagnosis

  • Blood Culture: Sample during fever spike, before starting antibiotics
  • Bone Marrow Culture: May be +ve when blood culture is -ve
  • Culture of pus or tissue culture
  • CSF culture: In cases of meningitis

III. Serological Diagnosis

  • Rose Bengal Test: Rapid slide agglutination, screening test
  • STAT: Measures total antibody. Titer ≥ 1/160 supports diagnosis. 4-fold increase/decrease over 4-12 weeks strongly supports
  • Coomb's Antiglobulin test: Measures IgG only, overcomes blocking Ab
  • ELISA & RIA: Detect both IgM and IgG
  • PCR: Of value if isolation is difficult or inconclusive serology
