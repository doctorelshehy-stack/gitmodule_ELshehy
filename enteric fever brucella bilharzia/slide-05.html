Section 1 • Hepatic Encephalopathy

Pathology / Pathogenesis & Precipitating Factors

Pathogenesis

  • Nitrogenous substances from gut adversely affect brain function. Compounds access systemic circulation due to decreased hepatic function or portal-systemic shunts.
  • In brain tissue, they produce alterations of neurotransmission affecting consciousness and behavior, leading to motor dysfunction and extrapyramidal symptoms.
  • The principal neuro-inhibitory neurotransmitter GABA is increased in the CSF.
  • Other CNS toxins: increased levels of endogenous benzodiazepine-like compounds, manganese, oxygen free radicals, circulation opioid peptides and nitric oxide.

Precipitating Factors

  • High protein intake
  • Infections
  • GI bleeding
  • Diuretic overdose
  • Electrolyte disorder
  • Constipation or Diarrhoea

Key insight: Nitrogenous compounds (especially ammonia) derived from gut adversely affect brain function. These gain access to systemic circulation due to decreased hepatic function or portal-systemic shunts.
