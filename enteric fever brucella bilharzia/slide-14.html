Section 2 • Enteric Fever (Typhoid)

Clinical Picture — Three Stages

Incubation period: 1-3 weeks. Onset: Insidious. Each stage lasts 7-10 days.

First Week

  • Malaise, headache, cough, sore throat (prodromal)
  • Stepladder fever (40-41°C) over 4-5 days with headache
  • Vague abdominal pain
  • Constipation or pea soup diarrhea

Second Week (7th-10th day)

  • Mild hepato-splenomegaly in majority
  • Relative bradycardia may occur
  • Rose spots: pink papule 2-3 mm on trunk, fade on pressure, disappear in 3-4 days

Third Week

  • "Typhoid state": prolonged apathy, toxemia, delirium, disorientation and/or coma
  • Diarrhea becomes apparent
  • If untreated: high risk (5-10%) of intestinal hemorrhage and perforation

Clinical Suspicion of Enteric Fever

Presence of: fever • relative bradycardia • toxemia • soft tender splenomegaly • rose spots • abdominal pain • abdominal distention • constipation or pea soup diarrhea
