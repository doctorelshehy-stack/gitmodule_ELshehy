Section 2 • Enteric Fever (Typhoid)

Complications of Enteric Fever

A. GIT Complications

Typhoid ulcer • Intestinal hemorrhage (bleeding per rectum) • Intestinal perforation (acute abdomen) • Paralytic ileus • Hepatitis • Liver abscess • Acute and chronic cholecystitis

B. Cardiopulmonary

Myocarditis & pericarditis • DVT with pulmonary embolization • Bronchitis & bronchopneumonia (more in children)

C. Hematological

Anemia & bone marrow suppression • Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome

D. Renal

Urine retention (early) • Febrile albuminuria • Cystitis • Nephritis • Nephropathy • Bacilluria (carrier) more in S. haematobium

E. Neurological

Meningism & meningitis • Delirium (typhoid state) may pass to coma (Typhoid Encephalopathy) • Psychosis (Typhoid encephalomyelitis) • Brain abscess • Peripheral neuritis • Myositis • DVT

F. Musculoskeletal

Periostitis • Osteomyelitis • Arthritis more in hip joint • Bone abscess • Zenker's degeneration of muscle

G. Relapse

Symptoms usually return around a week after antibiotic treatment has finished.

H. Typhoid Carrier

Asymptomatic, act as source of infection. Convalescent carrier or chronic carrier > 6 months (fecal or urinary).
