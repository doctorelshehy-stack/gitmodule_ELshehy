Section 2 • Enteric Fever (Typhoid)

Limitations of Widal Test & Chronic Salmonellosis

Limitations of Widal Test

  • A single test is of little clinical relevance especially in endemic areas
  • False positive test occurs in patients with previous vaccination or infection with S. Typhi
  • Cross-reactivity with other Salmonella species
  • Cannot distinguish between a current infection and previous infection or vaccination against typhoid
  • False negative test may be due to blocked antibody responses by early antimicrobial treatment or following a typhoid relapse

Chronic Salmonellosis

  • Chronic salmonella infection in patients with active Schistosomal infection, characterized by recurrent bacteremia and prolonged fever
  • The organism penetrates the schistosome and multiply within their ceca, so schistosome act as nidus producing showers of salmonella into the circulation
  • Treatment aim: Eradicate Salmonella bacilli by anti typhoid drugs for 10-14 days (choice individualized) and prevent relapse by anti bilharzial drugs (Praziquantel 40 mg/kg as single dose) given on the second apyrexial day

Typhoid Prevention (Vaccines)

Ty21a (Live attenuated)

Oral, 4 doses every other day. Min age 5. Protection 50-80%. Boost every 5 years.

VI Capsule Antigen (Polysaccharide)

IM, 1 dose. Min age 2. Protection 50-80%. Boost every 2 years in travelers.
