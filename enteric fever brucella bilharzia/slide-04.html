Section 1 • Hepatic Encephalopathy

Type C HE — Further Classification

Based on duration & neurological manifestations:

(a) Episodic HE

Episodes develop over a short period and fluctuate in severity.

Precipitated: by event such as GI hemorrhage or uremia.
Spontaneous: no recognized precipitating factors.
Recurrent: two episodes of episodic HE within 1 year.

(b) Persistent HE

Persistent cognitive deficits that impact negatively on social and occupational functioning.

Mild: HE grade 1
Severe: HE grades 2-4

(c) Minimal HE (MHE)

No recognizable clinical symptoms of HE but have mild cognitive and psychomotor deficits manifested by impairment in specialized testing of cognitive functioning.

Key Point: Type A = Acute liver failure. Type B = Porto-systemic bypass without intrinsic hepatocellular disease. Type C = Cirrhosis and portal hypertension (most common context for HE).
