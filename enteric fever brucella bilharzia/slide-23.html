Section 4 • Schistosomiasis

Species, Life Cycle & Pathology

Species

  1. S. Mansoni
  2. S. Haematobium
  3. S. Japonicum

Life Cycle (from image)

  1. Eggs (S. mansoni B, S. japonicum A in feces; S. haematobium C in urine)
  2. Eggs hatch releasing miracidia
  3. Miracidia penetrate snail tissue
  4. Sporocysts in snail (successive generations)
  5. Cercariae released by snail into water (free-swimming) – Infective stage
  6. Penetrate skin → lose tails → become schistosomulae
  7. Circulation → migrate to portal blood in liver → mature into adults
  8. Paired adult worms migrate to mesenteric venules (A, B) or venous plexus of bladder (C)

Pathology — 3 Stages

1. Stage of Invasion

  • Cercarial dermatitis (swimmer's itch) – from skin penetration by cercaria
  • Cercarial pneumonitis (eosinophilic pneumonitis) – patchy lung congestion, eosinophilic sputum, high eosinophilia

2. Stage of Ova Deposition & Granuloma Formation

  • Ova deposited in submucosa, surrounded by granulomatous reaction
  • S. haematobium: urinary system (ureter, bladder, seminal vesicles, prostate)
  • S. mansoni: large intestine (descending colon, rectum)
  • Granuloma: ova surrounded by epithelioid cells, eosinophils, lymphocytes, giant cells, later loose fibrous tissue
  • Soluble antigen leaks from eggs → Ag-Ab complex formation → generalized immune complex disease

3. Stage of Repair by Fibrosis

Granulomatous tissue replaced by fibrosis. Clinical manifestations depend on affected organ.
