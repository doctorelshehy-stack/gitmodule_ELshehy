Section 2 • Enteric Fever (Typhoid)

Diagnosis of Enteric Fever

I. Laboratory Diagnosis

  • CBC: Leucopenia due to neutropenia with relative lymphocytosis, variable degree of anemia
  • Liver function tests: Febrile albuminuria, liver enzymes and bilirubin may be normal or slightly elevated

II. Bacteriological Diagnosis

Standard: Culture isolation of the organism

  • First 7-10 days: Whole blood culture or bone marrow culture. Blood culture positive in ~85-90% within first week of onset.
  • Third week and thereafter: Fecal • Urine • Tissues • Pus Culture • Bile (Duodenal Aspirate) Culture

III. Serological Diagnosis

1) Widal Agglutination Test

Measures agglutinating antibodies against H and O antigens.

  • Agglutinin starts appearing by end of 1st week with sharp rise in 2nd and 3rd week, then declines
  • Diagnostic titer: O-antibody titer ≥ 1/80 in non-endemic area or ≥ 1/160 in endemic area
  • 4-folds rising titer of 2 samples with one week apart = diagnostic

2) Other Serologic Tests

  • IHAT • Indirect fluorescent VI-Ab test • Indirect ELISA • Monoclonal Ab
  • PCR: Reported sensitivity 82.7%, specificity 100%. Not widely available for diagnosis.
