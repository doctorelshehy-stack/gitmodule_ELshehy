Section 1 • Hepatic Encephalopathy

Definition & Classification

Definition: HE is a chronically incapacitating syndrome of neuropsychiatric symptoms, which can develop in patients with both acute and chronic liver dysfunction once other known brain diseases have been excluded.

Disease Classification (based on type of liver dysfunction):

Type A

Acute liver failure

Type B

Porto-systemic bypass
No intrinsic hepatocellular disease

Type C

Cirrhosis & portal hypertension

Etiology: Primarily due to accumulation of toxins in the serum due to liver dysfunction. Major factors: reduction in hepatic function and mass + development of porto-systemic collaterals leading to circulatory bypass of the liver. Ammonia is the toxin most frequently implicated in pathogenesis of HE.
