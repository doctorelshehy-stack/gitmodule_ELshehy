Summary & Review

Hepatic Encephalopathy

  • Types A, B, C based on liver dysfunction
  • Ammonia is key toxin
  • Lactulose 1st line, Rifaximin 2nd line
  • Protein 1-1.5 g/kg/day

Hepatopulmonary Syndrome

  • IPVDs + increased A-a gradient
  • SaO2 < 96% suggests HPS
  • Contrast echo for shunt detection
  • LTX if PaO2 > 55-60, shunt < 20%

Enteric Fever (Typhoid)

  • Salmonella enterica (typhi & paratyphi)
  • 3 stages: 7-10 days each
  • Rose spots, relative bradycardia
  • 3rd/4th gen cephalosporins, FQ, AZM

Brucellosis

  • Zoonotic: B. melitensis, abortus, suis
  • Undulant fever, spondylitis
  • Doxycycline + Streptomycin/Rifampin 6wk
  • Triple therapy for complicated cases

Schistosomiasis

  • S. mansoni, haematobium, japonicum
  • Cercaria (infective stage) from snails
  • Katayama syndrome, cercarial dermatitis
  • Urinary, intestinal, hepatosplenic forms
  • Praziquantel 40 mg/kg single dose
  • Complications: varices, SBP, nephropathy
