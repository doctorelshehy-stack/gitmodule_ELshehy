Section 4 • Schistosomiasis

Investigations & Chemotherapy

Investigations

1. Direct Methods

  • Urine analysis: Detecting eggs of S. haematobium
  • Stool analysis: Detecting eggs of S. mansoni & japonicum
  • Biopsy techniques:
    • Rectal mucosa biopsy from suspected lesion or rectal valves (examined by transparency technique)
    • Liver biopsy: transparency technique or histopathology

2. Indirect Methods

  • Serology: Detecting circulating antibodies by ELISA or IFAT

Disadvantages of serology: Not species specific. Doesn't distinguish between past & present infection.

3-5. Other Investigations

  • Cystoscopy: For detection of urinary bladder lesions
  • X-ray of urinary tract: To detect calcification of bladder and ureter
  • Abdominal ultrasound (US): To detect periportal fibrosis of liver, splenomegaly and ascites

Chemotherapy

1) Praziquantel

  • Dose: 40 mg/kg single oral dose is sufficient for all species
  • Indications: Effective against all human Schistosomes
  • Side effects: Usually mild: epigastric pain, nausea, dizziness, loose stool, pruritus and fever

2) Metriphonate

Active against S. haematobium only

3) Oxamniquine

Active against S. mansoni only
