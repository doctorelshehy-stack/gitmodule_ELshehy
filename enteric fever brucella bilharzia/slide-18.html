Section 2 • Enteric Fever (Typhoid)

Treatment of Enteric Fever

I. General Measures

  • Good nursing care, sanitary disposal of excreta
  • Adequate soft diet of high caloric value
  • Fluid and electrolyte balance
  • Avoid purgatives even if constipated
  • Monitoring vital signs

Role of Surgery

  • Intestinal hemorrhage (if conservative measures fail to stop bleeding)
  • Intestinal perforation
  • Cholecystectomy if antibiotic treatment fails to eradicate the hepatobiliary carrier

II. Anti-Typhoid Drugs

3rd Gen Cephalosporin (5-7 days after fever subsides)

Cefotaxim/Cefoperazone: IM/IV Adult 1gm/8-12h. Or Ceftriaxone: IV/IM Adult 2gm.

4th Gen Cephalosporin (5-7 days after fever subsides)

Cefepime (Maxipime): Infusion/Injection Adult 1-2gm/8-12h.

Fluoroquinolone (10-14 days)

Not in pregnant, lactating, children <16y. Ciprofloxacin 500-750mg/12h, Norfloxacin 400mg/12h, Levofloxacin 500-750mg/day.

Azithromycin (Zithromax)

Oral 10 mg/kg (max 500 mg/d). For uncomplicated typhoid fever in children 4-17 y.

Treatment of Typhoid Carrier

  • Co-Trimoxazole, Amoxycillin, or Ampicillin for 1 month (if no resistance)
  • Ciprofloxacin or Norfloxacin for 1 month + 2gm Probencid/day for fecal carrier
  • Cholecystectomy if medical treatment fails (especially with gallbladder disease)
  • Three successive negative stool or urine cultures before discharge to avoid carrier state
