Section 3 • Brucellosis

Definition, Organism & Classification

Definition

Brucellosis is a systemic zoonotic and occupational disease, transmitted from animals (cattle, sheep, and goats) to humans, caused by bacteria of the genus Brucella (Mediterranean fever or undulant fever).

Brucella Organism

  • Gram negative cocco bacilli
  • Intracellular aerobic (may need ±5-10% CO2)
  • Non-spore forming, non-motile, non-capsulated

Classification

Eight Brucella species exist, four are pathogenic to humans:

  • B. melitensis, B. abortus, B. suis — important human pathogens
  • B. canis — lesser importance, causes mild disease

Source & Mode of Infection

Source: Brucella localized in animal's reproductive organs, shed in urine, milk, semen, placental fluid causing abortion, sterility and death.

Mode: Ingestion of infected milk/products or direct contact with infected animals.

Risk groups:

Veterinarians, slaughter house workers, farmers, shepherds, laboratory workers.
