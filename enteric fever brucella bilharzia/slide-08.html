Section 1 • Hepatic Encephalopathy

Differential Diagnosis of HE

Metabolic Encephalopathies

Hypoxia, hyponatremia, azotemia, diabetic coma.

Tests: Blood gas, clinical chemistry, pulmonary evaluation, urinalysis based on indication.

Intracranial Disorders

Tumors, hemorrhage, hematoma, meningitis, seizure disorder, dementia.

Tests: Neurological imaging, lumbar puncture, EEG based on clinical suspicion, rapid plasma regain, vitamin B12 serum level.

Toxins, alcohol, drugs: Hypnotics, tranquilizers, analgesics, heavy metals. Tests: Urine and blood toxin screen, blood alcohol level.

Managing the Hospitalized Patient

  • Ensure airway, breathing, and circulation. Intubate for airway protection if indicated.
  • Evaluate for infection, bleeding or renal failure and treat appropriately.
  • Correct electrolyte abnormalities, consider empiric antibiotics.
  • CT head to rule out bleeding in patient with focal neurological signs.
  • Lumbar puncture is risky in coagulopathic patients; do only if strong suspicion for meningitis.
  • Place NG tube and administer lactulose 30 cm³ every 1-2 hours until bowel movement.
  • Haloperidol can be used to calm an agitated patient.
  • Lactulose enemas can be used in patients who are bleeding or have large varices.
  • Monitor closely for hypernatremia from excessive lactulose. Add Rifaximin if no improvement.
