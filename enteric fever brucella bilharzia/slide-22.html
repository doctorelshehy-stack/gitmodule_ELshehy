Section 3 • Brucellosis

Treatment of Brucellosis

A. Uncomplicated Brucellosis (6 weeks)

Regimen 1: Doxycyclin (200 mg/day PO) for 6 weeks + Streptomycin (1gm IM/day for 21 days)

Regimen 2: Doxycyclin (200 mg/day PO) for 6 weeks + Rifampicin (600-900 mg/day for 6 weeks)

Children ≤ 8 years: Co-trimoxazole = TMP (10 mg/kg) + SMZ (50mg/kg) + Rifampicin (10 mg/kg)

C. Pregnancy

Rifampicin ± TMP-SMZ for 8-12 weeks. Avoid TMP-SMZ in first trimester and at end of pregnancy (risk of kernicterus).

B. Complicated Brucellosis (Endocarditis, Meningitis, Osteomyelitis)

  • Triple antibiotic therapy: Rifampin + Doxycycline + Streptomycin often used
  • Duration: 4-6 months

Role of Surgery

  • Valve replacement surgery in treatment of Brucella endocarditis: Valvular lesions in aortic valve are typically large and destructive
  • Drainage of pyogenic joint effusions or para spinal abscesses
