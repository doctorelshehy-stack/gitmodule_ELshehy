HCC — Overview

Epidemiology
• Main primary liver malignancy
• Far East & equatorial Africa (highest: Mozambique)
• Commoner in men
Etiology
• Hepatitis B: >50% of HCC worldwide
• Hepatitis C: 90% of HCC in Egypt
• Alcoholic cirrhosis: free radicals → chronic inflammation
• Obesity & DM: impaired glucose handling
• Aflatoxin: Aspergillus Flavus on grains
• Liver fluke: Clonorchis sinensis → cholangiocarcinoma
Complications
• Spread: lymphatic, portal vein, peritoneal
• Spontaneous rupture → massive hemorrhage
Clinical Presentation
• Small lesions: accidentally discovered
• In Egypt: US every 6 months for cirrhotics
• General deterioration in cirrhosis
• Weight loss, anorexia, jaundice
• Late: pain, ascites, hemorrhage
Investigations
• Lab: high ALP, disturbed LFTs
• AFP >200: suggestive
• AFP >1000 + mass: diagnostic
• US: detect lesions >2 cm
• Triple-phase CT / MRI: standard of care
• EUS: detect small lesions
• Laparoscopy: assess disease + biopsy
