Benign Liver Tumors

Liver Cell Adenoma (LCA)
• True benign hepatocyte neoplasm
• Women 25-50 years
• Associated with OCP use
• Histology: regular hepatocytes, no portal triads
Complications:
• Malignant transformation (up to 10%)
• Spontaneous rupture → hemorrhage
Treatment:
• Discontinue OCP
• Excise if >5 cm
Focal Nodular Hyperplasia (FNH)
• Females on contraceptive medications
• CT/MRI: central scarring, hypervascular
• NO malignant potential
• Once diagnosed: no treatment needed
• Excise if not resolved in 6 months
Hemangioma
• Commonest benign liver lesion
• Cavernous type (vessel plexuses)
• Usually harmless, rarely rupture
Diagnosis:
• CT: slow contrast enhancement
• Biopsy contraindicated!
Treatment:
• Asymptomatic: no treatment
• Symptomatic: embolization
• Bleeding/large: surgical excision
