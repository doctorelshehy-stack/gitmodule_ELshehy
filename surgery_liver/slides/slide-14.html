Obstructive Jaundice

Definition
• Jaundice due to obstruction of conjugated bile flow
• Conjugated hyperbilirubinemia
• Hepatic or extrahepatic level
• Benign or malignant cause
Benign Causes
1) CBD stones (secondary or primary)
• May pass spontaneously
• Impacted → rising jaundice ± cholangitis
2) Mirizzi syndrome
• Big stone at GB neck → CBD compression
3) Sclerosing cholangitis
• Autoimmune, associated with UC
4) Biliary atresia (congenital)
5) Iatrogenic bile duct injury
6) Parasitic: ascariasis, Clonorchis
7) Pancreatitis: bulky pancreas
Malignant Causes
1) Cancer head of pancreas
2) Cholangiocarcinoma
3) Duodenal carcinoma (ampulla of Vater)
4) Cancer gallbladder
5) Hepatocellular carcinoma
6) Metastatic porta hepatis lymph nodes
Pathophysiology
• Impaired bile acid secretion → fat malabsorption
• Fat-soluble vitamin deficiency:
— Vit A: visual problems
— Vit D: osteomalacia
— Vit E: neuropathy
— Vit K: bleeding tendencies
• Steatorrhea, pale stools
• Pruritus (bile acid accumulation)
• Bilirubinostasis → hepatocyte degeneration
• Stasis → bacterial colonization → cholangitis
