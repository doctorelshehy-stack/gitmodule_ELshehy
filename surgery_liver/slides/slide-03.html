Pyogenic Liver Abscess

Sources of Infection
1) Biliary Tract (Cholangitic) — Most common
Ascending cholangitis → multiple abscesses
Organism: E. coli, gram-negative bacilli
2) Portal Vein
Appendicitis, diverticulitis → portal pyaemia
Organism: Streptococci, anaerobes
3) Arterial (Hematogenous)
Endocarditis, tonsillitis, IV drug misuse
Organism: S. aureus
Predisposing Factors
• Immunocompromised: DM, leukemia, alcoholics
• Existing liver lesion (hydatid cyst, hematoma)
Clinical Features
• Fever, toxemia, right hypochondrial pain
• Tender hepatomegaly (may be absent)
• Charcot's triad of cholangitis
Investigations
• Lab: Leucocytosis, anemia, high ESR
• Low albumin, high ALP, high transaminases
• Imaging: Ultrasound or CT scan
Treatment
• Broad-spectrum antibiotics
• Percutaneous guided drainage (US/CT)
• Open drainage if needed
