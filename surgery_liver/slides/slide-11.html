LT — Indications

Common Indications
1) Chronic Hepatitis C
• Cirrhosis after 20 years
• 17-30% develop HCC
• Up to 40% of LT
2) Chronic Hepatitis B
• Now with HBV Ig + antivirals
3) Alcoholic Liver Disease
• 6 months abstinence required
4) AIH-induced cirrhosis
5) HCC
• Single <5 cm or 3 <3 cm
• 5-year survival ~70%
6) Acute Liver Failure
7) NAFLD
8) PBC
9) PSC
Uncommon Indications
• Hereditary hemochromatosis
— Caucasians, prevalence 1:200-300
• Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency
— Autosomal recessive
— Common LT in children
• Wilson disease
— Autosomal recessive
— Good outcome with LT
• Glycogen storage disease
• Tyrosinemia
• Amyloidosis
• Polycystic liver disease
• Budd-Chiari syndrome
• Metastatic neuroendocrine tumors
• Branched-chain aminoacid disorders
