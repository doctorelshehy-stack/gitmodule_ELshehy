Hydatid Disease of the Liver

Etiology & Pathology
• Echinococcus granulosus (tapeworm)
• Sheep-rearing areas: Iraq, Yemen, Libya, Egypt
Cyst Wall (3 layers):
1) Inner germinal layer (endocyst)
2) Outer laminated membrane
3) Host's adventitial fibrous tissue
• Daughter cysts may form inside
Fate:
• Most: continue to enlarge
• Some: parasite dies, cyst calcifies
• Some: complications (life-threatening)
Clinical Features
• Often asymptomatic for years
• Chronic RUQ pain, hepatomegaly
• Large cyst: painless swelling with thrill
Diagnosis
• Eosinophilia, Casoni's test
• Ultrasound/CT: cystic lesion with daughter cells
Complications
• Secondary infection
• Rupture into biliary passages → jaundice
• Rupture into peritoneum → anaphylaxis
Treatment
1) Medical
• Albendazole or mebendazole course
2) PAIR (Puncture-Aspiration-Instillation-Respiration)
• Under image guidance + albendazole
• No biliary communication
3) Surgery
• Liver resection or local excision
• Deroofing with evacuation
• Pack with 20% hypertonic saline
• Suture biliary communication
• Omentoplasty for residual cavity
• Calcified cysts may be dead — follow with US
