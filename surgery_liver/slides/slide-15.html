OJ — Clinical Features & Diagnosis

Clinical Features
1) Jaundice (scleral icterus >2.5 mg%)
2) Dark frothy urine (direct bilirubin + bile salts)
3) Clay-colored bulky offensive stool
4) Pruritus
Painful Jaundice (calculi):
• RUQ pain → right shoulder/back
• Recurrent attacks, ↑ by fatty meals
Painless Jaundice (malignancy):
Charcot's Triad: pain + jaundice + fever
Reynold's Pentad: triad + altered mental state + shock
Courvoisier's Law:
• Palpable GB + jaundice = probably non-calculi
• Exceptions: metabolic stone, Hartmann's pouch
Laboratory
• Rising total & direct bilirubin
• Rising ALP and GGT
• WCC ↑ (cholangitis), amylase ↑ (pancreatitis)
• Impaired PT in prolonged jaundice
• CA19-9 ↑ (malignancy)
• Stool: clay-colored; Urine: dark
Imaging
U/S — Preliminary investigation
• Detects obstruction & level
CT/MRI — Suspected malignancy
MRCP — New gold standard
• Non-invasive, highly sensitive
• Reserves ERCP for therapeutic cases
EUS — 96% accuracy for CBD stones
