LT — Complications

Surgical (Days-Weeks)
1) Primary Graft Non-Function
2) Hepatic Artery Thrombosis
• 2-9% incidence
• Predisposing: small donor, clotting, smoking
3) Biliary Complications
• Up to 25%
• Stricture, bile leaks
• Immunologic or ischemic injury
Rejection
Hyper-acute
• Pre-sensitization to donor antigens
• Rare with good matching
Acute & Chronic
• Up to 60% incidence
Immunosuppression Protocol:
• 0-1 mo: Tacrolimus + MMF + Prednisone
• 1-3 mo: Same, taper prednisone
• 3-6 mo: Same
• 6-12 mo: Tacrolimus only
• >12 mo: Low-dose tacrolimus
Infections
1) Pneumocystis carinii
• TMP-SMX × 4 months
2) CMV
• Most common post-transplant
• Leukopenia, pneumonitis, colitis
• Treatment: IV ganciclovir
3) PTLD (EBV)
• Up to 3%
• Mononucleosis → lymphoma
Long-term:
DM, HTN, hyperlipidemia, renal failure
Osteoporosis, malignancy, recurrent HCV
