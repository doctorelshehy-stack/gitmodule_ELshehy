HCC — Treatment

1) Hepatic Resection
Indications:
• Child class A
• No extra-hepatic spread
• Single lesion <5 cm OR 3 lesions <3 cm
• Sufficient residual liver volume
Contraindications:
• Extrahepatic metastasis
• Multiple/bilobar tumors
• Main bile duct involvement
• Tumor thrombus in portal vein/IVC
2) Liver Transplantation
Indications:
• Child class C
• No extra-hepatic spread
• Single lesion <5 cm OR 3 lesions <3 cm
Advantage:
• Treats tumor + removes diseased liver
• Reduces intrahepatic recurrence
• Downstaging with ablation possible
3) Palliative (Inoperable)
TACE:
• Trans-arterial chemo-embolization
• Ischemia + selective chemotherapy
Ablation:
• Alcohol injection (US-guided)
• Radio-frequency ablation
• Microwave ablation
• Laser ablation
Systemic chemotherapy
