Liver Transplantation — Overview

History
• First LT: 1963
• Before cyclosporin: 25% 1-year survival
• Now: ~90% 5-year survival
• New immunosuppression: tacrolimus, mycophenolate
Key Concepts
• HCV and HCC: main worldwide indications
• MELD score: priority for listing
• Acute rejection: up to 60%
• Biliary stricture: 15% after transplant
Types of Transplantation
1) Cadaveric (Orthotopic) — Most common
• Cirrhotic liver removed
• Anastomosis: portal vein, hepatic artery, bile duct
2) Split Liver
• Right lobe → adult, Left lobe → child
3) Living Donor
• Same as cadaveric but donor morbidity risk
Donor Evaluation
Selection Criteria:
• ABO compatible
• Brain dead with stable BP & oxygenation
• Compatible liver size
Exclusion Factors:
• Preexisting liver disease
• Uncontrolled DM, hypertension
• Active malignancy/infection
• Hypotension/asystole ischemia
• Active alcohol/drug abuse
• Morbid obesity
Note:
If liver enzymes high → live donor biopsy before transplant
