Summary — Liver

Liver Infections
• Pyogenic: biliary tract most common source
• Amoebic: metronidazole is drug of choice
• Hydatid: 3-layer cyst wall, PAIR or surgery
Benign Tumors
• LCA: stop OCP, excise if >5 cm
• FNH: no malignant potential
• Hemangioma: no biopsy, treat if symptomatic
HCC
• HCV (Egypt), HBV (worldwide)
• AFP >1000 + mass = diagnostic
• Resection: Child A, single <5 cm
• Transplant: Child C, Milan criteria
Liver Transplantation
• HCV + HCC: main indications
• MELD score for listing priority
• CMV: most common post-transplant infection
Obstructive Jaundice
• Dark urine, clay stools, pruritus
• Charcot's triad: pain + jaundice + fever
• Courvoisier: palpable GB = non-calculi
• MRCP: new gold standard
• ERCP for therapeutic intervention
• Whipple: for resectable pancreatic head
