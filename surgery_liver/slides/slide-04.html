Amoebic Hepatitis & Abscess

Etiology & Pathology
• E. histolytica invades colon → portal blood → liver
• Right lobe more affected (right colon drainage)
• Most common site: posterosuperior segment RLL
• Liquefactive necrosis → usually single, unilocular
• Pus: sterile, brown "anchovy sauce" color
Clinical Features
• Upper abdominal pain, low-grade fever
• Anorexia, nausea, weight loss
• Pale + toxic appearance = "earthy look"
• Tender hepatomegaly
• R basal lung abscess or pleural effusion
Complications
• Secondary bacterial infection
• Rupture: pleura, lung, pericardium, peritoneum
Diagnosis
• Stool analysis & serological tests
• Leukocytosis and anemia
• Ultrasound or CT: single abscess
• CXR: elevated R cupola, pleural effusion
• Therapeutic test: metronidazole response
Treatment
Metronidazole — Drug of choice
800 mg TID × 7-10 days
• US-guided aspiration: if no response in 72h
• Open drainage: secondary infection, thick pus
Key Difference from Pyogenic:
• Amoebic: low-grade fever, single abscess
• Pyogenic: high-grade fever, multiple abscesses
