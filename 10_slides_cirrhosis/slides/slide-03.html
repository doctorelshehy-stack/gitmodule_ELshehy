Definition & Causes

Definition: Fibrosis and regenerative nodules resulting from hepatocellular injury. Characterized by diffuse hepatic fibrosis and nodule formation.

Most common causes:

  • Chronic hepatitis
  • Prolonged excessive alcohol consumption

Most common cause of: 1) Portal hypertension 2) Chronic liver cell failure

Causes (10 Categories)

  1. Chronic viral hepatitis (B, C, HDV)
  2. Alcohol and toxins (arsenic)
  3. NAFLD (most "cryptogenic cirrhosis")
  4. Immune (PSC, autoimmune liver disease)
  5. Biliary (PBC, secondary, cystic fibrosis)
  6. Genetic/metabolic (hemochromatosis, Wilson's, A1AT deficiency)
  7. Cryptogenic (15%)
  8. Chronic venous outflow obstruction
  9. Vascular (Budd-Chiari, CHF, constrictive pericarditis, SOS)
  10. Medications (amiodarone, methotrexate, vitamin A, valproate, INH)
