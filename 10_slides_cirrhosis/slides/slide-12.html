Pathophysiology — Stellate Cell Activation

Following liver injury, stellate cells in the space of Disse are activated by cytokines produced by Kupffer cells and hepatocytes, transforming into myofibroblast-like cells.

Myofibroblast-like Cell Products

  • Collagen — primary structural protein causing fibrosis
  • Pro-inflammatory cytokines — amplify inflammation
  • Other mediators — promote hepatocyte damage and tissue fibrosis

Architecture Destruction Consequences

  1. Distortion and loss of normal hepatocyte vasculature
  2. Development of portosystemic vascular shunts
  3. Formation of nodules

Key Point: Histological diagnosis requires bridging fibrosis and nodule formation. Cirrhosis is classified as micronodular (small nodules ~1mm, seen in alcoholic cirrhosis) or macronodular (larger nodules of various sizes with areas of previous collapse evidenced by large fibrosis).
