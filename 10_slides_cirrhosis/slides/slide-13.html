Haemorrhagic Tendency & Endocrine Changes

Haemorrhagic Tendency

  • Bruises
  • Purpura
  • Epistaxis
  • Variceal bleeding
  • Prolonged PTT

Mechanism: Decreased synthesis of clotting factors (II, VII, IX, X) by damaged hepatocytes, decreased vitamin K absorption, and thrombocytopenia from hypersplenism.

Endocrine Changes

General:

  • Loss of libido
  • Hair loss

Men:

  • Gynecomastia
  • Testicular atrophy
  • Impotence

Women:

  • Breast atrophy
  • Irregular menses
  • Amenorrhoea
