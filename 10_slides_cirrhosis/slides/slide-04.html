Pathophysiology & Histology

Pathophysiology

  1. Progressive and widespread death of liver cells
  2. Inflammation → loss of normal liver architecture
  3. Stellate cells activated by cytokines from Kupffer cells
  4. Transformed into myofibroblast-like cells producing:
    • Collagen
    • Pro-inflammatory cytokines
    • Mediators promoting hepatocyte damage
  5. Increase in fibrous tissue

Consequences of Architecture Destruction

  1. Distortion and loss of normal hepatocyte vasculature
  2. Development of portosystemic vascular shunts
  3. Formation of nodules

Histological Types

Micronodular: Small nodules (~1mm) — seen in alcoholic cirrhosis

Macronodular: Larger nodules of various sizes with areas of previous collapse and large fibrosis
