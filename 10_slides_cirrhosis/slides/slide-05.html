Clinical Features

Some patients are asymptomatic (diagnosed incidentally). Others present with hepatomegaly, splenomegaly, or signs of portal hypertension.

Liver Exam:

Initially enlarged, palpable, firm → eventually shrunken, nodular

Non-Specific Symptoms:

  • Weakness
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle cramps
  • Weight loss
  • Anorexia
  • Nausea / Vomiting
  • Upper abdominal discomfort
  • Clubbing of fingers/toes

Signs

  • Jaundice: Usually mild (failure to excrete bilirubin)
  • Palmar erythema: Limited diagnostic value
  • Spider telangiectasis: Central arteriole with radiating vessels, usually above nipple
  • Florid spider + gynecomastia + parotid enlargement → alcoholic cirrhosis
  • Pigmentation: Hemochromatosis, prolonged cholestasis
  • Splenomegaly, variceal bleeding, collateral vessels → portal HTN
  • Ascites: Liver failure + portal hypertension
  • Hepatic encephalopathy: Advancing disease
  • Dupuytren's contracture

Endocrine Changes:

Loss of libido, hair loss. Men: gynecomastia, testicular atrophy, impotence. Women: breast atrophy, irregular menses, amenorrhoea.
