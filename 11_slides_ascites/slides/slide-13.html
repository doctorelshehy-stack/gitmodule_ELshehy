Paracentesis & Refractory Ascites

Paracentesis

  • Treatment of refractory ascites
  • Large-volume paracentesis with IV albumin replacement
  • Removing 3–5 L daily is safe
  • Circulation supported with IV colloid (human albumin or plasma expander)

Refractory / Diuretic-Resistant Ascites

Patients who do not respond to 400 mg spironolactone + 160 mg furosemide.

  • Seen in 5–10% of patients
  • 2-year survival: 25%
  • Diuretic-resistant on 2g Na diet
  • Minimal weight loss on maximal diuretic doses
  • Diuretic-induced complications (AKI, Na <125, hyperkalemia, encephalopathy)

LVP (>5 L): Give 6–8 g albumin per L fluid removed. Decreases risk of post-paracentesis circulatory dysfunction & possibly ↓ mortality.
