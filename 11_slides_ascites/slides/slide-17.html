Key Takeaways

Definition

Pathological fluid accumulation in peritoneal cavity. Small amounts asymptomatic; >1L causes distension, shifting dullness, fluid thrill.

SAAG

SAAG >11 g/L = portal hypertension (96% predictive). Transudate: protein <25 g/L.

Management

Na restriction (100 mmol/day) + Spironolactone (100-400mg) ± Furosemide. LVP for refractory cases with albumin.

SBP

Sudden pain + fever + cloudy fluid. Neutrophils >250 × 10⁶/L. Treat with cefotaxime immediately.

TIPSS: Reduces ascites in 75%. Contraindicated in encephalopathy, CHF, active infection. Albumin: 40g 2×/wk × 2wk then weekly × 16wk → ↓ mortality 38%.
