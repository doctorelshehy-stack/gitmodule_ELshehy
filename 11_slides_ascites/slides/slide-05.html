Clinical Significance

In cirrhotic patients, ascites may be complicated by:

  • Renal failure
  • Spontaneous infections — often precipitated by variceal bleeding

Blood in the GI tract affects permeability to bacteria.

Both complications have adverse prognostic significance and may prompt referral for transplantation. Successful treatment relieves discomfort but doesn't prolong life.
