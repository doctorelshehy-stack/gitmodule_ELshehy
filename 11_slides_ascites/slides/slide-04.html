Definition & Clinical Features

Definition: Pathological accumulation of fluid within the peritoneal cavity.

Small amounts of ascites are asymptomatic. Larger accumulations (>1 L) produce:

  • Abdominal distension
  • Fullness in the flanks
  • Shifting dullness on percussion
  • Fluid thrill (when marked)

Other Signs

  • Distortion or eversion of the umbilicus
  • Herniae
  • Abdominal striae
  • Divarication of the recti
  • Scrotal edema

Dilated superficial abdominal veins may be seen if ascites is due to portal hypertension.
