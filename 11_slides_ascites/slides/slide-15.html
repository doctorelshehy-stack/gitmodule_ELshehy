Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP)

Presentation

Sudden onset in patient with cirrhosis and ascites:

  • Abdominal pain
  • Rebound tenderness
  • Absent bowel sounds
  • Fever

Note: Abdominal signs are mild or absent in ~1/3 of patients. In these, hepatic encephalopathy and fever are the main features.

Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnostic paracentesis: Cloudy fluid

Neutrophil count >250 × 10⁶/L almost invariably indicates infection

Most organisms: E. coli (enteric origin)

Treatment:

  • Immediate broad-spectrum antibiotics (cefotaxime 1g/8h)
  • Prophylactic quinolones: Norfloxacin 400 mg daily or Ciprofloxacin 250 mg 12-hourly
