Peritoneo-Venous Shunt & TIPSS

Peritoneo-Venous Shunt

From peritoneum to internal jugular vein (systemic circulation).

Complications (limit use):

  • Infection
  • Superior vena caval thrombosis
  • Pulmonary oedema
  • Bleeding from oesophageal varices
  • DIC

Insertion of these shunts is now rare.

TIPSS

Transjugular intrahepatic portosystemic stent shunt.

  • Relieves resistant ascites
  • Reduces ascites in 75% of cases
  • Alternative to frequent LVP
  • Increases creatinine clearance

Contraindications:

  • Grade II encephalopathy
  • CHF or pulmonary HTN
  • Active infection
  • Biliary obstruction

Complications: Bleeding, stent thrombosis, "endotipsitis", encephalopathy (20–30%)
