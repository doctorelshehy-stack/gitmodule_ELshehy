Pathophysiology of Obstruction

Early Phase
• Intestinal motility INCREASES
• Above obstruction: propels contents past block
• Below obstruction: diarrhea (until empty)
Late Phase:
• Bowel fatigues, contractions ↓
• Bowel dilates
Gas Accumulation
• Significant bacterial overgrowth
• Aerobic + anaerobic organisms
• Majority: nitrogen (90%) + H₂S
Fluid Accumulation
• Water + electrolytes intra-luminally
• Bowel wall edema
• Third-space fluid loss
• → Dehydration, hypovolemia
• → Oliguria, shock
Consequences
↑ Intra-abdominal Pressure:
• ↓ Venous return
• Diaphragm elevation
• Compromised ventilation
↑ Intraluminal Pressure:
• ↓ Mucosal blood flow
• → Ischemia → perforation
• Closed-loop: malignant stricture
Flora Changes:
• E. coli, S. faecalis, Klebsiella
• Concentrations: 10⁹-10¹⁰/ml
Electrolyte Effects
• Proximal obstruction: hypochloremia, hypokalemia, metabolic alkalosis (from vomiting)
• Dehydration + hemoconcentration → hypotension → shock
