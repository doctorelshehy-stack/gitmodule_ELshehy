Mesenteric Ischemia

Acute Mesenteric Ischemia
Etiology:
• Arterial: Embolic (AF, mural thrombus) / Thrombotic (atherosclerosis)
• Venous: Portal HTN, hypercoagulable states (ATIII, C/S deficiency)
Pathophysiology:
• Rich collateral flow — gradual occlusion tolerated
• Sudden occlusion → poor tolerance
• Tissue injury → anaerobic metabolites
• Mucosal compromise → toxic influx
• Full-thickness necrosis → perforation
Classic Presentation:
• Middle-aged/elderly with cardiac arrhythmia
• Severe abdominal pain OUT OF PROPORTION to exam
• Initially unremarkable abdominal signs
• Signs appear only when peritonitis develops
Chronic Mesenteric Ischemia
• Older age group
• Diffuse atherosclerosis (aorta + mesenteric arteries)
• Gradual occlusion tolerated via collaterals
DD of Acute Ischemia:
• Acute pancreatitis
• Perforated viscus
• Ruptured aneurysm
• Kidney stone
Diagnosis & Treatment
Investigations:
• Marked leukocytosis + metabolic acidosis
• X-ray: fluid-filled loops, wall edema
• Duplex scanning (visceral circulation)
• Arteriography (planning reconstruction)
• CT/MRI (venous clots)
Treatment:
• Rapid fluid resuscitation + antibiotics
• Exploratory laparotomy:
— Necrotic bowel → resection + anastomosis
— Patchy necrosis → thromboembolectomy
— Reversible ischemia → "second look" at 24-36h
