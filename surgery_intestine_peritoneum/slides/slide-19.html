Summary

Intestinal Obstruction
• Mechanical (dynamic) vs Adynamic (paralytic)
• Adhesions most common cause
• Colicky pain → distention → obstipation
• X-ray: dilated loops, air-fluid levels
• Resuscitation first, then conservative/surgical
• Signs of strangulation → operate
Adynamic Ileus
• Postoperative 24-72h most common
• Distention WITHOUT colicky pain
• May still pass flatus
• Entirely supportive treatment
Mesenteric Ischemia
• Acute: pain out of proportion to exam
• Embolic (AF) or thrombotic (atherosclerosis)
• Venous: hypercoagulable states
• Laparotomy → resection or thromboembolectomy
Colorectal Cancer
• 2/3 in rectum + sigmoid
• Duke's/TNM staging
• Rt hemicolectomy / Lt hemicolectomy / APR
• 100% malignant potential in FPC
Mesenteric Cysts
• Tillaux's sign (moves perpendicular to mesentery)
• Enucleation 1st choice
Peritonitis
• Bacterial, chemical, primary
• Localized vs diffuse
• Guarding, rebound, rigidity
• Erect CXR: free air
• Resuscitation + antibiotics + surgery
Abdominal Abscesses
• Pelvic: most common site
• Subphrenic: swinging pyrexia
"Pus somewhere, pus nowhere, pus under the diaphragm"
• CT-guided drainage is standard
