Colorectal Cancer — Clinical & Staging

Clinical by Site
Lt Colon:
• Change of bowel habits
• PR bleeding (fresh)
• Obstruction (sigmoid)
Rectum/Sigmoid:
• PR bleeding, tenesmus
• Painless unless spread
• Bladder symptoms
• PR: palpable if <10cm
Transverse:
• Vague: dyspepsia, anemia, epigastric mass
Cecum/Ascending:
• Weight loss, iron deficiency anemia
• Hard mass in R abdomen
• RIF pain attacks
Complications
1. Obstruction (20%, left colon)
2. Perforation + peritonitis
3. Fistula (bladder/vagina)
4. Bleeding (chronic, massive rare)
5. Spread complications (ascites, jaundice)
Duke's Staging:
• A: Limited to bowel wall
• B: Beyond bowel wall, no nodes
• C: Regional LN deposits
C1: pararectal/paracolic LNs
C2: LNs at supplying vessels
• D: Distant metastasis
TNM Staging
T:
• Tis: carcinoma in situ
• T1: submucosa | T2: muscularis propria
• T3: perirectal tissues
• T4a: visceral peritoneum | T4b: adjacent structures
N:
• N1: 1-3 regional LNs
• N2: ≥4 regional LNs
M:
• M1a: 1 organ/site
• M1b: ≥2 sites
• M1c: peritoneal metastasis
