Adynamic Ileus & Pseudo-obstruction

Paralytic Ileus
Definition:
• Intestinal distention + slowing/absence of passage
• WITHOUT demonstrable mechanical obstruction
Causes:
• Neurogenic: Postoperative (24-72h), spinal injury
• Metabolic: Hypokalemia, hyponatremia, hypomagnesemia, uremia, diabetic coma
• Drugs: Opiates, psychotropics, anticholinergics
• Infectious: Peritonitis, sepsis
• Other: Retroperitoneal hemorrhage, intestinal ischemia
Key Finding:
• Distention WITHOUT colicky pain
• May still pass flatus/diarrhea
Treatment (Supportive)
I. Nasogastric decompression
II. IV fluids + NPO
III. Correct underlying cause
IV. Guanethidine (block sympathetic)
V. Bethanechol/neostigmine (stimulate parasympathetic)
VI. CCK or motilin (hormonal)
VII. Domperidone/erythromycin (resistant cases)
Key Points:
• Abdominal X-ray: distended small + large bowel
• Barium studies if difficult to differentiate from obstruction
• ENTIRELY SUPPORTIVE treatment
• Must exclude intraperitoneal cause
