Mesenteric Cysts

Classification
• Small intestine: 60% | Colon: 40%
Types:
1) Chylolymphatic (commonest)
— Congenitally misplaced lymphatics
— No efferent communication
— Ileum most common site
— Solitary, often unilocular
— Independent blood supply → enucleation possible
2) Enterogenous
— From diverticulum or duplication
— Common blood supply with bowel → resection needed
3) Urogenital remnant
4) Dermoid
Clinical Features
Most common: Painless abdominal swelling
Physical Signs:
• Fluctuant swelling near umbilicus
• Tillaux's sign: moves at right angles to mesenteric attachment
• Zone of resonance around cyst
Other Presentations:
• Recurrent abdominal pain (food bolus impaction)
• Acute abdomen (torsion, rupture, hemorrhage)
Epidemiology:
• Rare, F>M (2:1)
• Mean age: 45 years
Investigation & Treatment
Investigations:
• US and CT (demonstrate lesion, cyst type)
Treatment (Surgery):
1st choice: Enucleation
• Possible if independent blood supply (chylolymphatic)
2nd choice: Bowel resection
• If enucleation not possible or bowel viability concerns
3rd choice: Partial excision + marsupialisation
• For deep/root cysts
• Sclerose lining: 10% glucose, diathermy, or tincture of iodine
