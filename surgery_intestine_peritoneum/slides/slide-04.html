Mechanical Obstruction — Etiology

Extrinsic Causes
1) Adhesions (most common)
• Post-surgical (pelvic surgery)
• Peritoneal irritation → fibrin → adhesions
2) Extrinsic neoplasms (20%)
• Carcinomatosis
• Extra-intestinal neoplasm
3) Hernia (10%)
• External: inguinal, femoral, umbilical
• Internal: congenital, postoperative
4) Intra-abdominal abscess
5) Volvulus
• Bowel rotation around mesentery
• >180° → obstruction
• >360° → vascular occlusion
• Coffee bean sign (sigmoid volvulus)
Intrinsic (Wall) Causes
Congenital: malrotation, duplications
Inflammatory:
• Crohn's (5%) — strictures
• TB, Actinomycosis
Neoplastic: primary/metastatic
Traumatic: hematoma, ischemic stricture
Miscellaneous:
• Intussusception
• Endometriosis
• Radiation enteropathy
Intraluminal (Obturator)
• Gallstone (Rigler's triad)
• Bezoar (phyto/tricho)
• Ascaris worms
Rigler's Triad (Gallstone Ileus)
1. Small bowel obstruction
2. Pneumobilia
3. Radio-opaque shadow in RIF
Gallstone Ileus:
• Elderly patients
• Gallstone erodes through GB into duodenum
• Impacted ~60 cm proximal to ileocecal valve
Volvulus Risk Factors:
• Redundant sigmoid colon
• Constipation, high-fiber diet
• Neurological disease, immobility
