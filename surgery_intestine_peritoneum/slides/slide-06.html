Diagnosis of Obstruction

Simple vs Strangulated
Simple:
• Mechanical blockage only
• Bowel wall viable
Strangulated:
• Vascular supply COMPROMISED
• → Intestinal infarction
• Mostly closed-loop
Signs of Strangulation:
• Tachycardia, fever
• Constant non-cramping pain
• Tenderness with rigidity
• Leukocytosis
• ↑ LDH, amylase, ALP
Physical Examination
General:
• Tachycardia, hypotension
• Dehydration (dry skin/tongue)
• Fever → strangulation
• Hypothermia → septic shock
Abdomen:
• Distention
• Previous scars
• Rule out hernias
• Early: hyperactive bowel sounds (borborygmi)
• Late: minimal/no sounds
• Localized tenderness → peritonitis
Rectal Exam:
• Intraluminal masses
• Occult blood
Investigations
Labs:
• Elevated hematocrit (dehydration)
• Leukocytosis (strangulation)
• ↓ Na, Cl, K
Supine X-ray:
• Dilated loops
• Jejunum: valvulae conniventes (concertina)
• Ileum: featureless
• Colon: haustra (irregular)
Erect X-ray:
• Multiple air-fluid levels
CT scan:
• Sensitive for complete obstruction
• Determines location & cause
• Detects strangulation
Water-soluble contrast:
• Diagnostic + therapeutic (adhesions)
