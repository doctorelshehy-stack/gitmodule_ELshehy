Colorectal Tumors — Polyps & FPC

Benign Tumors (Polyps)
Epithelial:
• Adenoma (solitary or multiple)
• Familial Polyposis Coli (FPC)
Hamartomas:
• Juvenile polyps (solitary/multiple)
• Peutz-Jeghers polyps
Mesenchymal:
• Lipoma, Leiomyoma, Hemangioma
FPC (Familial Polyposis Coli)
• Autosomal dominant (M=F)
• Site: sigmoid + rectum (commonest)
• ≥100 polyps (sessile + pedunculated)
• Histology: tubular, villous, tubule-villous
100% malignant potential if untreated
• Malignancy related to polyp size
• Villous adenoma ≥2cm = highest risk
Tx: Pan proctocolectomy + ileoanal anastomosis + ileal reservoir
Hereditary Syndromes
Gardner's Syndrome:
• FPC + desmoid tumors
• Osteochondroma, sebaceous cysts
Peutz-Jeghers:
• AD, multiple polyps (stomach, SI, colon)
• Melanotic pigmentation (skin/mucous membranes)
• Malignant potential: 2%
• Intussusception, bleeding, anemia
Lynch Syndrome:
• Type I: cancer colon
• Type II: cancer colon ± endometrial/ovarian
• More on Rt side, better prognosis
Li-Fraumeni:
• Defective P53 gene
• Cancer breast, endometrium, colorectal
