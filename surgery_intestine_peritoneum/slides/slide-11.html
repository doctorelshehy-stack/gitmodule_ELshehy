Colorectal Cancer — Pathology

Incidence & Risk Factors
• 3rd leading cause of death (West)
• Incidence ↑ with age (doubles each decade after 40)
• Peak: 65-74 years
• Right colon: more common in women
• Rectum: more common in men
Risk Factors:
• Low fiber, high animal protein diet
• Smoking, alcohol
• IBD (pan-colitis >10 years)
• Villous adenoma >2cm
• FPC, Gardner's (100% by 5th decade)
• Lynch syndrome, Li-Fraumeni
• APC, K-RAS mutations
Pathology
Site:
• 2/3: rectum + sigmoid
• 25%: cecum + right colon
• 5-7%: multiple tumors
Macroscopic:
• Annular (obstruction)
• Tubular
• Ulcerative (most common, left side)
• Cauliflower (least malignant, right side)
Microscopic:
• Adenocarcinoma
• Colloid, Anaplastic
• Rare: melanoma (rectum), carcinoid, lymphoma, GIST
Spread
Direct:
• Mainly upward in colonic wall
• Then to neighboring organs
• Fascia of Denonvilliers (rectum → bladder/prostate barrier)
Lymphatic:
• Right/transverse → superior mesenteric LNs
• Left colon → inferior mesenteric LNs
• Nodal involvement: 40%
Blood:
• Mainly to liver (20% hepatic mets)
Transcelomic:
• Peritoneal nodules + ascites
