Peritoneum — Anatomy & Function

Anatomy
• Largest cavity in the body
Parietal Peritoneum:
• Lines abdominal cavity surfaces
• Richly supplied with nerves
• Irritation → severe, well-localized pain
Visceral Peritoneum:
• Surrounds viscera
• Poorly supplied with nerves
• Irritation → poorly localized midline pain
Function
Peritoneal Fluid:
• Few mL in health
• Lubricates viscera
• Allows easy movement + peristalsis
• Capacity to absorb large volumes (peritoneal dialysis)
Circulation:
• Expiration → ↓ intra-abdominal pressure
• Fluid travels upward to diaphragm
• Bacteria/particles absorbed through diaphragmatic pores
• → Lymphatic network
Clinical Significance:
• May explain distant abscesses from primary disease
Retroperitoneal Space
Psoas (Retroperitoneal) Abscess:
Etiology:
• TB spine (Pott's disease) — historical
• Direct spread from GI/urinary tract
• Hematogenous (immunocompromised, elderly, IV drug use)
Presentation:
• Back pain, lassitude, fever
• Swelling pointing to groin
• Hip extension pain, fixed flexion
Tx:
• CT-guided percutaneous drainage
• Appropriate antibiotics
