Medical Causes of Acute Abdominal Pain (cont.)

Urinary Causes

  • Urinary tract infection
  • Urinary calculi

Female Genital System

  • Threatened abortion
  • Hematocolpos
  • Dysmenorrhea
  • Pelvic inflammatory disease

Metabolic Disorders

  • Diabetic ketoacidosis
  • Hypoglycemia
  • Porphyria
  • Acute adrenal insufficiency

Hematological Disorders

  • Sickle cell anaemia
  • Henoch-Schönlein purpura
  • Hemolytic uremic syndrome

Drugs & Toxins

  • Erythromycin
  • Salicylates
  • Lead poisoning
  • Venoms

Pulmonary Causes

  • Pneumonia
  • Diaphragmatic pleurisy

Miscellaneous

  • Infantile colic, Functional pain
  • Pharyngitis and otitis media
  • Angioneurotic edema
  • Familial Mediterranean fever

Acute Abdomen Signs

  • Sudden onset of severe abdominal pain developing over a short time period
  • Guarding: Contraction of abdominal muscles and discomfort when pressing
  • Rigidity: Hardness of abdominal muscles
  • Rebound tenderness: Increase in severe pain when abruptly stops pressing on a localized region
