ORS Preparation & Plan A

ORS Preparation

  • Tap water without boiling; unsafe water needs boiling
  • Packet dissolved in 200 ml water (small soft drink bottle size)
  • Overdilation makes ORS less useful; overconcentration makes it dangerous
  • ORS solution should NOT be boiled after preparation
  • Do not keep prepared solution >24 hours
  • Give by cup and spoon, 1 tsp every 1-2 minutes
  • If vomiting: rest 10 min, restart slowly. If continues >4 times/hr → NGT

Limitations of ORS

Not suitable in:

  • Severe dehydration
  • Paralytic ileus
  • Rapid stool losses (>15 ml/kg/hr) with inability to drink sufficient rate
  • Glucose malabsorption (rare, short period)

Plan A – Home Therapy (No Dehydration)

Give More Fluids

  • Salted drinks: ORS, chicken soup, rice water
  • Unsalted drinks: Plain water, rice water, unsweetened tea, lemonade, fresh fruit juice
  • Avoid: Soft drinks, carbonated beverages, canned fruit juices
  • Amount after each loose stool: 50-100 ml (<2 yrs), 100-200 ml (>2 yrs)
  • ORS by cup and spoon 1 tsp/1-2 min

Continue Feeding

  • Breastfeeding continued frequently
  • Formula feeding continued as usual
  • Soft complementary foods (>6 mo): mashed potatoes, vegetable soup, yoghurt, mashed bananas (rich in K), beans, lentils, meat, fish, egg yolk
  • Add 1 tsp vegetable oil to each serving
  • Give extra meal daily after recovery for 2 weeks

Zinc: 10-20 mg/day for 10-14 days

Red Flags for Mothers: Many watery stools | Eating/drinking poorly | Repeated vomiting | Becomes very thirsty | Fever | Blood in stools | Does not get better in 3 days
