Diarrhea — Definitions

  • Diarrhea: Increased frequency &/or fluidity of the stool deviated from the normal habit at least 3 times per 24hrs.
  • Dysentery: Diarrhea with visible blood in stool.

Duration of Diarrhea

Acute Diarrhea

Duration < 2 weeks

Persistent/Chronic Diarrhea

Duration ≥ 2 weeks

Etiology of Acute Diarrhea

Infective (Gastroenteritis)

  • Bacterial (more serious, mostly Summer): Salmonella, E. coli, Shigella, Campylobacter jejuni, Staphylococci, Cholera, Yersinia enterocolitica
  • Viral (commoner in winter): Rota virus, Noro virus
  • Parasitic (usually not severe): Entamoeba histolytica, Giardia lamblia

Non-Infective

  • Diet: Over feeding, irregular feeding, inappropriate foods
  • Drug: Antibiotics, especially oral ampicillin
  • Diseases: Parenteral diarrhea (Respiratory or UTI)
