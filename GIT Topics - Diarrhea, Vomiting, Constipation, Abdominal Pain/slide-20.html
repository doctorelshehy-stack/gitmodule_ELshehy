Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)

Physiological Reflux

  • Vomiting is the most common symptom in infants
  • During first 3 months, recurrent vomiting occurs in 50% of infants
  • Incidence of recurrent vomiting peaks at age 4 months

Pathological Reflux (GERD)

  • Presence of associated symptoms: anemia, irritability, feeding resistance, hematemesis, failure to thrive
  • Less than 5% of infants with GERD carry this problem into childhood

Clinical Presentation by Age

Pre-school Children

  • Intermittent vomiting
  • Abdominal pain
  • Unexplained nighttime awakening
  • Feeding resistance
  • Respiratory symptoms

Older Children & Adolescents

  • Heartburn
  • Epigastric pain, chest pain
  • Nocturnal pain
  • Dysphagia or water brash
  • Erosive esophagitis → anemia, hypoalbuminemia, hematemesis, melena
  • Barrett’s esophagus may occur in children
