Recurrent Abdominal Pain (RAP)

RAP is probably the most common pain complaint below 15 years, but cause is not defined in many cases.

Modified Apley and Naish Criteria

  • Three episodes of pain over at least three months affecting the child’s ability to perform normal activities (sleep, study) with symptom-free interval between episodes
  • The severe phase of pain lasts at least 3 minutes
  • Usually seen in children above 3 years of age
  • The pain peaks between ages 5 to 7 years and tends to fall in intensity and frequency around 10 years

Classification of RAP

Organic Pain

Pain due to identifiable organic pathology

Dysfunctional

Abdominal pain in absence of anatomic abnormality, inflammation, or tissue damage. Most common form of RAP.

Psychiatric

Pain seen as part of psychiatric illness
