Abdominal Pain — Introduction

Abdominal pain is one of the most common complaints in childhood. The cause is typically a self-limited minor condition (constipation, gastroenteritis, viral infection), but the challenge is to identify few cases with life-threatening conditions.

Acute Abdominal Pain

< 4-6 weeks (sub-acute < 12 weeks), single episode, localized pain, sharp/stabbing

Surgical Causes

  • Acute Appendicitis
  • Intussusceptions
  • Intestinal obstruction
  • Renal calculus
  • Ectopic pregnancy
  • Ovarian/testicular torsion
  • Abdominal trauma
  • Peritonitis
  • Meckel’s diverticulum

Medical Causes – GIT

  • Gastroenteritis
  • Inflammatory bowel disease
  • Mesenteric lymphadenitis
  • Constipation
  • Peritonitis
  • Food poisoning
  • Peptic ulcer
  • Lactose intolerance

Liver, Spleen, Biliary

  • Hepatitis, Pancreatitis, Cholecystitis, Cholelithiasis, Splenic infarction, Rupture of spleen
