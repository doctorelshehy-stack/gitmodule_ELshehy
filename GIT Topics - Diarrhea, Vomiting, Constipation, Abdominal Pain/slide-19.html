Hypertrophic Pyloric Stenosis

Definition

Progressive hypertrophy of circular muscle fibers of the pylorus with subsequent pyloric narrowing and gastric outflow obstruction.

Etiology

  • Unknown
  • Abnormal muscle innervations
  • Elevated serum levels of prostaglandins
  • Reduced levels of pyloric nitric oxide synthase
  • Infant hypergastrinemia

Clinical Manifestations

  • Nonbilious vomiting ± projectile initially & progressive, immediately after feeding
  • May follow each feeding or be intermittent
  • Starts after 3 wk of age (as early as 1st wk, as late as 5th mo)
  • After vomiting, infant is hungry and wants to feed again
  • Chronic malnutrition and severe dehydration
  • Jaundice in 5%

Diagnosis

Clinical palpation: Firm, movable, ~2cm olive-shaped mass, best palpated from left side, above and to right of umbilicus in mid epigastrium beneath liver edge. Visible gastric peristaltic wave may be seen after feeding.

Radiology

  • Ultrasound: Pyloric thickness >4 mm or pyloric length >14 mm
  • Barium studies: Elongated pyloric channel, bulge of muscle into antrum (shoulder sign), parallel streaks of barium (double tract sign)

Lab: Hypochloremic metabolic alkalosis

Treatment

  • Preoperative: Correct fluid, acid-base, and electrolyte losses. Continue until rehydrated and serum bicarbonate <30 mEq/dL to prevent postoperative apnea.
  • Surgery: Ramstedt pyloromyotomy (procedure of choice)
