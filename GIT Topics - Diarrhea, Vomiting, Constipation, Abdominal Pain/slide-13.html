Plan B & Plan C

Plan B — Dehydration Centre

Fluid Therapy (ORS)

Amount: 75 ml/kg (guided by thirst, stop when child refuses, usually after 4 hr)

Rate & Method:

  • 1 tsp/1-2 min by cup and spoon; older children from a cup
  • Young infants unable to take spoon: dropper, syringe or NGT
  • Mother sleepy and unable to give ORS: NGT
  • Allow breastfeeding; other foods only after 4 hours

Monitoring & Troubleshooting

  • Puffy eyes = overhydration → stop ORS, give breastmilk/water
  • Repeated vomiting: slow ORS (1/2 tsp every 2-3 min)
  • Persisting vomiting: ORS by NGT 20 ml/kg/hr for 6 hr
  • Refusal/no dehydration → Plan A
  • Still dehydrated, weak/unable to drink → NGT
  • Increasing stool output > drinking ability → consider glucose intolerance, change to IV fluid

Reassess after 4 hr: No dehydration → Plan A. Some dehydration → repeat Plan B. Severe → Plan C.

Plan C — Hospital (IV)

IV Ringer’s Lactate 100 ml/kg

Age First 30 ml/kg Then 70 ml/kg
≥ 12 months 1 hour 5 hours
≤ 12 months ½ hour 2½ hours

If Ringer’s lactate not available: Use normal saline. Never glucose 5% → leads to water intoxication, brain edema, convulsions.

Monitor

  • Pulse and respiratory rates
  • Amount of fluid received
  • Improved level of consciousness and ability to drink
  • By end of IV therapy, reassess and choose plan accordingly
