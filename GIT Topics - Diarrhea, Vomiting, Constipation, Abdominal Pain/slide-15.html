Prevention of Diarrhea

Hospitalized Patients

  • Handwashing before and after patient contact
  • Gloves when touching contaminated material

Notification to Health Department

Required for cases caused by: C. botulinum, E. coli O157:H7, Salmonella, Shigella, V. cholerae, Cryptosporidium, Cyclospora, also during outbreaks due to food-borne or water-borne diseases.

Vaccines

  • Available for Salmonella typhi, Shigella, ETEC
  • Newer rotavirus vaccine introduced with no risk of intussusception

Promotion of Exclusive Breastfeeding

Protects very young infants through passive immunity and reduction in intake of contaminated food/water. Continued breast milk during diarrhea diminishes adverse impact on nutritional status.

Improved Complementary Feeding

Complementary foods introduced at 6 mo of age while breastfeeding continues up to 1 yr (or longer). In developing countries, complementary foods are often poor in quality and heavily contaminated, predisposing to malnutrition and diarrhea.

Water, Sanitation & Hygiene

Improved water and sanitary facilities, promotion of personal and domestic hygiene. Routine hand washing with plain soap reduces diarrhea incidence. WHO/UNICEF recommend low osmolality ORS, zinc supplementation, and antibiotic therapy to reduce diarrheal deaths.
