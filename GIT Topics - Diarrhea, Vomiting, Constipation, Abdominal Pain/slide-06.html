Complications of Gastroenteritis (1)

1. Dehydration

Cause: Loss of ECF

Manifestations: Sunken eyes, depressed AF, dry tongue, lost skin turgor

Types:

  • Isonatraemic (Na 130-150): Balanced loss. Normal osmolality (275-295)
  • Hypernatraemic (Na >150): Water loss > Na. Raised osmolality (>295). Most common in young infants. May result from hypertonic fluids.
  • Hyponatraemic (Na <130): Na loss > water. Low osmolality (<275). Results from hypotonic fluids.

Clinical differentiation between the 3 types is not possible with reasonable accuracy; not essential as treatment is the same.

2. Shock

Types: Hypovolemic shock (Loss of ECF), Septic shock may also occur

Manifestations: Tachycardia, hypotension, poor peripheral perfusion (pallor, mottling, coldness), oliguria

3. Acute Renal Failure

Cause: ↓↓ Renal perfusion (Prerenal failure) → renal failure. Also, may develop HUS

Manifestations: Oliguria, anuria, acidosis

4. Metabolic Acidosis

Cause: Lactic acidosis (Tissue hypoperfusion) & renal failure

Manifestations: Deep rapid respiration (Acidotic breathing)
