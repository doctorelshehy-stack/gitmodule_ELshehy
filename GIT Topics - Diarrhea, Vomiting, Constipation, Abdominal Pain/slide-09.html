Assessment of the Child with Diarrhea

History

  • Presence of blood in the stool
  • Duration of diarrhea
  • Number of watery stools and vomiting episodes/day
  • Presence of fever, cough, convulsions
  • Pre-illness feeding practices
  • Type and amount of fluids, breast milk, food taken during illness
  • Drugs or other remedies taken
  • Immunization history

Physical Examination

  • General condition: Alert, restless/irritable, lethargic/unconscious
  • Eyes: Normal or sunken?
  • Thirst: Offer water/ORS – taken normally, refused, eagerly, or unable?
  • Skin turgor: Pinch abdomen – flattens immediately, slowly, or very slowly (>2 sec)
  • Malnutrition: Muscle wasting (marasmus), edema of feet (kwashiorkor)
  • Weight-for-age, weight-for-length (growth chart)
  • Check for pneumonia: Cough, rapid breathing, chest indrawing
  • Temperature: Fever may be from severe dehydration or non-intestinal infection (pneumonia, UTI)
