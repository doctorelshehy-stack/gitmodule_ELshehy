Hirschsprung Disease (Congenital Aganglionic Megacolon)

Pathology

  • Absence of ganglionic cells in myenteric & submucosal plexuses of rectum & variable distance of colon (Neurocristopathy; arrest of neuroblast migration)
  • Rectosigmoid (80%), long segment (15%), total bowel (5%)
  • Failure of relaxation of bowel wall (Obstruction)
  • Distension, stasis & devitalization → Enterocolitis (C. difficile, Staph., anaerobes)
  • Incidence: 1:5,000 live births (M:F = 4:1)
  • Sporadic (AD & AR demonstrated)

Associations & Clinical Picture

Associations:

  • Down syndrome, CVS or urogenital anomalies

Clinical Picture:

  • Neonatal period: Delayed passage of meconium, Intestinal obstruction, Hirschsprung’s enterocolitis
  • Childhood: Chronic constipation

Investigations

  • PR examination
  • Barium enema (transition zone)
  • Anorectal manometry
  • Suction rectal biopsy: Gold standard

Treatment

  • Surgical (usually 1-stage pull-through procedure)
  • May be laparoscopic
  • Narrow segment resection
  • Gush of liquid stools & flatus after procedure
