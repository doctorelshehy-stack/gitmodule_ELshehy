Rome IV Criteria & Red Flags

Rome IV Criteria

Standardized system to assist with diagnosis and management of childhood functional GI disorders. Includes:

  • Functional dyspepsia
  • Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)
  • Functional abdominal pain – NOS
  • Abdominal migraine

General Criteria (Dysfunctional Pain)

  • Paroxysmal pain with variable severity
  • Clustering of pain
  • Gradual onset
  • Usually periumbilical, occasionally epigastric
  • Poor relationship to food/defecation
  • Inability to clearly describe nature or location
  • Associated symptoms (30%): pallor, nausea, fatigue, anxiety

Red Flags for Organic Pain

  • Clearly localized pain (burning, stabbing) away from umbilicus
  • Radiating pain
  • Pain awakening child at night
  • Pain with fever
  • Pain with weight loss
  • Tenderness/organomegaly
  • Blood in stools (occult/obvious)
  • Altered bowel movements
  • Anemia
  • Urinary symptoms
  • ↑ ESR and ↑ CRP
  • Arthralgia, rash, purpura
