GERD — Diagnostic Evaluation & Treatment

Diagnostic Evaluation

  • UGI contrast → Anatomic abnormalities
  • Esophageal pH monitoring – Valuable for evaluating correlation between acid reflux and atypical symptoms (chronic cough, stridor, wheezing, apnea, irritability)
  • Esophagoscopy with biopsy – Accurate way to diagnose reflux esophagitis. Markedly increased eosinophils (>15 eosinophils/hpf) suggests eosinophilic esophagitis

Treatment Options

As in adults, treatment options include:

  • Lifestyle changes – Feeding modifications, thickened feeds, positioning (elevated head)
  • Pharmacological – Acid suppression (PPIs, H2 blockers), prokinetic agents
  • Surgery – Fundoplication for refractory cases
