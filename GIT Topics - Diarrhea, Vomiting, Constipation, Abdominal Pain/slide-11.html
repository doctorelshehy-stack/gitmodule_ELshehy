Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS)

Goal of Rehydration Therapy

  • Correct existing deficits of fluids and electrolytes (initial rehydration phase)
  • Replace further losses (maintenance phase) until diarrhea stops

ORS Composition

  • Water, electrolytes (Na, K, Cl) to replace losses
  • Base (citrate or bicarbonate) to correct acidosis
  • Glucose (certain amount) to promote sodium absorption (co-transport)

Standard WHO ORS: 311 mmol/L
Adding more glucose/sugar increases diarrhea (osmotic effect).
Glucose can be replaced by sucrose, cooked rice, peptides, aminoacids.
Rice powder (50 g/L) decreases stool volume in cholera.

Types of ORS

Standard Glucose-Based

90 mmol Na (Rehydran). Large WHO packet in 1000 ml water: NaCl 3.5g, KCl 1.5g, trisodium citrate 2.9g, glucose 20g (111 mmol). Used only for children >5 years.

Low Osmolar ORS

75 mmol Na (Hydrosafe, Ohydran). Lower NaCl 2.6g, lower glucose 13.5g (70 mmol). Reduces stool output and duration of diarrhea. Safer if used excessively.

Zinc-ORS (Rehydro-zinc)

Similar to low osmolar ORS with added zinc.
