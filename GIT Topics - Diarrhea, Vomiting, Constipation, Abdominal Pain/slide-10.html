Determine Degree of Dehydration

A – No Dehydration

<5% of body wt (<50 ml/kg)

B – Some Dehydration

5-10% of body wt (50-100 ml/kg)

C – Severe Dehydration

>10% of body wt (>100 ml/kg)

Assessment A – No Dehydration B – Some Dehydration C – Severe Dehydration
Condition Well, alert Restless, Irritable Lethargy, coma
Eyes Normal Sunken Sunken
Thirst Drinks normally Thirsty, drinks eagerly Drinks poorly or not able
Skin pinch Goes back quickly Goes back slowly Goes back very slowly (>2 sec)
Treat Plan A Plan B Plan C urgently

* In some children, eyes are normally somewhat sunken. Ask mother if eyes are more sunken than usual.

Diagnose Other Problems:

Dysentery (blood in stools), Persistent diarrhea, Malnutrition, Associated bacterial extra-intestinal infection.
