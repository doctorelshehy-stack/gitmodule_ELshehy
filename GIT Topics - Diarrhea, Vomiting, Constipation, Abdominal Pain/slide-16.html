Persistent Diarrhea

Definition: Diarrhea that persists for ≥ 14 days after acute GE  |  Incidence: 5-20% of acute GE

1. Lactose Intolerance

Pathogenesis: Disaccharidases on brush border. GE destroys brush border → loss of disaccharidases (mainly lactase). ↑↑ lactose → osmotic diarrhea → acids (acidic stools) → gases (abdominal distension)

Diagnosis: Watery diarrhea. Stool analysis: acidic stools + reducing substances.

2. Cow’s Milk Protein Allergy

Pathogenesis: Normally GIT impermeable to cow’s milk proteins. Damage of GIT wall (GE) → Intestinal allergy & inflammation. Mucus & blood (frank or occult) in stools.

Diagnosis: Withdrawal of cow’s milk improves diarrhea (reappears with reintroduction). Stool analysis: mucus & blood.

3. Bacterial Overgrowth

Pathogenesis: Normally upper small intestine is sterile. After acute GE, colonic bacteria may invade upper small intestines. Bacterial invasion → damage of mucosa → Persistent diarrhea

Diagnosis: Stool analysis & culture: Bacteria

4. Persistent Infections: Giardiasis

Pathogenesis: Offending agents: Disaccharides, milk proteins, bacteria. Vicious circle.

Treatment of Persistent Diarrhea

Dietetic Management

  • Lactose intolerance: lactose-free diet
  • Cow’s milk protein allergy: elimination of cow milk products, soy bean-based formula or amino acid-based formula
  • Fat given as medium-chain triglycerides (Pregestimil)

Additional Measures

  • Vitamins: Vitamin A
  • Trace elements
  • Treatment of infection if present
  • Total parenteral nutrition (TPN) may be needed
