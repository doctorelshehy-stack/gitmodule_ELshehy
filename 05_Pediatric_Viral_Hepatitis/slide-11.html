PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Hepatitis E Virus (HEV)

Virology

• Hepeviridae family

• Non-enveloped, single-stranded RNA

• 4 genotypes (1–4)

• Genotypes 1 & 2: human-only (waterborne)

• Genotypes 3 & 4: zoonotic (pork, deer)

Epidemiology

• Fecal-oral (waterborne outbreaks)

• Common in developing countries

• Epidemic potential (contaminated water)

• Endemic in South/Southeast Asia, Africa

Clinical Presentation

• Similar to HAV: self-limited acute hepatitis with jaundice

• Severe in pregnant women (up to 25% mortality in 3rd trimester)

• Can cause chronic hepatitis in immunocompromised patients

• Extrahepatic: neurological (Guillain-Barré), renal involvement

Acute Mortality Rate

1–2% — Higher in pregnant women and immunocompromised

Vaccine Status

NO VACCINE AVAILABLE — Hecolin® exists but not widely available
