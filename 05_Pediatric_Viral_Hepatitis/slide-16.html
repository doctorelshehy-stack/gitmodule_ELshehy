PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Key Points & Summary

HAV — Enteric, Self-Limited

Fecal-oral. 0.1% mortality. Inactivated vaccine (2 doses, 6mo). No chronicity.

HBV — Blood-borne, Chronic Risk

1% mortality. Recombinant vaccine (3 doses). 90% chronicity in neonates.

HCV — Blood-borne, No Vaccine

1–2% mortality. 55–85% become chronic. High mutation rate prevents vaccine.

HDV — Satellite, Most Severe

2–20% mortality. Requires HBV. HBV vaccine protects against HDV.

HEV — Enteric, Waterborne

1–2% mortality. No widely available vaccine. Severe in pregnancy.

Non-Hepatotropic Viruses

CMV, EBV, Rubella, HIV — hepatitis as part of clinical presentation.
