PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Hepatitis C Virus (HCV)

Virology

• Flaviviridae family

• Enveloped, single-stranded RNA

• High mutation rate (quasispecies)

• 6 major genotypes (1–6)

Epidemiology

• Primarily blood-borne transmission

• Vertical transmission: 5–8%

• IV drug use, transfusions (pre-screening era)

• Less common in pediatrics than HBV

Clinical Presentation

Acute Hepatitis C

Often asymptomatic or mild. Most acute infections become chronic (55–85%).

Chronic Hepatitis C

Silent progression to cirrhosis over 20–30 years. HCC risk. Extrahepatic manifestations.

Acute Mortality Rate

1–2% — Higher mortality than HAV/HBV acutely

Vaccine Status

NO VACCINE AVAILABLE — High mutation rate prevents vaccine development
