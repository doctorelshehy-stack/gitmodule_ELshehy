PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Hepatitis A Virus (HAV)

Virology

• Picornaviridae family

• Non-enveloped, single-stranded RNA

• 7 serotypes (no cross-protection)

• Stable in environment

Epidemiology

• Fecal-oral transmission

• Contaminated water/food (shellfish)

• Endemic in developing countries

• Peak: children 5–14 years

Clinical Presentation

Prodrome (children often anicteric)

Fever, malaise, anorexia, nausea, vomiting, RUQ pain

Icteric Phase

Jaundice, dark urine, pale stools, hepatomegaly

Diagnosis

Anti-HAV IgM (acute infection), Anti-HAV IgG (past infection/immunity)

Acute Mortality Rate

0.1% — Lowest among all hepatitis viruses

Self-limited disease. No chronic carrier state. Fulminant hepatitis is very rare.
