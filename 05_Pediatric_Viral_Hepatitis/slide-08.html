PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Hepatitis B Virus (HBV)

Virology

• Hepadnaviridae family

• Enveloped, partially dsDNA

• HBsAg, HBcAg, HBeAg antigens

• Can integrate into host genome

Epidemiology

• Blood, sexual, vertical (perinatal)

• High endemicity in Asia & Africa

• Perinatal transmission: 70–90% if mother HBeAg+

• Risk of chronicity inversely related to age

Clinical Presentation

Acute Hepatitis B

Jaundice, hepatomegaly, fever, malaise. >95% recovery in adults, but 90% chronicity in neonates.

Chronic Hepatitis B

HBsAg persisting >6 months. Can progress to cirrhosis, HCC. Immune tolerance phase in children.

Acute Mortality Rate

1% — Higher than HAV, risk increases with chronicity

Chronic HBV can lead to cirrhosis, hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), and liver failure.
