PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Non-Hepatotropic Viruses Causing Hepatitis

Note: Hepatitis can occur as part of their Clinical Presentation (C/P)

Virus Hepatitis Association Key Features in Pediatrics
CMV
Cytomegalovirus		 Most common cause of viral hepatitis in neonates. Can cause cholestatic hepatitis, hepatosplenomegaly, and jaundice in congenital infection. Congenital CMV: hearing loss, microcephaly, petechiae. Acquired: mononucleosis-like syndrome with hepatitis.
EBV
Epstein-Barr Virus		 Infectious mononucleosis with hepatitis in 80–90% of cases. Usually mild transaminitis. Can cause fulminant hepatitis rarely. Pharyngitis, lymphadenopathy, splenomegaly, atypical lymphocytes. Hepatitis is usually subclinical.
Rubella Congenital rubella syndrome can present with hepatitis, jaundice, and hepatosplenomegaly in neonates. Congenital: cataracts, cardiac defects, deafness, blueberry muffin rash. Hepatitis part of multi-organ involvement.
HIV
Human Immunodeficiency Virus		 HIV-associated hepatitis: direct viral effect, immune reconstitution, drug-induced (ART), or co-infection with HBV/HCV. Chronic liver disease common in HIV+ children. Immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome (IRIS) can cause hepatitis flare.

Clinical Pearl: In any pediatric patient with unexplained hepatitis, consider non-hepatotropic viruses — especially CMV in neonates and EBV in older children.
