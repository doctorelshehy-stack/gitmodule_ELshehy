PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Hepatitis D Virus (HDV)

Virology

• Deltaviridae family (satellite virus)

• Circular, single-stranded RNA

• Requires HBV for replication (HBsAg envelope)

• Can only co-infect or super-infect with HBV

Two Patterns

Co-infection:

Simultaneous HBV + HDV. Usually self-limited. Low chronicity risk.

Super-infection:

HDV on chronic HBV. Severe disease. High risk of cirrhosis & HCC.

Clinical Significance

HDV accelerates liver damage in HBV carriers. Associated with more severe hepatitis, faster progression to cirrhosis, and higher rates of fulminant hepatitis than HBV alone.

Acute Mortality Rate

2–20% — HIGHEST among all hepatitis viruses (wide range)

Vaccine Status

YES — via HBV Vaccine — HBV vaccination prevents HDV (no independent replication without HBV)
