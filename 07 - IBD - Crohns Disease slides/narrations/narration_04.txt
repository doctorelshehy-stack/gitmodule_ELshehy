Now let us focus on Crohn disease specifically! Crohn disease is a chronic inflammatory condition that can affect any part of the digestive tract from the mouth to the bottom. Doctors call this gum to bum disease. The inflammation goes through all layers of the bowel wall which is why it can cause deep ulcers and holes called fistulas. About 135 thousand Canadians live with Crohn disease. It affects about 3 to 20 people out of every 100 thousand worldwide. It usually starts before age 30 with a smaller peak around age 60. Men and women are affected equally. Here is something interesting: smoking actually increases the risk of Crohn disease. And when people move from Asia to Western countries their risk goes up too.
