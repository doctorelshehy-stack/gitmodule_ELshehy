How do doctors diagnose Crohn disease? The most important test is a colonoscopy with biopsies. The doctor looks at the lining of the colon and takes small tissue samples. CT enterography and MR enterography are special imaging tests that look at the small intestine which a regular colonoscope cannot reach. Blood tests are also useful. C reactive protein or CRP is usually elevated during active disease and helps monitor treatment response. Doctors also check for infections that can cause similar symptoms. They test the stool for bacteria parasites and C diff. Putting all these pieces together helps doctors make the right diagnosis and start the right treatment!
