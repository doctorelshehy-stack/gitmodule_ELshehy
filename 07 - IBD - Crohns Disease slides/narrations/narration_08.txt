Let us talk about the first steps in treating Crohn disease! Lifestyle changes are important. The most critical one: stop smoking! Smoking makes Crohn disease much worse. During a bad flare patients may need to drink only liquids to rest the bowel. Special liquid diets called enteral diets can help achieve remission but only for Crohn of the ileum. Antidiarrheal medicines like loperamide can help with symptoms but they must be used carefully during severe flares because they can cause a dangerous complication called toxic megacolon. Five ASA medications like sulfasalazine and mesalamine release anti-inflammatory medicine in the bowel. They are used for mild ileitis but their benefit in Crohn is controversial.
