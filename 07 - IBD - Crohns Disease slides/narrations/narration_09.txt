When first treatments are not enough doctors turn to stronger medications! Antibiotics like metronidazole and ciprofloxacin are especially helpful for perianal Crohn disease. But the symptoms often come back when the antibiotics are stopped. Corticosteroids like prednisone are very effective for acute flares. The typical dose is 40 milligrams daily. For severe disease steroids are given through an IV. But here is the catch: steroids do not work for maintaining remission. They should only be used for short periods to calm a flare. Long term steroid use has many side effects. Also steroids can hide infections like abscesses so doctors need to be careful. They are powerful but need to be used wisely!
