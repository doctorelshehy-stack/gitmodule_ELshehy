SECTION 02 — HEPATORENAL SYNDROME

Pharmacological Treatment

❖ Vasodilators

Dopamine

When used at renal support doses has a renal vasodilatory effect. Although widely used clinically there is no clear evidence of efficacy.

Prostaglandins

Showed no significant effect on renal functions.

❖ Vasoconstrictors

Terlipressin + IV Albumin

Reversal of HRS has been reported in 7/9 patients treated with terlipressin and IV albumin (5-15 days) without side effects.

Midodrine + Octreotide + IV Albumin

Long term combination improved renal functions in 8 patients with type 1 HRS with no side effects.

❖ Antioxidant Therapy

A preliminary uncontrolled study has suggested improvement in renal function after IV N-acetyl cysteine.

Vasoconstrictors (terlipressin, midodrine+octreotide) + albumin are the most promising pharmacological options.
