Summary & Key Takeaways

TB Peritonitis

  • Caused by M. tuberculosis complex (several species)
  • Risk factors: cirrhosis, CRF, malignancy, anti-TNF, DM, immunosuppression, HIV, PD
  • 5 routes: reactivation, hematogeneous, direct spread, LN rupture, PD contamination
  • Forms: wet (ascites), encysted (loculated), fibrotic (masses), overlap
  • Presentation: abdominal pain, swelling, fever, night sweats, weight loss
  • Ascitic fluid: high protein >3g, SAAG <1.1, ADA >39, lymphocytes
  • Gold standard: laparoscopy + peritoneal biopsy (granuloma)
  • Treatment: 4-drug regimen (2 mo RIPE + 4 mo RI)

Hepatorenal Syndrome

  • Functional renal failure in severe liver disease — no identifiable renal pathology
  • 20% of cirrhotics develop HRS in 1 year; median survival <2 weeks without LT
  • Type 1: rapid (<2 wks), 80% mortality; Type 2: slower, ~6 mo survival
  • Pathogenesis: portal HTN → vasodilators → splanchnic vasodilation → ↓ effective volume → RAAS/SNS activation → renal vasoconstriction → ↓ GFR
  • Diagnosis: SCr >1.5, no shock/sepsis/fluid loss, no improvement after volume expansion
  • Treatment: terlipressin + albumin, midodrine + octreotide + albumin
  • TIPS (better in type 2), MARS for type 1
  • Prevention: albumin after large volume paracentesis & in SBP
