IBS — Alarm Features & Diagnosis

Alarm Features (NOT Consistent with IBS)

  • Onset in middle age or older
  • Acute symptoms (IBS is chronic)
  • Progressive symptoms
  • Nocturnal symptoms
  • Anorexia or weight loss
  • Fever
  • Rectal bleeding
  • Painless diarrhea
  • Steatorrhea
  • History of gluten intolerance

Rome III Diagnostic Criteria

Recurrent abdominal pain/discomfort ≥3 days/month for 3 months, with ≥2 of:

  • Relieved by defecation
  • Onset associated with change in stool frequency
  • Onset associated with change in stool form/appearance

Supporting symptoms:

  • Altered stool frequency, form, passage (straining/urgency)
  • Mucorrhea, abdominal bloating/distention

ACG does not recommend lab testing or imaging in patients <50 years with typical IBS symptoms and without alarm features (weight loss, iron deficiency anemia, family history of IBD, celiac, colorectal cancer).
