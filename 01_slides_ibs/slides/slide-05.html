IBS — Signs & Symptoms

1. Abdominal Pain (Mandatory)

  • Frequently diffuse without radiation
  • Common site: lower abdomen, especially left lower quadrant
  • Acute sharp pain superimposed on constant dull ache
  • Meals may precipitate pain
  • Defecation commonly improves but may not fully relieve

2. Abdominal Distention

Bloating and subjective distention are supporting symptoms.

3. Altered Bowel Habits

  • Constipation: hard stools, narrow caliber, painful/infrequent defecation
  • Diarrhea: small volumes loose stool, urgency, frequent defecation
  • Postprandial urgency common
  • Alternation between constipation and diarrhea
  • One feature predominates in single patient

4. Additional Symptoms

  • Clear or white mucorrhea (noninflammatory)
  • Dyspepsia, heartburn
  • Nausea, vomiting
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Urinary frequency and urgency
  • Peri-menstrual worsening
  • Comorbid fibromyalgia
  • Stressor-related symptoms

Bowel Pattern Classification

IBS-D (Diarrhea) IBS-C (Constipation) IBS-M (Mixed) IBS-A (Alternating)
