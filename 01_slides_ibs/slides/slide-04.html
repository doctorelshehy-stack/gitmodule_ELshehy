IBS — Definition & Risk Factors

Definition: Functional GI disorder characterized by abdominal pain and altered bowel habits in the absence of a specific organic pathology, although microscopic inflammation has been documented in some patients.

Prevalence: 10-20% of population

Risk Factors:

  • Female gender
  • Pre-existing life events
  • Anxiety and neurotic scores at initial illness
  • Stress or adverse life events often precede onset
  • Infectious diarrhea precedes IBS in 7-30% of patients

Psychological Association

  • Symptoms of anxiety and depression more common in IBS
  • Stress/adverse life events often precede onset of chronic bowel symptoms

IBS Coexists With:

  • Chronic fatigue syndrome
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Temporomandibular joint dysfunction
  • Sexual dysfunction (dyspareunia, poor libido)
  • Urinary frequency and urgency
  • Worsening peri-menstrual symptoms
