IBS — Dietary Management

Management consists primarily of psychological support and dietary measures. Pharmacologic treatment is adjunctive and directed at symptoms.

Dietary Measures

  • Fiber supplementation: may improve constipation and diarrhea
  • Judicious water intake: recommended for constipation-predominant
  • Caffeine avoidance: may limit anxiety and symptom exacerbation
  • Legume avoidance: may decrease abdominal bloating
  • FODMAPs restriction: fermentable oligosaccharides, disaccharides, monosaccharides, and polyols — limit or avoid
  • Probiotics: being studied for decreasing IBS symptoms

Psychological Interventions

ACG conclusions:

  • Effective: Psychological interventions, cognitive-behavioral therapy, dynamic psychotherapy, hypnotherapy — more effective than placebo
  • Not effective: Relaxation therapy — no more effective than usual care

Key Point

Evidence is mixed regarding long-term improvement in GI symptoms with successful treatment of psychiatric comorbidities.
