Aerophagia, Functional Vomiting & Rumination

Aerophagia

  • Repetitive pattern of swallowing/ingesting air and belching
  • Usually unconscious, unrelated to meals
  • No investigation required
  • Treatment: Explanation + reassurance

Rumination Syndrome

  • Persistent/recurrent effortless regurgitation of recently ingested food
  • Re-mastication and re-swallowing (not preceded by retching)
  • No nausea, vomiting, abdominal discomfort, heartburn
  • Regurgitant has pleasant taste; stops when material becomes acidic
  • Common in individuals with learning difficulties
  • Medical, behavioral, nutritional approaches with varying success

Functional Vomiting

Rare in clinical practice. Characterized by:

  1. Frequent vomiting ≥1 day/week
  2. No eating disorder, rumination, or major psychiatric disease
  3. No self-induced or medication-induced vomiting
  4. No organic abnormalities in gut, CNS, or metabolic disease

Investigation & Treatment:

  • Often not required — always exclude non-GI disorders
  • Treatment: Anti-nausea drugs, antidepressants
  • Behavioral therapy and psychotherapy helpful
  • Dietary changes occasionally help
