PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Key Points & Summary

Amoebiasis

E. histolytica. Cyst = infective. Fecal-oral. Acute dysentery (blood + mucus + tenesmus). Metronidazole 10 days. Diloxanide for cyst passers.

Giardiasis

G. lamblia. Watery diarrhea, greasy floating stools, failure to thrive. Furazolidone (DOC children). Tinidazole single dose.

Ascariasis

Most prevalent helminth. Loeffler syndrome (pulmonary). Intestinal obstruction. Mebendazole × 3 days. Piperazine × 2 days.

Hookworm

Iron deficiency anaemia (0.5–1 ml blood/day/worm). Ground itch. Loeffler. Iron replacement + anti-helminthic.

Tapeworms

T. saginata (beef), T. solium (pork → cysticercosis), H. nana (most common in Egypt). Niclosamide or Praziquantel.

Schistosomiasis

Most important in Egypt. S. haematobium (urinary), S. mansoni (intestinal). Hepato-splenic → portal HTN. Praziquantel DOC.

Immunization

Egypt EPI: Birth (OPV0 + Hep B) → BCG → 2/4/6 mo (DPT + OPV + Hep B) → 9 mo (Measles + Vit A) → 12 mo (MMR) → 18 mo (boosters). Cold chain: 2–8°C. DPT contraindicated in severe reactions/CNS disease. NOT contraindicated in mild illness/antibiotics.
