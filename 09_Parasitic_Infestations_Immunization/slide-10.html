PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Ascariasis — Complete

Most prevalent human helminthic infection worldwide. Pre-school & early school age mostly affected.

Transmission

Hand-to-mouth (soil contact). Contaminated vegetables, flies. Infective stage: mature egg with larva.

Diagnosis

Direct faecal smear → ova

Clinical Picture

• Majority asymptomatic

• Loeffler-like syndrome: cough + blood-stained sputum, dyspnoea + wheezing (asthma) + eosinophilia. CXR: transient pulmonary infiltrates

• Vague abdominal complaints: pain, distention

• Malabsorption: steatorrhoea, diminished vit-A absorption

• Growth retardation in children

• Adult worms may be passed in stools

• Intestinal obstruction in heavy infestation (mass of worms)

• Migration → biliary obstruction, appendicitis

Treatment

• Mebendazole / Flubendazole: 100 mg/day × 3 days all ages PO

• Pyrantel pamoate: 10 mg/kg single dose

• Levamisole: single dose (as enterobius)

• Piperazine: 50–75 mg/kg/day × 2 days
