PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Giardiasis — Complete

Etiology & Transmission

Giardia lamblia — protozoan flagellate inhabiting duodenum & jejunum. Cysts pass in stools, resistant to chlorine. Fecal-oral: person-to-person, water/food-borne.

Clinical Features

More symptomatic in children. Prolonged, intermittent course.

Presentations

• Diarrhea, weight loss, failure to thrive — sudden with gradual onset of watery diarrhea

• Stools: foul odor, greasy, float on water (not flushed easily). No blood, mucus, or tenesmus

• Vague symptoms: anorexia, nausea, vomiting, epigastric pain

• Malabsorption: sugars, fat, fat-soluble vitamins

Diagnosis

Stool examination for cysts/trophozoites. Duodenal aspirate or jejunal biopsies.

Treatment

• Metronidazole: 20 mg/kg/day PO 3 doses × 10 days

• Tinidazole (Fasigyn): single oral dose 50 mg/kg — 80% cure rate

• Furazolidone: 8 mg/kg/day 4 doses × 10 days (drug of choice in children)

• Control: as in amoebiasis
