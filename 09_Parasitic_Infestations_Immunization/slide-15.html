PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Schistosomiasis — Clinical Manifestations

Transmission

S. haematobium (bladder) or S. mansoni (rectum/colon). Excreta in fresh water → specific snails → swimming/wading. Children & young adults most prone.

Early Stages

Swimmer's Itch: cercarial penetration → papular urticarial rash
Katayama fever: serum sickness-like (1 month): fever, chills, lymphadenopathy, hepatosplenomegaly, eosinophilia up to 80%

S. haematobium (Urinary)

• Terminal haematuria (aggravated by exertion), pyuria, frequency, urgency. Haematuria decreases with fibrotic (closed) lesions.

S. mansoni (Intestinal)

• Bilharzial dysentery: diarrhea, tenesmus, blood & mucus. Rectal prolapse, anal fistula/fissure. Colon palpable & tender.

Hepato-Splenic Schistosomiasis

• Ova emboli + dead worms → portal tracts → inflammation → hepatomegaly

• Peri-portal fibrosis → portal hypertension → splenomegaly, porto-systemic anastomoses, ascites

• Hepato-cellular function preserved till late
