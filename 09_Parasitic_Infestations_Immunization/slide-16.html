Schistosomiasis — Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis

• History of canal water contact / endemic area residence

• Direct methods: ova in stool, urine, or tissue biopsies (rectal, bladder, liver). Living ova = active infection.

• Indirect methods: serological diagnosis when ova detection fails.

Anti-Bilharzial Drugs

Praziquantel (Biltricide) 600 mg: 40 mg/kg single dose — DOC for both S. haematobium & S. mansoni

Metrifonate (Bilarcil) 100 mg: 10 mg/kg every 2 weeks × 3 doses — S. haematobium only

Oxamniquine (Vansil) 250 mg: 20 mg/kg/day PO × 3 days — S. mansoni only
