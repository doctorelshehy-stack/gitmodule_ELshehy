PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Amoebiasis — Complications, Investigations & Treatment

Complications

• Intestinal perforation → peritonitis/internal haemorrhage

• Intestinal haemorrhage

• Stricture of large intestine (chronic)

• Intussusception

• Skin extension (anus/colostomy)

• Amoebic hepatitis & liver abscess

Investigations

• Fresh stools: microscopic exam for motile vegetative & cyst forms

• Sigmoidoscopy: visualise amoebic ulcers + biopsy

Treatment

Metronidazole (Flagyl): 50 mg/kg/day PO 10 days (3 doses)

Tinidazole (Fasigyn): 50–60 mg/kg/day PO single dose × 5 days

D. Dehydro-emetine: SC/IM 1 mg/kg/day × 10 days (liver abscess)

Diloxanide furoate (Furamide): 10 mg/kg/day PO 10 days (asymptomatic cyst passers, >2 yrs)

Side effects: nausea, vomiting, metallic taste, leukopenia

Control:

Sanitary measures, fly control, regular examination of food handlers, investigation of diarrheal episodes.
