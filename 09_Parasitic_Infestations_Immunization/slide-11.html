PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Ancylostomiasis — Hookworm

Mild Cases

• May be asymptomatic
• "Ground itch" — skin invasion by larvae
• "Loeffler-like syndrome" — dry cough, fever, wheezes, pulmonary infiltrates, eosinophilia

Adult Worm Effects

• Iron deficiency anaemia (0.5–1 ml blood/day/worm)
• Fatigue, shortness of breath, palpitations
• Puffy face & ankle oedema
• Pica (eating dirt)
• Blood in stool
• Retarded physical/mental/sexual development

Investigations

• Direct faecal smear for ova (concentration method may help)

• Eosinophilia marked during larval migration

• Stools positive for occult blood

• Blood picture: hypochromic microcytic anaemia

Treatment

Anti-helminthic: same as ascariasis

Anaemia: Iron replacement 6 mg/kg/day elemental iron (3 doses). Severe with heart failure: diuretics + slow packed RBC transfusion

Control: general sanitation measures + mass chemotherapy
