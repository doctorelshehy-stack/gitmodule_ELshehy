PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Enterobiasis — Oxyuriasis (Pinworm)

Overview

Enterobius vermicularis — worldwide, especially common in children. More social than medical problems.

Clinical Manifestations

Asymptomatic with small worm load

• Nocturnal anal pruritus (pruritus ani)

• Sleeplessness, irritability, teeth grinding during sleep

• Nocturnal enuresis may occur

• Vulvo-vaginitis in females

• Ectopic sites: appendix (appendicitis), female genito-urinary tract (enuresis, cystitis, salpingitis)

Diagnosis

• Peri-anal skin scrapings / cellophane tape test (early morning)

• Finding worms in peri-anal region (preserved in 75% alcohol for microscopy)

Treatment

• Mebendazole (Vermox) / Flubendazole: single dose 100 mg all ages PO

• Pyrantel pamoate (Combantrin): 10 mg/kg single dose

• Levamisole (Ketrax 40 mg tab): 1–4 yrs: 1 tab; 5–15 yrs: 2 tabs; Adults: 3 tabs

• Piperazine salts: 50 mg/kg PO every evening × 7 days

• White precipitate ointment around anus before sleeping

⚠ Repeat TTT after 2–3 weeks. Treat all infected family members simultaneously. Strict hygiene.
