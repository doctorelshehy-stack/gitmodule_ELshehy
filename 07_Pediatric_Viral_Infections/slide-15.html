PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Mumps (Epidemic Parotitis)

Causative Agent

Mumps virus (RNA paramyxovirus)

Transmission

Droplet + direct contact with fomites

Infectivity

24h before swelling to 3d after

Incubation: 2–3 weeks

Clinical Picture

• Prodrome: Very short. Fever, muscle pain, headache, malaise

• Parotid swelling: Bilateral (80%) or unilateral, painful, tender. Pain increased by sour liquids and chewing. Obliterates angle between mandible and mastoid, raises ear lobule. Stenson duct hyperemic. Subsides in 3–7 days.

• Bidigital examination important to exclude cervical lymphadenitis

• Submandibular and sublingual glands may also be affected

Complications

Meningo-encephalitis, orchitis & oophoritis, acute pancreatitis, thyroiditis, mastitis, myocarditis, arthritis, hepatitis, nephritis, unilateral deafness, ocular complications. Mumps embryopathy: endocardial fibroelastosis

Treatment

Prophylactic: MMR at 15 months. Curative: symptomatic, bed rest, no spices/sour fluids. Orchitis: bed rest, local support, analgesics, corticosteroids/NSAIDs for 2 weeks.
