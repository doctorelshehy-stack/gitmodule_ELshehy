Poliomyelitis — Overview

3 Strains

I (Brunhild) — epidemics
II (Lansing) — common in Egypt
III (Leon)

Transmission

Fecal-oral, naso-pharyngeal (droplet)
Source: only humans (cases & carriers)

Incubation: 7–10 days

Virus persists in stools 6–8 weeks

4 Clinical Forms

1. Silent (90–95%)

No symptoms. Solid immunity. May be carrier for months.

2. Abortive Polio

Non-specific: URI or GIT disturbances. Diagnosis impossible.

3. Non-paralytic Polio

Abortive symptoms + meningeal irritation, transient hyperesthesia, paraesthesia, bladder paralysis. May recover or progress.

4. Paralytic Polio

Acute flaccid paralysis. Asymmetrical, spotty. Large muscles more affected. Massive in 1st 2d → stationary → slow improvement. No sensory loss.
