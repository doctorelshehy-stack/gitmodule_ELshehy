PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Erythema Infectiosum (Fifth Disease)

Causative Agent

Parvovirus B19 (also causes aplastic crises in chronic hemolytic anemia)

Incubation: 1 week

No prodrome, fever absent or very mild

Rash — 3 Stages

1. Slapped Cheek

Sudden erythema of cheeks

2. Trunk & Limbs

Erythematous maculo-papular rash

3. Lacy/Reticulated

Central clearing, characteristic pattern

Rash lasts 2–40 days, pruritic, resolves without desquamation. Tends to recur with exercise, warm baths, rubbing, emotional upsets.

Complications & Treatment

Rare: arthritis, hemolytic anemia, pneumonia, encephalopathy. No treatment needed.
