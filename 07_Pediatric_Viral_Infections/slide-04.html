PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Measles — Overview

Transmission

Droplet from a case

Incubation

10 days

Infectivity

7th day after exposure to 5th day after rash onset

Prodromal Stage (4 days before rash)

• High fever • Dry irritating cough (bronchitis) • Running nose (conjunctivitis)

• Koplik spots — pathognomonic: small grayish-white dots on erythematous buccal mucosa opposite 2nd molar teeth, 1–2 days before rash

• Generalized lymphoid hyperplasia

Eruptive Stage

• Maculo-papular rash, may be confluent, hemorrhagic in severe cases ("Black measles")

• Starts at hairline → face → neck → UL → LL → feet (by day 3)

• Fades in same sequence

Convalescence

• Fine "branny desquamation" (not hands/feet) • Brownish discoloration (7–10 days)
