PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

German Measles (Rubella)

Causative Agent

Rubella virus (RNA virus)

Incubation

2–3 weeks

Transmission

Droplet, transplacental

Infectivity

7d before rash to 5d after. Congenital: up to 2 years

Prodrome

Very short, mild URI, fever (<38.5°C), malaise, anorexia, headache. Tender lymphadenopathy: sub-occipital, post-auricular, posterior cervical (may persist after rash).

Eruptive Stage

Maculo-papular, erythematous, discrete rash. Starts on face → LL in 24h → clears by day 3. No desquamation or pigmentation. Mild itching.

Single attack gives solid, life-long immunity

Complications

Encephalitis (1/6000), neuritis, arthritis

Prevention

Avoid contact in pregnancy. Active: live attenuated rubella virus (SC ½ ml) or MMR at 15 months. Passive: ISG ¼–½ ml/kg within 1 week after exposure.

Treatment

Symptomatic. Isolation: avoid contact with pregnant females during infectivity.
