Poliomyelitis — Diagnosis & Prevention

Diagnosis

• History & clinical manifestation

• Virus isolation: stools (6–8 wks from onset), naso-pharynx (2 wks from onset)

• Specific neutralizing Ab (2 wks after infection)

• CSF: differentiate from TB & bacterial (septic) meningitis

Prediction of Paralytic Progression

• Disappearance of superficial reflexes

• Change in deep reflexes

Prevention

Active immunization: Sabin vaccine (oral, live attenuated)
