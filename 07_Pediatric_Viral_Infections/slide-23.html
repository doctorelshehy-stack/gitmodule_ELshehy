PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Key Points & Summary

Measles

Koplik spots, downward rash, branny desquamation. Vitamin A reduces mortality. SSPE complication.

Rubella

Sub-occipital lymphadenopathy. CRS: PDA/VSD, cataract, deafness. MMR at 15 months.

Roseola

HHV-6. Fever drops by crisis at rash onset. Rash: trunk first (unlike measles).

Varicella

Pleomorphic vesicles (crops). ZIG for prophylaxis. Reye syndrome complication.

Fifth Disease

Parvovirus B19. Slapped cheek → lacy reticulated rash. Aplastic crisis in hemolytic anemia.

Mumps

Bilateral parotid swelling, sour foods painful. Orchitis, pancreatitis, endocardial fibroelastosis.

Polio

4 forms (silent → paralytic). Asymmetrical flaccid paralysis. Sabin vaccine. No sensory loss.

FUO

>38°C for >14 days. Infectious vs non-infectious causes. Hospitalize for evaluation.
