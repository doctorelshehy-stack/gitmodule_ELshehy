Pancreatic Cancer — Overview

Epidemiology
• 2-3% of all cancers worldwide
• M > F, peak incidence 55-70 years
• 3-year survival <5%
Risk Factors
• Age (65-75), male, black ethnicity
• Smoking
• Genetic: BRCA2, Lynch syndrome, familial breast-ovarian
• Hereditary pancreatitis, DM, chronic pancreatitis
Pathology
• Head (70%), Body/Tail (30%)
• >85% are ductal adenocarcinomas
• Solid, scirrhous, desmoplastic stroma
Spread:
• Direct: duodenum, CBD, pyloric antrum, transverse mesocolon
• Lymphatic: regional nodes at porta hepatis
• Blood: liver (10%), then lungs
• Peritoneal: ascites
Clinical Features
Head tumors:
• Painless jaundice (most common)
• Pruritus, dark urine, pale stools
• Steatorrhea, weight loss
• Courvoisier sign (palpable GB)
Body/Tail tumors:
• Grow silently → late presentation
• Back pain (retroperitoneal infiltration)
Ampullary tumors:
• Present early with biliary obstruction
• Better prognosis than ductal adenocarcinoma
Investigations
• Lab: CBC, PT, LFTs, CA19-9
• US: dilated bile duct, mass, metastases
• Contrast CT: resectability assessment
• MRI/MRA: comparable to CT
• ERCP: stenting + brush cytology
• EUS: if CT fails, tissue diagnosis
• Diagnostic laparoscopy: spare unnecessary laparotomy
