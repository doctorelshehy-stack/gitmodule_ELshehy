Appendicitis — Diagnosis & DD

Investigations
Laboratory
• WCC elevated (>75% neutrophils)
• WCC >20,000 = complicated
• Urinalysis (exclude renal causes)
• Pregnancy test if ectopic suspected
Plain X-ray:
• Appendicolith in 10-15%
• If present + pain = strong support
Ultrasound:
• Appendix ≥7 mm diameter
• Thick-walled, non-compressible
• Appendicolith
• Peri-appendiceal fluid/mass
CT:
• Distended appendix >7 mm
• Wall thickening, fat stranding
• Edema, peritoneal fluid
• Phlegmon or abscess
Differential Diagnosis
Thoracic:
• R pneumonia, R basal pleurisy
Upper abdominal:
• Perforated peptic ulcer
• Acute cholecystitis
• Cyclic vomiting (children)
Lower abdominal:
• Mesenteric lymphadenitis
• Iliac adenitis
• Cecal carcinoma
• Regional ileitis (Crohn's)
• Meckel's diverticulitis
• Gastroenteritis
Female genital:
• Ruptured ectopic, follicle
• Tubal abortion, ovarian torsion
• PID, red degeneration fibroid
Urological:
• Renal colic, pyelonephritis
