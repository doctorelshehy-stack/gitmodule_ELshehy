Appendicitis — Treatment

Uncomplicated Appendicitis
Open Appendectomy:
• Davis-Rockey: transverse RLQ incision
• McArthur-McBurney: oblique incision
Laparoscopic Appendectomy:
• Diagnostic + therapeutic
• Shorter recovery, less pain
• Less conspicuous incisions
Perforated Appendicitis
• Fluid resuscitation before anesthesia
• Broad-spectrum antibiotics (aerobes + anaerobes)
• Laparoscopic preferred (↓ wound infection, ↓ ileus)
• May convert to open if needed
• Exploratory laparotomy in some cases
Appendiceal Abscess
• Abscess >4-6 cm: drainage first
• Transrectal/transvaginal (US-guided)
• Percutaneous image-guided
• Small abscess/phlegmon: antibiotics alone
• Persisting fever → appendectomy same admission
• Improving → interval appendectomy at 6 weeks
Appendicular Mass
Emergency surgery NOT recommended:
1) Mass = body's success at localizing
2) Surgery technically difficult
3) Risk of intestinal injury/fistula
Ochsner-Sherren Regimen:
• Bed rest
• IV fluids until nausea subsides
• Meticulous observation (T, P, pain, mass size)
• IV then oral antibiotics
• Analgesics
Results:
• 80-90% resolve with conservative treatment
• Adults: investigate for colon cancer (5% detected)
• Recurrence: 15-25% → consider interval appendectomy
• Interval LA at 6 weeks: low morbidity, short stay
