Pancreatic Pseudocyst

Definition & Pathology
• Amylase-rich fluid in fibrous/granulation wall
• No epithelial lining (distinguishes from true cyst)
• Represents 80% of pancreatic cysts
• Etiology: acute pancreatitis, pancreatic trauma
• 20-40% resolve spontaneously within 6-7 weeks
Pathophysiology:
• Arises from APFC that hasn't resolved
• Requires ≥4 weeks from onset
• >50% have communication with main pancreatic duct
Sites (in order of frequency):
1) Between stomach and transverse colon
2) Between stomach and liver
3) Behind/below transverse colon
Clinical Features
• History of pancreatitis or trauma
• Epigastric swelling: tense, cystic
• Shows transmitted pulsations from aorta
Investigations
• US or CT scan
• EUS + aspiration (CEA, amylase, cytology)
• Percutaneous FNA (no drain insertion)
• ERCP/MRCP: duct communication, anomalies
Complications
• Rupture, hemorrhage, abscess, CBD obstruction
Management
Conservative: Most resolve spontaneously
• Indications for intervention: symptoms, complications, uncertain diagnosis
Endoscopic: Transgastric cystgastrostomy
Surgery: Only for chronic cyst (>6 weeks)
• Cysto-gastrostomy (trans-gastric route)
• Cysto-jejunostomy (Roux-en-Y 40cm loop)
Percutaneous drainage:
• High recurrence, avoid unless certain non-neoplastic
