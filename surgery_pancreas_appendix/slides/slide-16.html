Appendiceal Tumors

Carcinoid Tumors
• Commonest site in gut (65%)
• Usually benign, 5% may invade/metastasize
Size matters:
• <1 cm: usually benign
• >2 cm: almost malignant
Carcinoid Syndrome:
• Flushing, diarrhea
• Bronchospasm, pulmonary stenosis
• Dx: urinary 5-HIAA
Treatment:
• <1 cm, tip: appendectomy (curative)
• >1-2 cm, base involved, mesoappendix invasion:
→ Right hemicolectomy
Non-Carcinoid Tumors
• Rare, usually present as appendicitis in older patients
• Longer duration of symptoms
• Mild anemia may be present
• Usually not diagnosed preoperatively
Types:
• Mucinous adenocarcinoma (most common)
— Better prognosis than colon type
• Signet-ring cell type
Mucocele:
• Distended appendix with mucus
Treatment:
• Right hemicolectomy for tumors >1 cm
