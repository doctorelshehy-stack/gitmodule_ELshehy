Appendix — Anatomy

Embryology
• Derived from midgut
• Appears at 8th week as cecal outpouching
• Rotates to medial location as gut rotates
Anatomy
• Length: 2-20 cm (average 9 cm in adults)
• Base at convergence of taeniae on cecum
• Tip: variable position
Positions (in order):
• Retrocecal: 74%
• Pelvic: 21%
• Paracecal: 2%
• Subcecal: 1.5%
• Pre-ileal: 1%
• Post-ileal: 0.5%
Subhepatic Appendix:
• Rare congenital anomaly
• Failure of cecal descent
Blood Supply
• Appendiceal artery (branch of ileocolic)
• Runs in mesoappendix close to wall
Clinical Significance
Position affects presentation:
• Retrocecal:
— Minimal abdominal tenderness
— Pain in right loin
— Iliopsoas sign positive
• Pelvic:
— Tenesmus (rectal irritation)
— Dysuria (bladder irritation)
— Obturator sign positive
— Pelvic exam tenderness
• Subhepatic:
— Mimics acute cholecystitis
• Long retrocecal:
— Pain in right loin
— Minimal guarding
