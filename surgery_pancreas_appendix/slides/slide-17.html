Summary — Pancreas & Appendix

Acute Pancreatitis
• Gallstones (Egypt) / Alcohol (West)
• Chemical inflammation, NOT infection
• Ranson's criteria: prognosis
• No surgery during resuscitation
Chronic Pancreatitis
• Alcohol 60-70%
• Irreversible damage, DM, malabsorption
• No specific treatment
Pancreatic Cancer
• Head 70%, Body/Tail 30%
• Painless jaundice (head tumors)
• Only 20% resectable at presentation
• 5-year survival <5%
Pseudocyst
• 80% of pancreatic cysts
• 20-40% resolve spontaneously
• Surgery only for chronic (>6 weeks)
Appendicitis
• Most common surgical emergency
• Migratory pain: periumbilical → RLQ
• McBurney's point tenderness
• Perforation after ≥48 hours
Appendicular Mass
• Ochsner-Sherren regimen
• 80-90% resolve conservatively
• Interval appendectomy at 6 weeks
Appendiceal Tumors
• Carcinoid: <1 cm appendectomy, >2 cm hemicolectomy
