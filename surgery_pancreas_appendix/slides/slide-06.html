Acute Pancreatitis — Complications

Systemic Complications
• Hypovolaemic shock (volume depletion + vasodilatation)
• Cullen's and Grey Turner signs
• DIC (pancreatic enzymes into circulation)
• Disseminated fat necrosis (subcutaneous, extremities)
• Hypocalcaemia (calcium sequestration in necrotic tissue)
• Acute renal failure (ATN)
• Hyperglycemia, Coma
Pancreatic Complications
• Pancreatic pseudocyst (chronic >6 weeks → surgical drainage)
• Pancreatic abscess (high fever)
Pulmonary Complications
• ARDS, pleural effusion, bacterial pneumonia
GI Complications
• Bleeding (erosive duodenitis, gastritis, gastric varices from splenic vein thrombosis)
• Paralytic ileus
Local Complications
1) APFC (Acute Peripancreatic Fluid Collection)
• Early, no necrosis, adjacent to pancreas
• No intervention unless symptomatic
2) Sterile/Infected Necrosis
• Sterile: no drainage
• Infected: percutaneous drainage → necrosectomy if worsens
3) Pancreatic Abscess
• Percutaneous drainage + antibiotics
4) Pancreatic Ascites
• Enzyme-rich fluid from duct disruption
• Paracentesis: high amylase
• Drainage + octreotide + ERCP stent
5) Hemorrhage
• Pseudocyst bleeding, pseudoaneurysm
• CT angiography → embolization or surgery
