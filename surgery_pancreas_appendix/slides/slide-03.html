Pancreas — Surgical Anatomy

Gross Anatomy
• Situated in the retroperitoneum
• Divided into: head (30%) and body/tail (70%)
Head:
• Lies within the curve of the duodenum
• Overlies L2 vertebra and vena cava
• Uncinate process: passes behind SMV
Neck:
• Aorta and SMA lie behind
• SMV + splenic vein → portal vein behind neck
Tail:
• Extends to splenic hilum
Functions
Endocrine: Insulin, glucagon (metabolism regulation)
Exocrine: Digestive enzymes
Key Relations
Anterior:
• Transverse colon, stomach
Posterior:
• IVC, aorta, SMA, left kidney, left adrenal
Superior:
• Hepatic artery, celiac trunk
Inferior:
• Duodenum, jejunum
Blood Supply
• Celiac trunk → pancreaticoduodenal arteries
• SMA → inferior pancreaticoduodenal
• Splenic artery → pancreatic body/tail
Lymphatic Drainage
• Coeliac, superior mesenteric nodes
• Pyloric, splenic, para-aortic nodes
