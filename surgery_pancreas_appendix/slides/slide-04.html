Acute Pancreatitis — Overview

Pathogenesis
• Chemical inflammation (NOT infection)
• Auto-digestion by endogenous trypsin activation
• Causes: tissue necrosis, fat necrosis, vessel erosion
Types:
1) Acute interstitial pancreatitis
2) Acute necrotizing pancreatitis
Causes (GET SMASHED)
Gallstones (most common in Egypt, F>M)
Ethanol (common in West, M>F, age 30-40)
Trauma (blunt/penetrating)
Steroids
MM
Autoimmune
Scorpion sting (endemic)
Hyperlipidemia (TG >1000), Hypercalcemia
ERCP (1% clinical pancreatitis)
Drugs: fibrate, carbimazole, frusemide, statins
Clinical Features
• Acute epigastric pain radiating to back
• Worse in supine position, dull character
• Jaundice (cholestatic)
• Nausea and vomiting
Severe Necrotizing (10-20%):
• Persistent SIRS (>48 hours)
• Organ failure:
— SBP <90 mmHg
— RR >24
— Creatinine >2 mg/dl
— Pulse >90
— PaO2 <60 mmHg
— WBC >12,000
Ranson's Criteria:
• 0-2: minimal mortality, regular ward
• 3-5: 10-20% mortality, ICU admission
• >5 after 48h: >50% mortality
