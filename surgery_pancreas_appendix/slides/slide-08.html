Chronic Pancreatitis

Definition
• Fibrosis, duct distortion, irreversible damage
• Advanced: calcification, dilated ducts, DM, maldigestion
Causes
1) Alcoholic (60-70%)
2) Tropical pancreatitis
3) Metabolic (Hypertriglyceridemia, Hypercalcemia)
4) Idiopathic
5) Obstruction (stricture or stone)
6) Scleroderma, CRF, PSC, PBC
Clinical Features
• Abdominal pain (postprandial)
• Nausea, vomiting, weight loss
• Malabsorption (fat mainly)
• DM (late, risk of hypoglycemia)
• Pancreatic pseudocyst
Complications
• Pseudocyst, ductal/duodenal obstruction
• Ascites, pleural effusion
• Splenic vein thrombosis, pancreatic cancer
Investigations
• Amylase/lipase: low or normal
• Fecal elastase-1: <100 mcg/g
• Fecal fat, US/EUS, MRCP
• Secretin test, plain X-ray (calcification)
• CT, ERCP, mixed triglyceride breath test
DD
• Causes of abdominal pain
• Peptic ulcer, IBD, IBS
• Causes of malabsorption
• Celiac disease, CHO malabsorption
Treatment
• No specific treatment
• Lifestyle: small low-fat meals, stop alcohol/smoking
• PPI, pancreatic enzyme replacement, octreotide
• Insulin for DM
• ERCP (sphincterotomy, stone extraction)
• Analgesics
