Acute Pancreatitis — Dx & Tx

Investigations
Laboratory
• Serum amylase (3× normal)
• Serum lipase (more specific)
• Isoamylase level analysis
• AST (ALT in complicated cases)
Imaging
• US abdomen
• EUS (better than MRCP)
• Multislice CT abdomen
• MRCP
DD (Acute Epigastric Pain)
Abdominal: Perforated viscus, PUD, acute cholecystitis, vascular occlusion
Chest: Inferior MI, basal pleurisy, pericarditis
Systemic: DKA, FMF, AIP
Treatment
1) Inhibition of pancreatic secretion
• NPO, PPI, somatostatin
2) Supportive Care
• Fluid replacement (hematocrit/12h)
• Analgesics
• Antibiotics
3) Complications
• Infected necrosis: endoscopic transgastric approach
• Open necrosectomy (obsolete)
Key Point:
No role for surgery during initial resuscitation
Surgery only for local complications after stabilization
