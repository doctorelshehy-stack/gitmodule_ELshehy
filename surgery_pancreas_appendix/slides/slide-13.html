Appendicitis — Clinical Features

Symptoms
Classic Migratory Pain:
1. Periumbilical (visceral)
2. Then → RLQ (parietal peritoneum)
• Anorexia (nearly constant)
• Nausea, vomiting
• Constipation (or diarrhea in pelvic)
Signs
General:
• Low-grade fever (<38°C)
• High fever = complications
Abdomen:
• Diminished bowel sounds
• McBurney's point tenderness (1/3 ASIS-umbilicus)
• Voluntary/involuntary guarding
• Rebound tenderness
Special Signs:
• Dunphy's sign (pain on coughing)
• Rovsing's sign (RLQ pain on LLQ palpation)
• Obturator sign (pain on internal hip rotation)
• Iliopsoas sign (pain on right hip extension)
Atypical Presentations
Pelvic appendicitis:
• Tenesmus, dysuria
• Tenderness on pelvic exam
Retrocecal:
• Minimal abdominal tenderness
• Pain in right loin
Subhepatic:
• Mimics acute cholecystitis
Special Populations
Children:
• Higher perforation rate
• Greater omentum not developed
• Often misdiagnosed as gastroenteritis
Elderly:
• Little tenderness/rigidity
• High perforation rate
Pregnancy:
• Pain displaced upward
• Less efficient omental localization
• Delay → 50% abortion/premature labor
