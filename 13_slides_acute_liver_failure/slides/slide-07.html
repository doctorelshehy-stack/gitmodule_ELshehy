Complications — Renal & Neurological

Renal

  • Acute kidney injury is common and associated with increased mortality
  • Causes are multifactorial:
    • Acute tubular necrosis
    • Drug toxicity (nephrotoxic drugs)
  • Management: stop nephrotoxic drugs, optimise haemodynamic status

Key Warning

Strategies for HE in chronic liver disease (non-absorbable antibiotics, lactulose) are inappropriate in ALF.

Neurological

  • Severe HE is a poor prognostic indicator
  • Associated with risk of cerebral oedema (CO) and intracranial hypertension
  • HE results from cerebral effects of high circulating neurotoxins and systemic inflammatory response

Neurological Management

  • Prevention of infection
  • Control of circulating ammonia concentration
  • Maintenance of stable cerebral perfusion & metabolism
  • Achieved with: sedation, avoidance of fever, RRT
