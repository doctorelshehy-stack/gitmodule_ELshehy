Management — Initial & Specific Therapies

Initial Measures

  • Recognise early — care in critical care setting
  • Early contact with specialist liver centre
  • Identify & treat precipitants
  • Optimise conditions for liver regeneration
  • Prevent sepsis and other complications
  • Continuous prognostic assessment for early LT waitlisting

Specific Therapies

  • Paracetamol-induced: N-acetylcysteine (NAC) — efficacy related to interval between ingestion and therapy
  • Non-paracetamol ALF: NAC also beneficial — improves survival in low-grade encephalopathy
  • NAC continued until synthetic function improves or transplant performed
  • Viral cause: specific antivirals (e.g., ganciclovir for CMV)
  • Autoimmune hepatitis: IV corticosteroids — but increases sepsis risk, may preclude LT
