Transplantation & King's College Criteria

Liver Transplantation

  • Early identification of patients unlikely to survive with medical therapy alone
  • Multiple prognostic evaluation systems developed
  • King's College Criteria — best characterised, basis of UK super-urgent listing
  • Waitlisting schemes prioritise ALF patients
  • Majority listed are transplanted

King's College Criteria (Selection for LT)

For Paracetamol-induced ALF:

  • Arterial pH <7.3 (after resuscitation)
  • OR all three of: INR >6.5, creatinine >300 µmol/L, Grade III-IV HE

For Non-paracetamol ALF:

  • INR >6.5 (prothrombin time >100 sec)
  • OR any three of:
    • Age <10 or >40 years
    • Etiology: non-A, non-B hepatitis, idiosyncratic drug reaction
    • Jaundice to encephalopathy >7 days
    • Bilirubin >300 µmol/L
    • Peak PT/prothrombin >100 sec or INR >6.5
