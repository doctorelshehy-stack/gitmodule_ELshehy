Constipation - History & Examination

📝 History Taking
  • Many patients have misconceptions regarding normal stool patterns
  • Frequency of bowel movements and consistency must be accurately determined
  • Ask about pain with defecation, abdominal distention, gas, nausea, emesis
  • Dietary history: type and quantities of liquid, fruits, vegetables, fiber
  • Review of systems: weight loss, fatigue, depression, anxiety
  • Past medical history
📄 Rome VI Diagnostic Criteria
  • Must include 2 or more: Straining, Lumpy/hard stools, Incomplete evacuation, Anorectal obstruction, Manual maneuvers, Fewer than 3 defecations/week
  • Loose stools rarely present without laxatives
  • Insufficient criteria for IBS
👀 Physical Examination
  • General assessment: nutritional status, weight, vital signs, thyroid gland, skin
  • Abdominal examination: bowel sounds, distention, masses, focal tenderness
  • Rectal examination: stool consistency, occult blood, rule out fissure, ulcers, masses, hemorrhoids
  • Neurologic examination: signs of dementia, Parkinson disease, neuropathy
🔬 Investigation
  1. CBC, serum electrolytes, TSH, calcium level
  2. Anoscopy: internal hemorrhoids and fissures
  3. Flexible sigmoidoscopy or barium enema
  4. Full colonoscopy with biopsies to rule out amyloidosis
  5. X-rays
