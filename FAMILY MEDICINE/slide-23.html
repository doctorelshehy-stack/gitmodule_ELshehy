Functional Dyspepsia - History & Examination

📝 Clinical Approach - History
  • Hx of NSAIDs ingestion or history of PUD
  • Classic PUD symptoms: epigastric pain improved with food, pain developing hours after eating
  • Nocturnal symptoms common with PUD
  • Pain radiating upward into lower chest may be atypical IHD
  • Decreased appetite, weight loss
  • Spicy foods and citrus beverages aggravate symptoms
  • Bleeding from PUD: dark stool/melena, vomiting altered blood
👀 Examination
  • Epigastric tenderness
  • Stool occult blood testing
  • Signs of anemia
📈 DD of Dyspepsia

Common:

  • GERD, Functional dyspepsia, PU, Aspirin/NSAID use

Less Common:

  • Alcohol consumption, Biliary colic, Celiac disease, Malignancy, Intestinal ischemia, Lactose intolerance, Gastroparesis, GIT inflammation, Pancreatitis, Pregnancy
🔬 Investigation
  • Stool studies: occult blood
  • CBC, Liver enzymes, amylase, lipase
  • Stool antigen or serum IgA ELISA for H. pylori
  • ECG
  • US for gallstones
  • Upper GI endoscopy (>55 years: exclude malignancy)
