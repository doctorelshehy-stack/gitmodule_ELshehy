Functional Dyspepsia - Management

💚 Non-Pharmacological
  • Reassurance and lifestyle modification
  • Avoid NSAIDs and tobacco
  • Avoid spicy food
  • Avoid fat, coffee, alcohol and cigarette smoking
💉 Pharmacological
  • No evidence of active infection: Ranitidine 400 mg twice before meals for 4-6 weeks
  • Dysmotility-related symptoms (nausea, bloating, early satiety): Metoclopramide
  • Positive for H. pylori: Triple therapy for 14 days
⚠ Triple Therapy (14 days)
  1. Amoxicillin 1 g twice daily
  2. Clarithromycin 500 mg twice daily
  3. Omeprazole 20 mg twice daily

Add Metronidazole 500 mg bid for quadrant therapy

Note: Omeprazole contraindicated in breast feeding

📚 Patient Education
  • Educated about dyspepsia and relationship to H. pylori
  • Instructed to take entire course of medication
  • Advised to return if any alarm sign develops
  • Follow-up visit almost 4 weeks after initial visit
⚠
Symptoms of Complications
Occult blood in stool without anemia • Coffee grounds-like emesis • Hematemesis • Melena • Sudden vascular collapse with shock
⚠ Alarm Features
  • Dysphagia, Blood in stools, Abdominal mass
  • Anorexia, Dyspepsia related lymphadenopathy
  • Weight loss, Family history of GIT malignancy
  • Hematemesis or melena, Anemia
DD: GERD — Patients with GERD report heartburn and acid regurgitation. Dyspepsia patients do not have heartburn/regurgitation. Epigastric pain in dyspepsia relieved by meal; pain in GERD occurs after meals.
