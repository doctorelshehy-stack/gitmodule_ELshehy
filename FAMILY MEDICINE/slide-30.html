Weight Loss - Treatment & Elderly Considerations

💉 Treatment
  • Patients with obvious cause (infection, DM, cancer): treat accordingly
  • Patients with dementia: caregivers provide feeding support + nutritional supplements
  • Patients with anorexia nervosa: psychiatric and very close follow-up
  • Patients with no explanation: observation + frequent follow-up
⚠ Weight Loss in Elderly
  • Very common: 15-20% of elderly patients
  • Older patients at greater risk than younger
  • Olfactory function declines with age
  • Dental problems and dry mouth cause decreased interest in food
  • Elderly who are alone less likely to eat adequate calories
  • Most common causes: malignancy, GI disorders, psychiatric conditions
📊 Organ-Specific Causes in Elderly
  • Cardiovascular: Cardiac cachexia (distinct from intentional weight loss from diuretics)
  • Respiratory: Cachexia due to increased energy expenditure from work of breathing
  • GI: Dental disease → decreased caloric intake
  • Renal: Advanced renal failure → protein energy malnutrition
  • Malignancy: Weight loss common with solid tumors (GI, pancreatic, lung)
  • Endocrinopathies: Hyperthyroidism (40-60%)
📅 Appropriate Follow-up
  • Assess response to management
  • Selected cancer screening tests
  • Radiographic contrast studies vs endoscopy
