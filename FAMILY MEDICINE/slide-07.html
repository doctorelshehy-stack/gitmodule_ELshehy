Pre-hospital/Emergency Department Care

⚠ Immediate Actions
  • Nothing Oral
  • Apply oxygen as appropriate
  • Intravenous (IV) fluids: set up immediately if shocked
  • Send blood for group and save/cross match
  • Nasogastric (NG) tube if severe vomiting or intestinal obstruction
  • Analgesia
  • Antiemetic: avoid using without considering diagnosis in community setting
⚠ Antibiotics & Admission
  • Antibiotics if systemic sepsis, peritonitis, or severe UTI suspected
  • IV cephalosporin plus metronidazole commonly used
  • Arrange urgent surgical/gynaecological review
  • Arrange investigations such as ECG if medical cause likely
  • Admit if surgery likely, unable to tolerate oral fluids, for pain control, or if IV antibiotics required
