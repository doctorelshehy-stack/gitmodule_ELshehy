Physical Examination

👀 General Assessment
  • Pulse, temp, BP & RR
  • Peritonitis: shallow, rapid breaths
  • Altered consciousness: check Glasgow Coma Scale
👁 Inspection
  • Anemia, jaundice, abdominal distention, dehydration
  • Cullen's sign (umbilicus) - hemorrhagic pancreatitis, ectopic pregnancy
  • Grey Turner's sign (flanks) - retroperitoneal hematoma
🔊 Auscultation
  • Absent bowel sounds: paralytic ileus, peritonitis
  • High-pitched/tinkling: subacute obstruction
  • Listen for abdominal and iliac bruits (AAA)
👆 Percussion
  • Assess swelling/distension
  • Tenderness to percussion: generalized peritonitis
  • Assess for shifting dullness and fluid thrill
💉 Palpation
  • Gentle then deeper, starting away from pain
  • Feel for masses, tenderness, guarding, organomegaly
  • Test for rebound tenderness
  • Examine groins for hernia, scrotum in men
  • Check supraclavicular and groin lymph nodes
💊 Further Examination
  • Rectal/pelvic examination as needed
  • Lower limb pulses (for AAA)
  • Pregnancy test in women of childbearing age
