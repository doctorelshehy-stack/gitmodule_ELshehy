Constipation - Treatment

💚 Non-Pharmacological
  1. Reassurance
  2. Increase fluid and fiber intake
  3. Drink at least 8 glasses of water, consume fresh fruits, vegetables, whole grains
  4. Stop drug causing constipation
  5. Bowel retraining: spend 10-15 quiet minutes daily on commode after meals (2-3 weeks to become effective)
⚠
Complications in Elderly
Fecal impaction, Stercoral ulcers, Anal fissures, Megacolon, Bacterial overgrowth, Volvulus
💉 Pharmacological
Bulk-forming agents
Psyllium, methylcellulose, polycarbophil — MOA: High in fiber, increase stool volume by absorbing water. AE: bloating, flatulence
Osmotic laxatives
Lactulose, magnesium salts, sorbitol — MOA: nonabsorbable solutes drawing fluid into lumen. AE: bloating, flatulence, cramping. Magnesium salts contraindicated in renal failure
Stimulant agents
Phenolphthalein, bisacodyl — MOA: altering mucosal permeability, stimulating smooth muscle. Chronic abuse leads to melanosis coli
Stool softeners
Docusate sodium — MOA: decreases surface tension, allows water and fat to mix
Enemas and suppositories
MOA: distending and stimulating rectum for evacuation. Useful in bedridden patients and stool impaction
Surgery (last option): hemicolectomy with ileorectal anastomosis for severe idiopathic constipation
