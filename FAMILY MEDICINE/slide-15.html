CRC Screening

🔍 Screening Methods
  • Proctoscopy
  • Flexible sigmoidoscopy
  • Colonoscopy gold standard
  • Barium enema
  • CT colonography
  • Liver US and CT or MRI for metastases
  • CEA (Carcinoembryonic Antigen)
📋 Referral Criteria
  1. 2 main symptoms: rectal bleeding + change in bowel habit
  2. 2 secondary effects: iron deficiency anemia + intestinal obstruction
  3. 2 physical findings: abdominal or rectal masses
⚠ When to Refer
  • Aged 40+: change in bowel habit with rectal bleeding for 6+ weeks
  • Aged 60+: change in bowel habit without rectal bleeding for 6+ weeks
  • Aged 60+: rectal bleeding without change in bowel habit for 6+ weeks
  • Any age with right lower abdominal mass
  • Any age with palpable rectal mass
  • Men: unexplained iron-deficiency anaemia, Hb ≤ 11 g/100 ml
  • Non-menstruating women: unexplained iron-deficiency anemia, Hb ≤ 10 g/100 ml
📄 USPSTF 2020 Screening
  • Adults aged 50-75: Grade A
  • Adults aged 45-49: Grade B
  • Adults aged 76-85: Grade C
