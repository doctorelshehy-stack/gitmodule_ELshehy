Weight Loss - Evaluation

📝 Detailed History Taking
  1. Determine amount and duration of weight loss
  2. Review of different body systems
  3. Questions about daily food intake, appetite changes, pain with swallowing, vomiting, change in bowel habits
  4. Questions to assess psychological state
  5. Past history
  6. Social history
👀 Complete Physical Examination
  • Measuring weight and height + vital signs
  • General examination
  • Examination of different body systems
🔬 Laboratory Investigations
  • CBC/diff, MCV, chemistries (electrolytes, glucose, calcium, phosphorus)
  • BUN, creatinine, AST, ALT, Alk Phos, bilirubin, ESR/CRP, UA
  • Chest radiograph
  • Stool Hemoccult
📌 Additional Considerations
  • Iron studies, B12, folate, serum protein electrophoresis, zinc
  • Endomysial antibody, fat-soluble vitamins, HIV
  • Stool: Fat collection, leukocytes
  • Pap smear, mammography, PSA
🔍 Gastroenterologic Evaluation
  • Radiographic contrast studies vs upper and lower endoscopy
  • Clinical situation dictates testing (diarrhea or anemia → endoscopy + biopsies)
  • Cancer screening: mammogram, pap smear, PSA, occult blood
