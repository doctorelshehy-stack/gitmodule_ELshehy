Colorectal Cancer - Incidence & Risk Factors

📈 Incidence
  • Third leading cancer for both men and women
  • Approximately 6% and 5-year survival rate is 62.1%
  • Incidence increases with age, starting at 40 years and doubling each decade
  • Most frequently diagnosed among persons aged 65-74 years
👦 Sex Distribution
  • Right colon: more common in women
  • Rectum: more common in men
⚠ Risk Factors
  1. Family Hx of colorectal neoplasia
  2. Past Hx of colorectal neoplasm
  3. Hx of small bowel cancer, endometrial, breast or ovarian cancer
  4. IBD: ulcerative colitis, Crohn's colitis
  5. Polyposis syndromes
  6. HNPCC (Hereditary non-polyposis colorectal cancer)
  7. Hormonal factors: nulliparity, late age at first pregnancy, early menopause
  8. Diet: rich in meat and fat; poor in fiber, folate and calcium
  9. DM
  10. Sedentary lifestyle, obesity, smoking, high alcohol intake
  11. Irradiation
