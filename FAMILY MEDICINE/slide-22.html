Functional Dyspepsia - Definition & Categories

📝 Definition
  • 2nd most common functional GIT disorder after IBS
  • Chronic or recurrent pain or discomfort centered in the upper abdomen
  • May be associated with nausea, bloating, early satiety, and/or weight loss
  • According to Rome VI criteria
📈 Mechanisms in Functional Dyspepsia
  • Visceral hypersensitivity
  • Motility disorders
  • H. pylori infection
  • Stress
  • Genetic predisposition
📌 Categories of Dyspepsia
  1. Dyspepsia with identified cause: chronic peptic ulcer disease, reflux esophagitis, malignancy
  2. Dyspepsia with identified abnormality of uncertain significance: H. pylori gastritis, duodenitis
  3. Dyspepsia with no explanation identified (functional)
📄 Rome VI Diagnostic Criteria
  • At least 3 months, onset at least 6 months previously
  • One or more: Bothersome postprandial fullness, Early satiation, Epigastric pain, Epigastric burning
  • No evidence of structural disease likely to explain symptoms
