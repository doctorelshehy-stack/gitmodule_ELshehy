HE — Managing Hospitalized Patients

Stepwise approach for managing patients hospitalized with acute HE:

1. Airway, Breathing, Circulation

Intubate for airway protection if Glasgow Coma Scale is low or patient cannot protect airway. Ensure adequate oxygenation and hemodynamic stability.

2. Evaluate & Treat Precipitants

Evaluate for infection, bleeding or renal failure and treat appropriately. Obtain blood cultures, urinalysis, chest X-ray. Check for GI bleeding — consider upper endoscopy.

3. Correct Electrolyte Abnormalities

Correct hyponatremia, hypokalemia, and metabolic alkalosis. Consider empiric antibiotics if infection suspected while awaiting culture results.

4. Neuroimaging

CT head to rule out intracranial bleeding in patients with focal neurological signs, headache, or history of falls.

5. Lumbar Puncture (Selective)

Lumbar puncture is a risky procedure in coagulopathic patients (prolonged PT/INR, thrombocytopenia). Should be done only if clinical suspicion for meningitis is strong and after correcting coagulopathy with FFP/platelets.

6. Lactulose Administration

Place NG tube and administer lactulose 30 cm³ every 1-2 hours until patient has a bowel movement. Then adjust to maintain 2-3 soft bowel movements per day. Lactulose enemas can be used in patients who are actively bleeding or have a history of large varices.

7. Agitation Management

Haloperidol (0.5-2 mg) can be used to calm an agitated patient. Avoid benzodiazepines as they can worsen HE.

8. Monitor & Escalate

Monitor closely for hypernatremia developing due to excessive lactulose use. Add rifaximin 550 mg PO BID if patient fails to improve on lactulose alone.
