HPS — Investigations

Initial Screening

  • Pulse Oximetry (SaO₂): Measure arterial oxygen saturation on room air. A value <96% suggests HPS in the absence of cardiopulmonary disease in cirrhotic patients.
  • Arterial Blood Gas (ABG): On room air, demonstrates hypoxemia (typically PaO₂ <80 mmHg) and enables calculation of the alveolar–arterial (A-a) oxygen gradient.
  • A-a Gradient: Should be elevated in HPS (usually >20 mmHg) due to ventilation-perfusion mismatch, diffusion-perfusion defect, and intrapulmonary shunting.

Severity Classification

Severe disease in this context can be described as:

  • Room air arterial oxygen tension (PaO₂) <60 mmHg
  • A-a gradient >20 mmHg
  • Shunt fraction >20%

Mild HPS: PaO₂ ≥80 mmHg, A-a gradient ≥15 mmHg

Moderate HPS: PaO₂ 60-79 mmHg

Severe HPS: PaO₂ <60 mmHg

Diagnostic Criteria for HPS:

  • Presence of liver disease (usually cirrhosis with portal hypertension)
  • Increased A-a gradient (≥15 mmHg) on room air
  • Evidence of intrapulmonary vasodilation (IPVD) on contrast echocardiography
  • PaO₂ <80 mmHg (or <70 mmHg for diagnosis in some guidelines)
  • Exclusion of other causes of hypoxemia (COPD, pulmonary fibrosis, heart failure)
