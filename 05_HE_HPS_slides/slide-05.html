HE — Disease Classification

Classification by Type of Liver Dysfunction:

  • Type A — Acute liver failure: develops rapidly in patients with no prior history of liver disease who develop massive hepatic necrosis.
  • Type B — Porto-systemic bypass: occurs in patients with porto-systemic shunts and no intrinsic hepatocellular disease (e.g., congenital porto-systemic shunt, TIPS).
  • Type C — Cirrhosis and portal hypertension: the most common form, occurring in patients with chronic liver disease and cirrhosis.

Severity Grading (West Haven Criteria):

Grade 0 — Minimal HE: no clinical symptoms, subtle cognitive deficits on psychometric testing

Grade 1 — Mild: trivial lack of awareness, euphoria or anxiety, shortened attention span, impaired addition/subtraction

Grade 2 — Moderate: lethargy or apathy, minimal disorientation, inappropriate behavior, slurred speech, asterixis

Grade 3 — Severe: somnolence to semi-stupor, gross disorientation, confusion, incomprehensible speech, hyperreflexia

Grade 4 — Coma: unconscious, no response to verbal or painful stimuli, decerebrate or decorticate posturing
