HE — Complete Treatment Summary

Category Management
General Treatment
  • Adequate caloric intake: 35-45 kcal/kg/day
  • Protein intake: 1-1.5 g protein/kg of bodyweight
  • Vegetable protein may be superior to animal protein (better tolerance, less ammonia production)
  • High fiber diet encouraged to promote regular bowel movements
  • Lactulose 15-30 cm³ q4-6 hours to ensure 2-3 bowel movements/day
  • Rifaximin 550 mg PO BID (for refractory or recurrent HE)
Treatment of Cause
  • Infection: Start appropriate antibiotics based on suspected source and culture results
  • GI Bleeding due to esophageal varices: Band ligation or injection sclerotherapy
  • Constipation: Increase lactulose dose, consider enemas
  • Electrolyte imbalance: Correct hyponatremia, hypokalemia cautiously
  • Renal failure: Identify and treat prerenal causes, adjust medications
Treatment of Complications
  • Surgical: Embolization of large portosystemic collaterals in patients with spontaneous chronic incapacitating encephalopathy
  • Liver transplantation: Definitive treatment for refractory HE in suitable candidates. HE improves or resolves in most patients after transplant.
  • TIPS reduction/occlusion: If HE is due to TIPS and refractory to medical therapy

Nutritional Note: Historically, protein restriction was recommended for HE, but current evidence supports maintaining adequate protein intake (1-1.5 g/kg/day) to prevent catabolism and muscle wasting. Muscle tissue is a major site of ammonia clearance via glutamine synthetase. Malnutrition worsens HE outcomes.
