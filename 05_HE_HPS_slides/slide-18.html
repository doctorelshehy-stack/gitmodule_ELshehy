HPS — Definition & Etiology

Definition: Hepatopulmonary Syndrome (HPS) is defined by an increased alveolar–arterial (A-a) gradient on room air with evidence of intrapulmonary vasodilations (IPVDs) occurring in patients with liver disease and in the absence of intrinsic lung disease.

There is no correlation between the degree of arterial hypoxemia or amount of shunting and the severity of liver disease as measured by the Child–Pugh score.

Conditions Associated with HPS:

  • Cirrhosis (any etiology) — most common association
  • Noncirrhotic portal hypertension — HPS can occur without cirrhosis
  • Ischemic hepatitis — with reversal of intrapulmonary vasodilation upon correction of the underlying ischemia, suggesting a dynamic vasoregulatory mechanism
  • Acute liver failure — rarely reported

Pathophysiology: Portal hypertension and liver dysfunction lead to release of pulmonary vasodilators (NO, CO, VEGF) causing dilation of pulmonary precapillary and capillary vessels, resulting in ventilation-perfusion mismatch, diffusion impairment, and right-to-left shunting.
