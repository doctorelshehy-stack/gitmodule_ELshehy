HE — Prevention Strategies

Primary Prevention

Preventing the first episode of HE:

  • Pre-emptive use of lactulose after TIPS: One third of patients may develop HE after transjugular intrahepatic portosystemic shunt (TIPS) placement. Prophylactic lactulose is recommended.
  • Avoidance of constipation: Regular bowel movements (2-3 per day) to reduce ammonia absorption from the colon.
  • Avoid psychoactive drugs: Benzodiazepines, opioids, and sedatives should be avoided or used with extreme caution.
  • Avoid excessive diuretics: Use the minimum effective dose to manage ascites.
  • Prophylaxis against variceal bleeding: Beta-blockers or band ligation to prevent GI bleeding.
  • Prophylaxis against SBP: Antibiotic prophylaxis in high-risk cirrhotic patients.

Secondary Prevention

Preventing recurrence after initial episode:

  • Compliance with lactulose: Ensure 2-3 bowel movements per day. Lactulose is the cornerstone of secondary prevention.
  • Avoidance of sedatives and hypnotics: In hospitalized patients, avoid these unless absolutely necessary for procedures.
  • Avoid dehydration: Overzealous use of diuretics or lactulose-induced diarrhea can lead to dehydration and prerenal azotemia.
  • Dietary protein management: Avoid high-protein loads while maintaining adequate nutrition (1-1.5 g/kg/day of protein).
  • Consider rifaximin: For patients with recurrent HE despite optimal lactulose therapy, add rifaximin 550 mg twice daily.

Regular outpatient follow-up, medication adherence counseling, and dietary education are essential components of HE prevention.
