HE — Type C Subclassification

Type C HE is subclassified based on duration and neurological manifestations:

(a) Episodic HE

Episodes develop over a short period and fluctuate in severity.

  • Precipitated: triggered by events such as GI hemorrhage, uremia, infection, electrolyte disorder
  • Spontaneous: no recognized precipitating factors identified
  • Recurrent: two or more episodes of episodic HE within 1 year

(b) Persistent HE

Persistent cognitive deficits that negatively impact social and occupational functioning.

  • Mild: HE grade 1 symptoms persist
  • Severe: HE grades 2–4 symptoms persist
  • Chronic progressive course with continuous neuropsychiatric manifestations

(c) Minimal HE (MHE)

Patients have no recognizable clinical symptoms of HE.

  • Mild cognitive and psychomotor deficits present
  • Manifested by impairment in specialized testing of cognitive functioning (psychometric tests)
  • Impairs quality of life and increases risk of progression to overt HE
  • Affects ability to drive and perform complex tasks
