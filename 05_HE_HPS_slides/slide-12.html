HE — Differential Diagnosis

The diagnosis of HE requires exclusion of other conditions that can cause similar neuropsychiatric symptoms. Key differential diagnoses with distinguishing features:

Differential Diagnosis Distinguishing Features & Workup
Metabolic Encephalopathies
Hypoxia, hyponatremia, azotemia, diabetic coma		 Blood gas analysis, clinical chemistry panel, pulmonary evaluation, urinalysis based on indication. Intracranial disorders should be ruled out.
Intracranial Pathology
Tumors, hemorrhage, hematoma, meningitis, seizure disorder, dementia		 Neurological imaging (CT/MRI), lumbar puncture, EEG based on clinical suspicion. Rapid plasma reagin (RPR), vitamin B12 serum level to exclude neurosyphilis and B12 deficiency.
Toxins & Drugs
Alcohol, drugs, hypnotics, tranquilizers, analgesics, heavy metals		 Urine and blood toxin screen, blood alcohol level. Review all medications including over-the-counter and herbal supplements. Consider heavy metal screening in refractory cases.

Key Diagnostic Principle: HE is a diagnosis of exclusion. In any patient with liver disease presenting with altered mental status, intracranial hemorrhage (especially subdural hematoma from falls due to coagulopathy) and meningitis must be ruled out before attributing symptoms to HE.
