HE — Treatment: Lactulose & Rifaximin

Lactulose (First-Line)

Lactulose is a non-absorbable disaccharide and the first-line drug for treatment of HE.

Mechanism of Action:

  • Not absorbed in the small intestine; reaches colon intact
  • Metabolized by colonic bacteria to lactic acid and acetic acid
  • Acidifies the colonic lumen (pH reduction)
  • Converts NH3 (absorbable) to NH4+ (non-absorbable) — "ammonia trap"
  • Osmotic cathartic effect — accelerates intestinal transit, reducing ammonia absorption time

Side Effects:

  • Abdominal cramping and bloating
  • Diarrhea (can lead to dehydration and electrolyte disturbances)
  • Flatulence and abdominal discomfort
  • Hypernatremia (due to excessive water loss from diarrhea)

Rifaximin (Second-Line)

Rifaximin is the second-line treatment for HE and has been approved for treatment of refractory encephalopathy.

Mechanism of Action:

  • Broad spectrum antibacterial activity against aerobic and anaerobic Gram-positive and Gram-negative organisms
  • Minimal systemic absorption (<0.4%) — acts locally in the gut
  • Reduces ammonia-producing bacteria in the colon
  • Alters gut microbiome composition favorably
  • Reduces bacterial translocation and inflammation

Dosage:

  • 550 mg orally twice daily
  • Can be used as add-on to lactulose for recurrent HE
  • Generally well-tolerated with minimal side effects
