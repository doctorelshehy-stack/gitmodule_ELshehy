Ventral / Umbilical / Incisional Hernias

Umbilical Hernia
Congenital (exomphalos):
• Minor: <5cm, sac present
• Major: >5cm, no sac, liver inside
• Urgent surgery
Infantile:
• Close spontaneously by age 2
• Repair if persists after age 5
Adult (Paraumbilical):
• F>M, ↑IAP (pregnancy, obesity, ascites)
• Painless swelling above umbilicus
• Narrow neck → adhesions → complications
• Small: primary closure
• >3cm: prosthetic mesh
Epigastric Hernia
• 3-5% of population
• M 2-3× more common
• Between xiphoid and umbilicus (80% off-midline)
• Small defects → pain disproportionate to size
• Multiple in 20%
• Excise fat + close fascial defect
Incisional Hernia
• 15-20% of all abdominal wall hernias
• F:M = 2:1
• Risk factors: obesity, age, malnutrition, ascites, SSI (most common), steroids, chemo
• ↑ Over time → pain, obstruction, strangulation
• Small (<2cm): primary repair
• Larger: prosthetic mesh (↓ recurrence by half)
Special Types
Divarication of Recti:
• Not a true hernia
• Linea alba stretched → bulge at medial rectus margins
• No fascial ring or sac
• Common in middle-aged F (pregnancy, ascites)
Spigelian Hernia:
• Through spigelian fascia (rectus → semilunar line)
• Nearly all at or below arcuate line
• Small (1-2cm), 4th-7th decades
• Interparietal (beneath external oblique)
• Often no bulge — just pain
• US/CT to diagnose
• Repair due to incarceration risk
Hernia Recurrence:
• 1-3% (tension-free) to higher (tissue repair)
• Most within first 2 years
• Technical causes most common
