Diverticulitis — Treatment

Uncomplicated (Stage 0-Ia)
Antibiotics (selective):
• Metronidazole + Ciprofloxacin/Levofloxacin
• Duration: 4-7 days
Supportive:
• Clear liquid diet until improvement
• Analgesics + antiemetics
Follow-up:
• 2-3 days for progression
• If no improvement → inpatient + repeat imaging
• Colonoscopy after resolution (if no recent scope)
Complicated (Stage Ib-IV)
IV antibiotics (routine)
Complete bowel rest (NPO) + IV fluids
Abscess Management:
• <4cm: IV antibiotics trial (48-72h)
— Improving → oral feeds + antibiotics
— Not improving → percutaneous drainage
• ≥4cm: CT/US-guided percutaneous drainage
— If not feasible → laparoscopic/open
Perforation (Stage III-IV):
• Stable → laparoscopic/open colectomy + primary anastomosis ± stoma
• Critically ill → Hartmann procedure
Long-term Management
Elective Colectomy Indications:
• 6-8 weeks after complicated diverticulitis
• High recurrence risk
• Persistent symptoms
• Chronic complications (fistula, strictures)
Complications:
• Early: perforation, abscess, obstruction, bleeding, pylephlebitis
Late:
• Fistulas: colovesical (most common), colovaginal, colocutaneous
— Pneumaturia, fecaluria, recurrent UTIs
— Tx: resection + primary anastomosis
• Recurrence: 13-23% uncomplicated, up to 40% complicated
• Possibly ↑ short-term colon cancer risk
