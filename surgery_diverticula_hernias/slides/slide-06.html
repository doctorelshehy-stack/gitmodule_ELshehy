Meckel Diverticulum — Rule of 2's

Rule of 2's
2% of population
2% are symptomatic
Mostly children < 2 years
Males 2× more than females
2 feet from ileocecal valve
≤ 2 inches long
2 types of mucosal lining
Mucosal Types
I. Native ileal mucosa
II. Ectopic mucosa:
— Gastric (60% — most common)
— Pancreatic
— Colonic
— Duodenal
Ectopic gastric mucosa → acid secretion → ulceration → bleeding
Clinical Features
Asymptomatic: Most common (incidental finding)
Symptomatic:
• Lower GI bleeding (most common feature)
— Hematochezia
— Tarry stools
— Currant jelly stools
• Abdominal pain (RLQ)
• Nausea + vomiting
