Inguinal Hernia — Repair Techniques

Operation Types
a) Herniotomy: Excision of sac only
b) Herniorrhaphy: Herniotomy + repair by tissue approximation
c) Hernioplasty (standard):
• Herniotomy + tension-free repair with mesh
• Materials: fascia lata or polypropylene
Truss:
• Avoided if possible
• Only for extremely unfit patients
• Complications: testicular atrophy, neuritis, incarceration
Anterior Repairs
Tissue Repairs (rare now):
• Iliopubic tract repair
• Shouldice (multilayer continuous suture)
• Bassini (conjoint tendon → inguinal ligament)
• McVay/Cooper's ligament (transversus → Cooper's)
Indication: strangulated hernia + bowel resection (mesh contraindicated)
Tension-Free (Standard):
• Lichtenstein approach
• Plug-and-patch technique
• Sandwich technique
• Tension = principal cause of recurrence
Posterior / Preperitoneal
Open Preperitoneal:
• Useful for: recurrent, sliding, strangulated, femoral
• Self-expanding polypropylene patch
• Preformed mesh covers direct, indirect, femoral spaces
Laparoscopic:
• Tension-free mesh behind defect
• Covers myopectineal orifice
• Natural forces anchor mesh
Two techniques:
• TEP: Totally extraperitoneal
• TAPP: Transabdominal preperitoneal
Recurrence:
• 1-3% with tension-free repair
• Most within first 2 years
• Lower with tension-free vs tissue repair
