Inguinal Hernia — Anatomy

Inguinal Canal
• Oblique canal, 4cm long
• 2-4cm cephalad to inguinal ligament
• Deep ring → Superficial ring
• Contains: spermatic cord (M) / round ligament (F)
Boundaries:
Posterior: fascia transversalis, inferior epigastric vessels (lateral), conjoint tendon (medial)
Anterior: external oblique aponeurosis, internal oblique (lower, lateral)
Superior: conjoint muscles
Inferior: inguinal ligament, lacunar ligament (medial)
Inguinal Rings:
• Deep: opening in transversalis fascia, ½ inch above mid-inguinal point
• Superficial: triangular in external oblique, above & medial to pubic tubercle
Hesselbach's Triangle
Borders:
• Superolateral: inferior epigastric vessels
• Medial: rectus sheath
• Inferior: inguinal ligament
Direct hernia: occurs WITHIN this triangle
Indirect hernia: arises LATERAL to this triangle
Protective Mechanisms:
1. Obliquity of canal
2. Transversalis fascia
3. Deep ring: internal oblique fibers
4. Shutter mechanism (conjoint tendon)
5. Valvular mechanism (external oblique)
6. Cremasteric mechanism (plug)
7. Transversus abdominis contraction
Posterior / Preperitoneal Space
Myopectineal Orifice:
• Lateral: iliopsoas muscle
• Medial: rectus sheath
• Superior: transversus + internal oblique arch
• Inferior: ilio-pectineal line, Cooper's ligament
Inguinal ligament divides:
• Supra-inguinal: spermatic cord / round ligament
• Sub-inguinal: femoral vein, artery, nerve, canal
Key Point:
• Single repair of myopectineal orifice = covers inguinal, direct, AND femoral hernias
• Principle of laparoscopic repair
