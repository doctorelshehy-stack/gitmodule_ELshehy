Esophageal Diverticula

Types & Features
Zenker (Upper — Killian triangle):
• Most common esophageal diverticulum
• False diverticulum (pulsion)
• Killian triangle = weak point between thyropharyngeal and cricopharyngeal muscles
Middle Esophageal:
• At tracheal bifurcation
• Usually traction (true)
Epiphrenic (Lower):
• Above LES, pulsion type
Epidemiology:
• Rare, middle-aged/older males
Clinical Features
• Dysphagia (most common)
• Halitosis
• Retrosternal pressure/pain
• Neck mass (Zenker)
• Regurgitation of undigested food
• Aspiration / coughing after eating
• Weight loss
Diagnosis:
• Barium swallow (best confirmatory)
• Lateral projection best for Zenker
• Endoscopy (rule out malignancy)
• Manometry (motor abnormalities)
Treatment
Medical:
• PPIs for reflux symptoms
• Botulinum toxin (motility disorders)
Surgical (Symptomatic):
• Zenker: Endoscopic diverticulostomy + cricopharyngeal myotomy
• Epiphrenic: Esophagomyotomy
• Open or rigid/flexible endoscopy
Complications:
• Aspiration pneumonia
• Perforation → mediastinitis + fistula
• Esophageal cancer (rare)
