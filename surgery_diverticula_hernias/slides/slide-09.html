Acute Diverticulitis — Classification

Clinical Features
• Low-grade fever
• LLQ pain (sigmoid most common)
• Tender palpable mass (peri-colonic inflammation)
• Change in bowel habits:
— Constipation: 50%
— Diarrhea: 25-35%
• Acute abdomen → perforation
• ↑ Urinary urgency/frequency (15%)
• Hematochezia (rare)
Investigations
Labs:
• CBC: leukocytosis, possible anemia
• BMP: electrolytes, ↑BUN, ↑creatinine
• CRP: ↑
CT Abdomen/Pelvis (preferred):
• Colonic outpouching
• Bowel wall thickening >3mm
• Peridiverticular fat stranding
• Abscess: hypodense collections
• Perforation: pneumoperitoneum
Colonoscopy:
• 6-8 weeks AFTER resolution
• Contraindicated during acute episode
Modified Hinchey Classification
Stage 0: Diverticula ± mural thickening (mild)
Stage Ia: Pericolic phlegmon
Stage Ib: Pericolic abscess
Stage II: Distant abscess
Stage III: Purulent peritonitis
Stage IV: Fecal peritonitis
DD:
• Crohn's, UC, CRC
• Ischemic colitis, appendicitis
• Ectopic pregnancy, ovarian torsion
• Renal colic, UTI
