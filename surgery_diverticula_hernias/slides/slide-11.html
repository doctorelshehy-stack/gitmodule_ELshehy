Abdominal Wall Hernias — Overview

Definition & Etiology
Definition: Abnormal protrusion of organ/tissue through abdominal wall defect (usually in peritoneal sac)
5% of population will develop abdominal wall hernia
Etiology:
• Congenital: Unobliterated processus vaginalis (inguinal), unobliterated umbilical ring
• Acquired:
— High intra-abdominal pressure (constipation, BPH, cough, obesity)
— Collagen diseases (atherosclerosis, varicose veins, flat foot)
— Hormonal (pregnancy — ligament laxity)
— Degenerative (elderly, weak muscles, nerve damage)
Classification
External (Abdominal Wall):
• Inguinal, femoral, umbilical
• Linea alba, semilunar line
• Incisional sites
Internal:
• Protrusion through defect within peritoneal cavity
Interparietal:
• Sac contained within musculoaponeurotic layer
Incidence:
• 75% in inguinal region
• ⅔ indirect, ⅓ direct
• M 25× more likely than F for groin hernias
• F:M = 10:1 for femoral hernias
Components of Hernia
1) Sac: Peritoneal pouch (neck, body, fundus)
2) Contents: Intestine, omentum, or both
— Everything EXCEPT pancreas
3) Covering: Stretched structures over sac
Intestine vs Omentum in Sac:
• Intestine: soft, gurgling on reduction, resonant
• Omentum: doughy, no gurgle, dull
