Summary

Esophageal Diverticula
• Zenker (Killian triangle) most common
• Pulsion = false, Traction = true
• Barium swallow best test
• Endoscopic diverticulostomy + myotomy
Small Bowel Diverticula
• Duodenal: periampullary, acquired
• Jejunal: silent, 15% complications
Meckel Diverticulum
• Rule of 2's, ectopic gastric mucosa (60%)
• Lower GI bleeding most common
• Littré hernia: Meckel in femoral hernia
Colonic Diverticulosis
• Western disease, low-fiber diet
• Sigmoid most common
• Diverticulitis: 4-20% of cases
• Hinchey classification (CT-based)
• Uncomplicated: oral antibiotics
• Abscess ≥4cm: percutaneous drainage
• Perforation: Hartmann procedure
Abdominal Wall Hernias
• 5% of population, 75% inguinal
• Components: sac + contents + covering
• Strangulation: most serious complication
• Femoral: highest strangulation rate (25-30%)
Inguinal Hernia
• Indirect: lateral to epi. vessels (any age)
• Direct: medial to epi. vessels (elderly)
• Tension-free mesh = standard
• Lichtenstein = most common anterior repair
• TEP/TAPP = laparoscopic
Femoral Hernia
• All should be repaired
• Below inguinal ligament, F>M
Ventral Hernias
• Umbilical: infantile closes by age 2
• Incisional: SSI is most common cause
• >3cm defects: mesh repair
• Spigelian: interparietal, pain without bulge
