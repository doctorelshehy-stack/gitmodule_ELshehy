Small Bowel Diverticula

Duodenal Diverticula
• Mostly acquired
• Periampullary region (medial wall)
• May be symptomatic (dyspepsia)
Complications:
• Bleeding
• Infection
• Perforation + peritonitis
Jejunal Diverticula
• Usually silent
• Complications in 15%:
— Abdominal discomfort
— Bleeding (chronic or hematochezia)
— Infection
— Enterolith
— Intestinal obstruction
— Perforation + peritonitis
— Malabsorption
Meckel Diverticulum
Most common congenital GI anomaly
Etiology:
• Incomplete obliteration of omphalomesenteric (vitelline) duct
• Duct normally obliterated by 6-7th week
• Persistence of proximal segment → Meckel
Anatomy:
• TRUE diverticulum (all 3 layers)
• 2 feet proximal to ileocecal valve
• Antimesenteric side of ileum
• ≤2 inches/5 cm long
• Blood supply: vitelline artery
