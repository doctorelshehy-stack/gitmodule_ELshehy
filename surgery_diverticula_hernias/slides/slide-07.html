Meckel — Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis
Imaging:
• CT angiography (vitelline artery, contrast extravasation)
• Mesenteric arteriography
• Tagged RBC scan
New Techniques:
• Double balloon enteroscopy
• Capsule endoscopy
Diagnostic laparoscopy:
• If hemodynamically stable + negative other investigations
Treatment
Asymptomatic (incidental):
• Imaging: no treatment needed
• Children/young adults: resect ALL
• Adults <50: resect if high-risk features
• Adults >50: no treatment needed
Symptomatic/Complicated:
• Initial stabilization
• Surgical resection of ALL symptomatic/complicated
Complications
1. Hemorrhage (most common)
2. Bowel obstruction:
— Intussusception
— Volvulus
— Littré hernia: Meckel incarcerated in femoral hernia
3. Perforation: peritonitis or abscess
4. Meckel diverticulitis: mimics acute appendicitis
5. Neoplasia (rare):
— Benign: leiomyoma (most common)
— Malignant: leiomyosarcoma, carcinoid
