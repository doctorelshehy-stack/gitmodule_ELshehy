Hernia — Diagnosis & Complications

Diagnosis
Is it a hernia?
• Swelling at anatomical site
• Expansible impulse on cough
• Reducible (unless complicated)
• Opaque on transillumination
Which type?
• Groin: inguinal / femoral
• Anterior wall: umbilical, epigastric, Spigelian
• Posterior wall: lumbar triangles
• Pelvic: obturator, sciatic, perineal
What contents? Bowel, omentum, both
Complicated? Inflamed, irreducible, obstructed, strangulated
Complications Overview
1. Inflammation: tender, red skin, edema
2. Hydrocele of sac: fluid collection
3. Irreducibility: adhesions, ↑ content
4. Obstruction: bowel symptoms, soft hernia
5. Strangulation (most serious):
— Interference with blood supply
— Inguinal: 2-4%
— Incisional: 3-5%
— Paraumbilical: 15-20%
— Femoral: 25-30% (highest!)
— Strangulated inguinal: >50% of all strangulated
Strangulation Stages
1. Venous impairment:
• Congestion → distension
• Gas + fluid accumulation
• Hemorrhage in bowel wall
2. Arterial impairment:
• Devitalized intestine exudes contents
• Dark toxic fluid in sac
3. Gangrene:
• Starts at constricting ring
• Affects antimesenteric border
• Perforation
4. Peritonitis:
• Terminal event
• Septic shock + dehydration → death
