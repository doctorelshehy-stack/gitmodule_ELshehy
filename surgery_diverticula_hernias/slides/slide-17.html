Femoral Hernia

Femoral Canal Anatomy
• Most medial compartment of femoral sheath
• ½ inch long, cone-shaped
• Mouth opens upward behind inguinal ligament
• Contains: fat + lymph node of Cloquet
Boundaries:
• Lateral: Femoral vein
• Medial: Iliopubic tract + Cooper's ligament (lacunar ligament)
• Superior: Iliopubic tract
• Inferior: Cooper's ligament
Clinical Features
• Common in F especially after repeated pregnancy
• Mass below medial inguinal ligament, lateral to pubic tubercle
High strangulation rate!
— May be first presentation
— Tense, tender, irreducible, no impulse
All femoral hernias should be repaired
Sac Path:
• Down femoral canal → forward (stretches cribriform fascia) → upward & lateral toward inguinal ligament
• Narrow neck → prone to irreducibility + strangulation
Contents: omentum, bowel, or part of bowel (Richter's hernia)
Treatment & DD
DD:
1. Inguinal hernia
2. Saphena varix
3. Femoral artery aneurysm
4. Psoas abscess
Repair Options:
• Cooper's ligament repair
• Preperitoneal approach
• Laparoscopic approach
Surgical Approaches:
1. Low anterior: incision below inguinal ligament (less common)
2. High approach: as inguinal hernia
3. Preperitoneal: best for complicated + non-complicated
4. Laparoscopic: best if available + expertise
Essential elements:
• Dissect + reduce sac
• Obliterate defect (iliopubic tract → Cooper's ligament or mesh)
