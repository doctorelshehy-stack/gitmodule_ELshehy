SECTION 02 — PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

Pathophysiology of Hepatocellular Jaundice

❖ Hepatocellular Uptake

▸Bilirubin released from the reticuloendothelial system is in unconjugated form (non-soluble).
▸Transported to hepatocytes bound to albumin (accomplishes solubility in blood).
▸The albumin-bilirubin bond is broken, and bilirubin alone enters hepatocytes through carrier-membrane transport.
▸Bound to proteins in the cytosol to decrease efflux of bilirubin back into the plasma.

❖ Conjugation of Bilirubin

▸Unconjugated bilirubin proceeds to the endoplasmic reticulum.
▸Undergoes conjugation to glucuronic acid.
▸Results in the formation of conjugated bilirubin, which is soluble in the bile.
▸This is rendered by the action of UDP glucuronosyl transferase.

Pathophysiology Summary: In hepatocellular jaundice, damaged hepatocytes have impaired ability to take up, conjugate, and excrete bilirubin, leading to mixed conjugated and unconjugated hyperbilirubinemia.
