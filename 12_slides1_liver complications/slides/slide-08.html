Balloon Tamponade, TIPSS & Surgery

Balloon Tamponade

Sengstaken-Blakemore tube:
• Two balloons (gastric + oesophageal)
• 4 lumens (aspirate from stomach & oesophagus)
• Confirm position by auscultation, X-ray
• Stop gastric inflation if pain (risk of rupture)
• Oesophageal balloon: deflate 10 min q3h

TIPSS

Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Stent Shunt:
• Stent between portal vein & hepatic vein
• Via internal jugular vein
• Reduces portal pressure
• Prior patency of portal vein must be determined
• SE: hepatic encephalopathy → shunt diameter
• Survival not improved

Shunt Surgery

Portosystemic shunt:
• Non-selective: diverts portal blood → liver failure + encephalopathy
• Selective (distal splenorenal): better but encephalopathy may supervene
• Survival not prolonged
• Reserved for failed other treatments
• Only for good liver function

Oesophageal Transection: Stapling gun transection. Risk: oesophageal stenosis, usually combined with splenectomy. Used when TIPSS not available and bleeding not controlled by other therapies. Rarely used.
