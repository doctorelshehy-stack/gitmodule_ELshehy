Definition, Causes & Investigations

Definition: Portal venous pressure >7 mmHg (normal 2-5). Clinical features develop >12 mmHg. Invariable complication of cirrhosis.

Causes:

  • Cirrhosis — at least 90% of cases in developed countries
  • Schistosomiasis — most common worldwide (infrequent outside endemic areas)

Investigations:

  • Clinical grounds (often diagnosed clinically)
  • WHVP via transjugular balloon catheter
  • CBC: thrombocytopenia, leucopenia, anemia

Classification by Obstruction Site:

  • Pre-hepatic: Portal vein thrombosis
  • Pre-sinusoidal: Schistosomiasis
  • Sinusoidal: Cirrhosis
  • Post-sinusoidal: Budd-Chiari, cardiac

Imaging:

  • US: splenomegaly, collateral vessels, portal vein patency
  • CT/MRI angiography: extent of portal vein clot, hepatic vein patency
