Pharmacological Reduction & Gastropathy

Pharmacological Portal Pressure Reduction

  • Reduces active bleeding while endoscopy is arranged
  • Terlipressin: over several hours, sufficient to reduce portal pressure without systemic effects

Hepatopulmonary Syndrome

  • Pulmonary hypertension in portal HTN without other cause
  • ESLD → ↑ endothelin → pulmonary vasoconstriction
  • Treatment: prostacyclin analogs, endothelin receptor antagonists, sildenafil
  • Liver transplant often curative

Congestive Gastropathy

  • Long-standing portal HTN → chronic gastric congestion
  • Endoscopy: "snake skin gastropathy" (punctate erythema)
  • May erode → bleeding (repeated minor bleeding → iron-deficiency anaemia)
  • Anaemia prevented by oral iron; repeated transfusions may be needed

Best Initial Treatment

Propranolol 80-160 mg/day — reduces portal pressure. If ineffective → TIPSS procedure.
