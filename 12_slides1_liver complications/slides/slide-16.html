Summary & Key Takeaways

Portal HTN Definition

Portal pressure >7 mmHg. Clinical features at >12 mmHg. Cirrhosis = 90% of cases.

Signs

Splenomegaly, ascites, caput medusae, varices (oesophagus most common), congestive gastropathy.

Variceal Bleeding Management

Primary: β-blockers + EVL. Acute: terlipressin, band ligation, antibiotics. Escalation: TIPSS, shunt surgery.

Hepatic Encephalopathy

Clinical diagnosis (NH3 poor sensitivity). Treat precipitants. Lactulose + Rifaximin.

Hepatorenal Syndrome

Type 1: rapid creatinine ↑ (2 weeks). Type 2: stable (6 months). Vasopressors + albumin. Bridge to Tx.

SBP & Hepatopulmonary

SBP: 20% mortality, cephalosporins + albumin. HPS: platypnea-orthodeoxia, liver transplant definitive.
