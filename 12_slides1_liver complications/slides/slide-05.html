Clinical Features — Varices

Variceal Bleeding

  • Oesophageal varices — most common, within 3-5 cm of oesophagogastric junction
  • Gastric varices
  • Other: Duodenum, small intestine, colon, rectum (rare)

Other Manifestations

  • Congestive gastropathy
  • Hypersplenism → cytopenias
  • Iron deficiency anaemia
  • Hepatic encephalopathy
  • Ascites

Predisposing Factors for Variceal Bleeding

  • Size of varices
  • Endoscopic features: red spots and red stripes
  • High portal pressure
  • Liver/cell failure
  • Drugs: salicylates, NSAIDs (mucosal erosion)

Note: Variceal bleeding is often severe, and recurrent bleeding occurs if preventative treatment is not given.
