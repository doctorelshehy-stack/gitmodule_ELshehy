Signs of Portal Hypertension

Portal venous congestion and collateral vessel formation produce the clinical features.

Splenomegaly

  • Cardinal finding
  • Confirmed clinically or by US
  • Rarely >5 cm below costal margin in adults

Ascites

  • Due to portal HTN + liver failure
  • Shifting dullness, fluid thrill

Caput Medusae

  • Dilated superficial abdominal veins
  • Venous hum on auscultation
  • Cruveilhier-Baumgarten syndrome

Collateral Vessels & Complications:

  • Varices: Oesophagus, stomach, colon, rectum
  • Congestive gastropathy
  • Hepatopulmonary syndrome
  • Hepatorenal syndrome
