Variceal Bleeding — Prevention

Primary Prevention

  • Screen at diagnosis with EGD
  • Nonselective β-blockers: 50% ↓ risk of bleeding & ↓ mortality if med-large varices
  • Propranolol/Nadolol — effective in reducing portal venous pressure
  • Titrate to max tolerated dose
  • EVL: Superior to βB in ↓ risk of 1st bleed but no difference in mortality
  • Repeat q1-2wk until varices gone, follow up EGD at 3 months then q6-12 months

Secondary Prevention

  • Beta-blockers used as secondary measure to prevent recurrent variceal bleeding following banding
  • Combined approach: EVL + β-blockers

Acute Upper GIT Haemorrhage from OV

  • Common manifestation of chronic liver disease
  • Can also occur from peptic ulceration (common in liver disease)
