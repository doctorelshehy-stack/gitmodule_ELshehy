Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP)

  • 20% mortality
  • Risk factors: ascitic total protein <1 g/dL, history of SBP, current GI bleeding
  • Can precipitate encephalopathy
  • Abdominal pain, fever — but often (25%) asymptomatic
  • Perform paracentesis in all hospitalized cirrhotics with ascites

Microbiology:

Gram -ve (E. coli, Klebsiella) > Gram +ve (S. pneumo, enterococcus)

Treatment:

  1. 3rd-gen cephalosporins or amoxicillin/clavulanic × 5 days
  2. If uncomplicated (no encephalopathy or AKI): fluoroquinolones
  3. IV albumin: 1.5 g/kg at diagnosis & 1 g/kg on day 3 → increased survival
  4. If not improving: repeat paracentesis at 48h (expect 25% ↓ in PMNs)

Other Complications:

  • Cirrhotic cardiomyopathy: ↓ inotropic/chronotropic response, systolic/diastolic dysfunction, prolonged QT, ↑ troponin, BNP
  • Infections: Cellulitis in 20% (abdominal wall/lower limb)
  • Endocrine: Diabetes (15-30%), adrenal insufficiency
  • Coagulopathy: No routine FFP/platelets unless DIC
