Toxoplasmosis: Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis

  • Culture of the parasite is difficult and can be done only at specialized laboratories.
  • Serology is the primary method of diagnosis. Results of IgM, IgG, and antibody avidity levels can be combined to help determine when infection may have occurred. (IgM can persist for >1 year.)

Treatment

  • Immunocompetent patients with only lymphadenopathy do not require treatment.
  • In those with severe disease (especially eye involvement) sulfadiazine 2–4 g daily and pyrimethamine 25 mg daily are given for 4 weeks, along with folinic acid.
  • Congenital infection is treated daily for 1 year with oral pyrimethamine sulfadiazine, and folinic acid.
  • The management of pregnant women giving spiramycin either alone or in combination with sulfadiazine (which is the recommended treatment). Infected infants should be treated from birth.
