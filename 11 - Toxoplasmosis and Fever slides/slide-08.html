Fever: Introduction & Definition

Introduction

Normal Body Temperature — 36.6 To 37.2 °C.

Regulated by thermosensitive neurons in the preoptic and anterior hypothalamus.

Thermoregulatory responses:

  • Redirecting blood to or from cutaneous vascular beds
  • Increased or decreased sweating
  • Extra cellular fluid regulation
  • Behavioral responses

Circadian rhythm or diurnal variation results in lower body temperature in early morning.

Definition of Fever

  • Fever is a controlled increase in body temperature above the normal hypothalamic set point.
  • Rectal temperature >38 °C
  • Hyperpyrexia is > 40°C

Temperature Measurement Reference

Skin temperature is 0.4 °C (0.7°F) lower than oral temperature
Rectal or eardrum temperature is 0.4 °C (0.7°F) higher than oral temperature
Oral temperature is the reference and fever is diagnosed when it exceeds (37.8°C).
