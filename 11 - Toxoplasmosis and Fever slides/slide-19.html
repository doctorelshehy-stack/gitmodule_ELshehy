Summary

Toxoplasmosis

  • Caused by T. gondii
  • Transmission via cat feces
  • Lymphadenopathy main symptom
  • Serology is primary diagnosis
  • Tx: sulfadiazine + pyrimethamine

Fever

  • Normal: 36.6–37.2°C
  • Fever: >38°C rectal
  • 4 patterns: intermittent, remittent, continuous, relapsing
  • Dangerous: >41°C
  • Critical: >44°C

Fever of Unknown Origin

  • Fever ≥3 weeks, cause unknown
  • 4 categories
  • Infectious & non-infectious causes
  • 3-level investigation approach
  • Treatment: symptomatic + specific
