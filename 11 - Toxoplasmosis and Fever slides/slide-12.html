Fever: Critical & Maximum Levels

What is the emergency level?

Fever itself is not considered dangerous as it is one way the body fights against infection. However, fever is considered dangerous in adults when your body temperature is greater than 105.8°F (41°C). A temperature that high can be damaging to body organs and particularly dangerous for someone with a heart or lung disorder.

What is the critical level?

44 °C (111.2 °F) or more — Almost certainly death will occur; however, people have been known to survive up to 46.5 °C (115.7 °F). Normally death, or there may be serious brain damage, convulsions, and shock. Cardio-respiratory collapse will likely occur.
