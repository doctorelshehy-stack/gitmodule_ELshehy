Fever: Symptoms

Symptoms of Fever

Depending on the cause of fever, other symptoms and signs may be present such as:

  • General weakness
  • Dehydration
  • Irritability
  • Loss of appetite
  • Muscle aches
  • Headache
  • Chills and shivering
  • Sweating

Emergency Level for a Fever

Fever in an adult usually isn't usually dangerous unless it registers 103 degrees F (39.4 degrees C) or higher. It may indicate a serious or life-threatening illness.

Seek immediate medical attention if:

  • Recent use of immunosuppressants
  • Recent travel to areas where serious diseases (e.g., malaria) are endemic
  • Temperature >40°C or <35°C
  • Significant tachycardia or tachypnea
  • Dyspnea
  • Hypotension
  • Petechial rash
  • Headache, stiff neck, or both
  • Altered mental status

Red flags: Temperature >40°C or <35°C • Significant tachycardia/tachypnea • Dyspnea • Hypotension • Petechial rash • Altered mental status
