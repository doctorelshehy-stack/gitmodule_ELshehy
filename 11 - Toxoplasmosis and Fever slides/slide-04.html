Toxoplasmosis: Organism & Transmission

Causative Organism

Toxoplasmosis is caused by the intracellular parasite Toxoplasma gondii; cats and their prey are the definitive hosts.

Route of Transmission

The primary route of transmission to humans is ingestion of tissue cysts from soil, food or water contaminated by cat feces.

Pregnancy transmission: Roughly one-third of women who contract T. gondii during pregnancy transmit the parasite to the fetus, with a 65% risk of transmission if maternal infection is acquired in the third trimester.

Definitive hosts: Cats & their prey

Transmission: Ingestion of tissue cysts

3rd trimester risk: 65% transmission
