5. Enterohemorrhagic E. Coli (EHEC) & HUS

EHEC (O157:H7)

Sources of infection:

  • Contaminated water & milk
  • Meat products (especially hamburgers incompletely cooked)
  • Lettuce and apple juice

Clinical:

  • Abdominal pain and bloody diarrhea without fever
  • Enterotoxins have both local effect on the bowel and distant effect on glomerular apparatus, heart and brain

Antibiotic NOT recommended

Due to increased incidence of HUS after antibiotic therapy

Hemolytic-Uremic Syndrome (HUS)

The major systemic complication

  • Characterized by the triad of acute renal failure, microangiopathic hemolytic anemia and thrombocytopenia
  • Occurs in 10-15% of sufferers, arising 5-7 days after onset
  • Most likely at extremes of age, high peripheral leucocyte count
  • May be induced by antibiotic therapy
  • Treated by: dialysis & plasma exchange
