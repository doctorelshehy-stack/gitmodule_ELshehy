Treatment of Acute Diarrhea

1. Fluid & Electrolytes

Assess dehydration and replace fluid and electrolyte deficits. Dehydration is the cause of death in acute diarrhea.

2. Diet

  • Frequent feedings: fruit drinks, tea, bananas, apple, rice, potatoes, noodles, toast, soups
  • Green bananas, pectins decrease diarrhea
  • AVOID: Dairy products, caffeinated beverages, alcohol

3-5. Ancillary Treatment

  • Anxiolytics (e.g., diazepam 2 mg)
  • Antiemetics make symptoms more tolerable and are safe
  • Zinc supplementation effective in preventing recurrences in malnourished children; improves enterocytes renewal

Food provides calories necessary to facilitate renewal of enterocytes.

6-7. Antibiotics

Antibiotic indicated if:

  • Immunocompromised or elderly
  • Mechanical heart valves
  • Recent vascular grafts

Empiric treatment:

  • Ciprofloxacin (500 mg bid for 3–5 d)
  • Metronidazole for suspected giardiasis (250 mg qid for 7 d)
