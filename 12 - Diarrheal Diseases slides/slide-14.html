Systemic Manifestations & Diagnostic Approach

Systemic Manifestations

Reiter's syndrome:

Arthritis, urethritis, and conjunctivitis may follow infections by Salmonella, Campylobacter, Shigella, and Yersinia.

Hemolytic-uremic syndrome:

Enterohemorrhagic E. coli (O157:H7) and Shigella.

Approach to Acute Diarrhea

The cornerstone of diagnosis in those suspected of acute infectious diarrhea is microbiologic analysis of the stool.

Workup includes:

  • Direct inspection for ova and parasites, fecal WBCs
  • Cultures for bacterial and viral pathogens
  • Immunoassays for: bacterial toxins (C. difficile), viral antigens (rotavirus), and protozoal antigens (Giardia, E. histolytica)

If stool studies are unrevealing → flexible sigmoidoscopy with biopsies and upper endoscopy with duodenal aspirates and biopsies.
