Salmonella & Campylobacter

2. Salmonella

Caused by: S. Enteritidis & S. typhimurium

Source: Contaminating food with feces (poultry, egg products)

IP: 8-48 hours

Clinical Picture:

  • Fever with chills, Nausea and vomiting
  • Cramping and abdominal pain
  • Diarrhea often grossly bloody 3-5 days
  • Reactive (post-infective) arthritis (Reiter's syndrome)

Tx: Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole, ampicillin, Quinolones (if not self-limited)

3. Campylobacter

Caused by: Campylobacter jejuni or C. coli

Source: Foodborne disease — primarily uncooked poultry

Clinical: Diarrhea (bloody ~10%), abdominal pain

Post-infection complications:

  • Guillain-Barre syndrome
  • Acute post-infective polyneuritis (Reiter's syndrome)

Tx: Ciprofloxacin 500 mg twice for 5-7 days. Azithromycin 500 mg orally once for 3 days.
