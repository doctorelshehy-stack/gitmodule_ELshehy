7. Cholera & 8. Giardiasis

7. Cholera

  • History of travel to endemic areas
  • Ingestion of contaminated food, water
  • Massive non-bloody watery diarrhea — "rice water diarrhea", No odor
  • Dehydration occurs quickly
  • Vaccine available but short-lived

Treatment:

Hydration and antibiotics — Tetracycline, ampicillin, azithromycin, TMP-SMX, fluoroquinolones

8. Giardiasis

  • One of the most common causes of diarrhea
  • Transmitted through water, food, and person-to-person
  • Watery diarrhea, foul-smelling soft, greasy stools
  • Abdominal cramps and bloating
  • Infections clear up within six weeks
  • You may have recurrent episodes or have intestinal problems after the parasites are gone

Treatment:

  • Tinidazole (fasigyn) 2 g orally as a single dose
  • Metronidazole (Flagyl) 250mg /8 H/5d
