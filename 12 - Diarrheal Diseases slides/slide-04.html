Definition & Classification of Diarrhea

Definition (WHO)

Diarrhea is defined by the World Health Organization as having 3 or more loose or liquid stools per day, or as having more stools than is normal for that person.

  • Usually a symptom of infection in GIT
  • Can be caused by bacterial, viral and parasitic organisms
  • More dangerous in extremes of age
  • For adults: stool weight >200 g/d can be considered diarrheal

Classification by Duration

  • Acute: <2 weeks
  • Persistent: 2–4 weeks
  • Chronic: >4 weeks

Classification by Blood

Bloody

  • Must evaluate ALL causes
  • Must do: C&S + Sigmoidoscopy
  • May need CT

Non-bloody

  • Most are viral causes
  • Most resolve without treatment
  • If fever, pus, dehydration → evaluate
