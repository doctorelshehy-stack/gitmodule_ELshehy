6. Clostridium Difficile Infection

Overview

  • A history of any antibiotic therapy in last 6 weeks prior to onset can be related to Cl. Difficile (most common is clindamycin)
  • The A and B toxins produced by Cl. difficile can cause severe diarrhea, pseudomembranous colitis, up to toxic megacolon

High risk patients:

Hospitalized pts, old ages, previous attacks, inflammatory bowel diseases

Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis:

  • Stool examination for Cl. difficile cytotoxin
  • Stool culture for Cl. difficile

Treatment:

  • Metronidazole 400 mg 8-hourly for 10 days
  • Vancomycin may be used (125 mg orally 6-hourly for 1 week)
