SIBO — Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth

Pathophysiology

SIBO causes malabsorption of foodstuffs, including fats and carbohydrates due to alteration of gut flora.

  • Normal → gut flora: lactobacilli, enterococci, gram-positive facultative anaerobes
  • SIBO → flora partially replaced with colonic bacteria (coliforms and anaerobes: Bacteroides and Clostridium)
  • → Increased amidase and protease → altered bile salts absorption
  • → Impaired micelle production → fat malabsorption
  • → Increased proteases → reduce villous surface enzymes (disaccharidases) → carbohydrate malabsorption

Predisposing factors:

  • Increased GI pH: PPI therapy, gastrectomy, atrophic gastritis
  • GI stasis or delayed gastric emptying: structural (stenosis, bypass) or functional (pseudo-obstruction, diabetic enteropathy)

Investigations

1. Small-bowel aspiration & quantitative culture (Gold-standard)

Bacterial overgrowth diagnosed when >10⁵ CFU/ml in proximal jejunum. Complex test — not used routinely.

2. ¹⁴C/¹³C D-xylose breath test (Most clinically-applied)

Administration of 1g of ¹⁴C-labelled D-xylose. Positive = detection of ¹⁴C in exhaled breath.

3. Hydrogen breath test

10g lactulose challenge. Positive: >20ppm H₂ in first 90 min (early peak), double peak, or sustained increase >10ppm over baseline.

4. Therapeutic trial with antibiotics

Used when pre-test probability is high and breath test negative/unavailable.
