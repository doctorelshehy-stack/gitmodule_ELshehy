Problem Solving Case

A 23-year-old Female presented with abdominal cramps and bloody diarrhea not associated with mucous, pus or tenesmus. She reported history of eating hamburger outdoors 3 days ago. On examination; she was generally good, temperature 37.1C. There was no history of medications.

A. Name the likely causal infectious agent

Enterohemorrhagic Escherichia coli (EHEC O157:H7)

B. Enumerate 3 complications

Hemolytic-uremic syndrome (HUS): acute renal failure, microangiopathic hemolytic anemia, thrombocytopenia

C. Outline your suggested plan of treatment

Fluid and electrolyte support. Antibiotic NOT recommended due to risk of HUS.
