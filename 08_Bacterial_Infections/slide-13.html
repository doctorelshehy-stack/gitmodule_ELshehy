Pertussis — Clinical Stages

Etiology

Bordetella pertussis (Gram −ve). B. parapertussis: milder. Droplet/direct contact. Incubation 3–12 days.

Key Facts

Infectivity: max during 1st 4 weeks. No trans-placental immunity — newborns susceptible.

Catarrhal (1–2 wks)

URI picture: rhinitis, conjunctivitis, lacrimation, mild cough, low-grade fever. Highest transmission chance.

Paroxysmal (2–4 wks)

5–10 forceful coughs per expiration → massive inspiratory effort → whoop. Associated with vomiting. Not all patients whoop.

Convalescent (1–2 wks)

Paroxysms gradually decrease. Cough may persist for months.

Any cough paroxysms with vomiting → suspect pertussis
