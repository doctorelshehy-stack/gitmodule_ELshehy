Typhoid Fever — Overview

Etiology

S. typhi (typhoid) — S. paratyphi A, B, C (paratyphoid, milder)

Incubation: 10–15 days

Endemic in Egypt (poor hygiene areas)

Transmission

Fecal-oral. Contaminated water, ice, milk, poultry, eggs, animal meats.

General Features

• Insidious onset: fever, headache, anorexia, myalgia

• Discrepancy between temperature (high) and pulse (low) — less common than adults

• Rose spots on lower chest/abdomen at end of 1st week (fair-skinned)

Abdominal Features

• Abdominal pain and distention

• Constipation in 50%; diarrhea in the rest

• Splenomegaly by end of 1st week (soft)

• Coated tongue

Suspect in any prolonged pyrexia with splenomegaly
