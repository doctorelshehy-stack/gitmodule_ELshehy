Bacterial Meningitis — Etiology & Clinical

Definition

Acute meningeal inflammation with purulent exudate. Neurological complications if early adequate TTT not given.

Incubation: 3–4 days

Winter, overcrowded/unventilated areas. Meningococcal may be epidemic.

Etiology by Age

Neonatal

E. coli, Group-B streptococci, Listeria monocytogenes

Childhood

N. meningitidis, Pneumococci, H. influenza type-b (until age 5)

Clinical Manifestations

• Abrupt onset, high fever

• Headache & projectile vomiting

• Bulging anterior fontanelle (<1 yr)

• Drowsiness → stupor → coma (if delayed TTT)

• Generalized convulsions

• Focal neurological signs: cranial nerve palsies, hemiplegia

• Meningeal signs: Neck stiffness, back stiffness, +Kernig, +Brudzinski

• Skin rash: Generalized petechial (meningococcal)
