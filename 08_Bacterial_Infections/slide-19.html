PEDIATRIC GASTRO-ENTEROLOGY, HEPATOLOGY & INFECTIOUS DISEASES

Key Points & Summary

Typhoid Fever

S. typhi. Rose spots, coated tongue, splenomegaly. Widal test after 1st week. Ceftriaxone 2 weeks. Watch for intestinal hemorrhage & perforation.

Brucellosis

Undulant fever from contaminated milk. Hepatosplenomegaly. 2-drug therapy (Streptomycin + Co-trimoxazole/Tetracycline). Pasteurization prevents.

Scarlet Fever

β-hemolytic streptococci. Circum-oral pallor, strawberry tongue, desquamation (hands/feet). Penicillin 10 days. Watch for rheumatic fever & nephritis.

Pertussis

B. pertussis. 3 stages: catarrhal → paroxysmal (whoop) → convalescent. DPT vaccine. Erythromycin 14 days. Cough may persist for months.

Bacterial Meningitis

Medical emergency. Age-specific etiology (neonatal vs childhood). CSF: turbid, ↑protein, ↓glucose, PMNs. Ampicillin + Chloramphenicol/Gentamicin. Treat raised ICP. +Kernig, +Brudzinski, petechial rash (meningococcal).
