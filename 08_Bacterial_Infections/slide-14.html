Pertussis — Diagnosis, Prevention & Treatment

Diagnosis

• Clinical picture (history & exam)

• Leukocytosis + lymphocytosis

• Cough-plate on Bordet-Gengou medium or throat swab

• CXR: hilar infiltrate, atelectasis, emphysema

Prevention

• DPT at 2, 4, 6 mo; boosters at 1.5, 3, 6 yrs

• Contacts: erythromycin 50 mg/kg/d (4 doses) 7 days

• Passive: hyper-immune gamma globulin 1.5 ml for exposed non-immune infants

Treatment

Erythromycin 50 mg/kg/d (4 doses) 14 days. Shortens illness if given early (catarrhal stage). Amoxicillin/ampicillin less effective.

Pertussis immune globulin 1.5 ml/d for 3–5 days (early, <2 yrs).

Supportive Care

• O₂ + aspiration of secretions

• Cough mixtures to inhibit spasms

• Phenobarbitone for convulsions

• Frequent small meals to avoid vomiting

• Avoid precipitants: yawning, sneezing, eating, exertion
