Typhoid — Investigations & Complications

Investigations

• Blood picture: Normocytic normochromic anemia (toxic marrow inhibition), leukopenia with eosinophil disappearance, relative lymphocytosis

• Blood culture: Positive in 1st week

• Stool & urine cultures: Positive after 2nd week

• Widal test: Positive after 1st week. Agglutination against O (somatic) and H (flagellar) antigens. Anti-O titer more significant than anti-H. Chloramphenicol may depress antibody response.

Complications

Intestinal

• Hemorrhage (signs of shock)

• Perforation (signs of peritonitis)

Toxic

• Toxic myocarditis

• Toxic encephalopathy

• Bone marrow depression

Relapse

May occur 1–4 weeks after temperature normalizes
