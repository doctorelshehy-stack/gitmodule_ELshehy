Feature
Achalasia
Scleroderma
Diffuse Esophageal Spasm
Definition
Failure of smooth muscle relaxation at LES. ↑ LES pressure. Progressive loss of peristaltic function.
Systemic disease with vasculopathy & tissue fibrosis (skin thickening).
Normal peristalsis with frequent, repetitive, high-pressure, non-peristaltic waves (tertiary peristalsis).
Etiology
Usually idiopathic. 2° (pseudoachalasia): malignancy, Chagas disease.
Autoimmune, genetic, hormonal, environmental factors.
Idiopathic.
Pathophysiology
Degeneration of Auerbach's plexus → ↑ LES pressure, incomplete relaxation, aperistalsis.
Vascular damage → neuronal dysfunction → esophageal weakening → aperistalsis, ↓ LES tone → reflux → stricture → BE.
Impaired inhibitory innervation, malfunction in endogenous nitric oxide synthesis.
Diagnosis
CXR: no air in stomach, dilated esophagus. Barium: "bird's beak". Endoscopy: normal. Manometry: definitive.
Clinical features of scleroderma. Manometry: ↓ LES pressure, ↓ peristalsis.
Barium: "corkscrew pattern". Manometry: >30% aperistaltic contractions. Endoscopy: normal mucosa.
Treatment
Balloon dilation ± GERD prophylaxis (50% response). Botox (temporary). POEM. Heller myotomy.
Medical: aggressive PPI BID. Surgery: anti-reflux (gastroplasty, last resort).
Reassurance (non-cardiac). Nitrates, CCBs, anticholinergics. Long myotomy if refractory.
