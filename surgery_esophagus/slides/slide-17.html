Carcinoma of the Esophagus

Incidence
• Fastest growing cancer
• Male-to-female ratio: 3:1
Pre-malignant Conditions
• Achalasia
• Plummer-Vinson syndrome
• Tylosis (40% increased risk)
• Esophageal strictures and diverticula
• Genetic alterations (p53 gene)
• Smoking and alcohol
• Food additives (nitrosamines)
• Hot liquids, vitamin/mineral deficiencies
Pathology — Gross
• Annular, scirrhous, ulcer, or fungating mass
Microscopic
1) Squamous cell carcinoma
• Upper and middle third of esophagus
2) Adenocarcinoma (increasing)
• Increasing incidence of GERD
• Western diet
• Increased acid-suppression medications
• Arises from:
— Submucosal glands
— Heterotopic columnar epithelium
— Barrett's esophagus
