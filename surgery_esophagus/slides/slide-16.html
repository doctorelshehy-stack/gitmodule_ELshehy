Esophageal Perforation

Surgical Emergency
• Early detection & repair: 80-90% survival
• After 24 hours: survival ↓ to <50%
Etiology
1) Forceful vomiting (Boerhaave's syndrome)
2) Foreign body ingestion
3) Post-endoscopic instrumentation (most)
4) Penetrating/blunt injuries
5) Pathological (carcinoma)
6) Other iatrogenic causes
Symptoms
• Cervical: neck ache, stiffness
• Thoracic: SOB, retrosternal chest pain
• Abdominal: epigastric pain radiating to back
Signs
• Early: tachypnea, tachycardia, low-grade fever
• Progression: hemodynamic instability, shock
• Subcutaneous air in neck or chest
• Shallow decreased breath sounds
Diagnosis
• CXR: hydropneumothorax
• Contrast esophagram (barium for thoracic)
• Gastrografin for abdominal perforation
• Chest CT: mediastinal air and fluid
Treatment
• ICU + OR management
• Resuscitation, large-bore IVs, antibiotics
• Stable + contained: conservative, stent
• Unstable + free: surgical intervention
— Debridement, diversion/resection
— Esophagostomy, drainage
— Gastrostomy + feeding jejunostomy
