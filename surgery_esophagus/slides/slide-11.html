Sclerodermal Dysmotility & Peptic Stricture

Sclerodermal Esophageal Dysmotility
• Esophageal smooth muscle atrophy
→ ↓ LES pressure and dysmotility
→ Acid reflux and dysphagia
→ Stricture, Barrett esophagus, aspiration
• Part of CREST syndrome
Case:
Severe heartburn resistant to ttt + fingertips ulceration + multiple telangiectasias → CREST syndrome
Esophageal dysmotility → GERD
Peptic Stricture (from Esophagitis)
• Dysphagia alongside long history of reflux
• Reflux symptoms may disappear as stricture develops
• Diagnosed with endoscopy or barium study
• Treatment: Endoscopic dilatation + indefinite PPI
Plummer-Vinson Syndrome Triad
Triad:
1) Iron deficiency anemia
2) Dysphagia
3) Esophageal webs
• RARE (prevalence <1 in 1,000,000)
• Good prognosis when treated with iron + dilatation
• Web = partial occlusion (upper esophagus)
• Ring = circumferential narrowing (lower esophagus)
• Increased risk for esophageal squamous cell carcinoma
Schatzki Ring
• Mucosal ring at squamo-columnar junction
• Causes intermittent dysphagia with solids
• Treatment: Endoscopic dilation
