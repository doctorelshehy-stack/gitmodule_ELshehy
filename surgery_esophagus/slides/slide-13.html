Candida Esophagitis

Endoscopic Findings
• White plaques on erythematous mucosa
• Whitish-yellow plaques without visible ulceration
Histology
• Pseudohyphae and budding spores
• Embedded in necrotic debris
Most Common
• Most common cause of infectious esophagitis
Treatment
• Nystatin swish and swallow
• Ketoconazole
• Fluconazole
Candida Esophagitis — Endoscopic Appearance
• Endoscopic view shows characteristic white/creamy plaques (pseudomembranes) adherent to the esophageal mucosa
• These whitish-yellow plaques appear without visible ulceration
• Histology: Pseudohyphae and budding spores embedded in necrotic debris
• Most common cause of infectious esophagitis
• Treatment: Nystatin, Ketoconazole, Fluconazole
