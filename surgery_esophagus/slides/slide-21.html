GERD — Treatment

1) Lifestyle Modifications
• Decrease transient LES relaxation episodes:
— Cessation of smoking
— Decreased caffeine intake
— Avoid large meals before lying down
• Prevent unfavorable pressure gradients:
— Elevation of head of bed
2) Medical Management
• Double dose PPI as initial approach
• Medical therapy is itself a diagnostic tool
• If symptoms persist → more extensive evaluation
3) Surgical Therapy Indications
• Severe esophageal injury (ulcer, stricture, BE)
• Incomplete resolution on medical therapy
• Long duration of symptoms or young age
• No response on medical treatment
• >10 years life expectancy
Surgical Procedures
Nissen Fundoplication
• Procedure of choice for most patients
• Complete 360° wrap
Partial Posterior (Toupet)
• When esophageal motility is poor
• 250-270° wrap
Anterior Wrap (Thal, Dor)
• No need to disrupt posterior attachments
Postoperative Complications
• Bloating (up to 30%)
• Dysphagia (edema, hematoma, tight wrap)
• Operative failures: persistent symptoms
