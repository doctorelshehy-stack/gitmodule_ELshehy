Carcinoma — Diagnosis

Esophagram
• Barium esophagram recommended for any patient with dysphagia
• Classic "apple-core" lesion in esophageal cancer
• Cauliflower lesion: persistent filling defect
Endoscopy
• Diagnosis best made from endoscopic biopsy
• Document during endoscopy:
— Location of the lesion
— Nature (friable, firm, polypoid, ulcer, stricture)
— Proximal and distal extent
— Relationship to cricopharyngeus, GEJ, gastric cardia
Computed Tomography
• CT scan of chest and abdomen
• Important to assess tumor extent and metastases
Staging
• Multiple modalities available for diagnosis and staging
• Accurate staging crucial for treatment planning
