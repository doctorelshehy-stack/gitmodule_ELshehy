Summary — Esophagus

Key Points
• Esophagus has no serosa — rapid tumor spread
• Three anatomic narrowings — important for foreign bodies
• Dysphagia evaluation: solids vs liquids, progressive vs intermittent
• Motility disorders: manometry is key
• Achalasia: Heller myotomy is definitive treatment
• Boerhaave = surgical emergency
Clinical Pearls
• Painless hematemesis = portal hypertension
• Painful hematemesis = Mallory-Weiss
• White plaques = Candida
• Linear ulcers = CMV
• Punched-out ulcers = HSV
• BE surveillance: every 3 years if no dysplasia
• GERD surgery: Nissen fundoplication (360°)
Remember: Early detection and surgical repair of esophageal perforation within 24 hours results in 80-90% survival.
