HSV & CMV Esophagitis

HSV-1 Esophagitis
• Punched-out ulcers
• Second most common
• Often self-limiting
• Treatment: Acyclovir, valacyclovir, famciclovir
CMV Esophagitis
• Linear ulcers
• Usually presents with odynophagia or dysphagia
• Can be accompanied by fever or burning chest pain
• Endoscopy: linear and shallow ulcerations in lower esophagus
• Biopsy: enlarged cells with intranuclear inclusion bodies
• Treatment: IV ganciclovir, famciclovir, oral valganciclovir
HSV & CMV Esophagitis — Endoscopic Findings
HSV-1 Esophagitis
• Endoscopic view shows punched-out ulcers — discrete, well-circumscribed, round/oval ulcers with sharp borders
• Second most common cause of infectious esophagitis
• Often self-limiting in immunocompetent patients
• Treatment: Acyclovir, Valacyclovir, Famciclovir
CMV Esophagitis
• Endoscopic view shows large, deep, linear ulcers with clean pale-gray bases and raised erythematous edges
• Typically in immunocompromised (AIDS, CD4 < 50)
• Biopsy: enlarged cells with intranuclear inclusion bodies
• Treatment: IV Ganciclovir, Famciclovir, Oral Valganciclovir
