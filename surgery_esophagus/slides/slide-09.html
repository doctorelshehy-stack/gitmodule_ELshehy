DES & Nutcracker Esophagus

Definition
• Periodic, non-peristaltic contractions
• Large amplitude and long duration
• Dysphagia + chest pain (mimics unstable angina)
Pathogenesis
• Impaired inhibitory neurotransmission
• Within esophageal myenteric plexus
Manometry Findings
• Disorganized non-peristaltic contractions
Barium Esophagogram
• "Corkscrew" esophagus
Clinical Presentation
• Intermittent solid/liquid dysphagia
• Chest pain, heartburn, food regurgitation
Treatment — Nonsurgical
• Evaluate for psychiatric conditions
• Nitrates, calcium channel blockers
• Sedatives, anticholinergics
• Peppermint for temporary relief
• Bougie dilation (50-60 French): 70-80% effective
• Botulinum toxin (not sustainable)
Surgical Indications
• Recurrent incapacitating dysphagia/chest pain
• Not responding to medical treatment
• Pulsion diverticulum of thoracic esophagus
• Long esophagomyotomy via left thoracotomy
