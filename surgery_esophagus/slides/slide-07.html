Achalasia — Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis
• CXR: no air in stomach, dilated esophagus
• Barium studies: "bird's beak" appearance
• Endoscopy: normal mucosa
• Manometry: definitive diagnosis
— Uncoordinated or absent peristalsis
— High LES resting pressure
Progressive dysphagia to solids and liquids
— vs obstruction: solids only
Secondary Achalasia (Pseudoachalasia)
• Chagas disease (T cruzi infection)
• Extraesophageal malignancies
— Mass effect or paraneoplastic
Treatment
1) Nonsurgical Treatment
• Sublingual nitroglycerin
• Nitrates or calcium channel blockers
• Bougie dilation up to 54 French
• Botulinum toxin (Botox) into LES
• Balloon dilation: effective in 60%
• POEM (emerging, minimally invasive)
2) Surgical Treatment
• Modified Heller myotomy
— Open, laparoscopic, or robotic
• Partial anti-reflux procedure
— Toupet or Dor fundoplication
• Esophagectomy indicated for:
— Megaesophagus, sigmoid esophagus
— Failed myotomy, undilatable stricture
