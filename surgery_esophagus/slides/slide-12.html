Infectious Esophagitis

Definition
• Severe mucosal inflammation and ulceration
• Due to viral or fungal infection
Risk Factors
• Diabetes mellitus
• Chemotherapeutic agents
• Immunocompromised states
Clinical Features
• Characteristically odynophagia
• Less often dysphagia
• Associated with reflux
Infectious Esophagitis — Types & Endoscopic Findings
Endoscopic Finding Organism
White pseudomembrane → Candida esophagitis
Linear ulcers → CMV
Punched-out ulcers → HSV-1
• Candida — White pseudomembranes/plaques (most common)
• HSV-1 — Punched-out ulcers (2nd most common)
• CMV — Linear ulcers
