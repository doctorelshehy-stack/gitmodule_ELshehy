Barrett's Esophagus

Definition
• Metaplasia of normal esophageal squamous epithelium
• To intestinal columnar epithelium
• Containing goblet-type intestinal mucosa
Etiology
• Acquired via long-standing GERD
• Consequent damage to squamous epithelium
Epidemiology
• 0.5-20% of adults in N. America/W. Europe
• Up to 10% of GERD patients already have BE
• More common: males, age >50, white, smokers
Pathophysiology
• Endoscopy: erythematous epithelium in distal esophagus
• Diagnosis: biopsy with specialized intestinal epithelium
• BE → premalignant changes → adenocarcinoma
Barrett's Esophagus — Anatomy & Histology
Anatomical Regions
• Esophagus → Squamous-lined esophagus
• Columnar-lined esophagus (Barrett's)
• Gastroesophageal junction
• Stomach
Histology: Intestinal Metaplasia
• Squamous mucosa → Intestinal metaplasia
• Characteristic: Goblet cells
Histology: Cardiac Mucosa
• Squamous mucosa → Cardiac mucosa
• Transition zone at GEJ
Key: Metaplasia of normal squamous epithelium → intestinal columnar epithelium containing goblet cells. Acquired via long-standing GERD. Premalignant → adenocarcinoma risk.
