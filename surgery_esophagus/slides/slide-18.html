Carcinoma — Spread & Presentation

Spread
1) Local Spread
• Only two layers to esophageal wall
• Tumors rapidly infiltrate through muscular wall
• Into surrounding structures:
— Trachea, pleura, RLN, diaphragm
• Longitudinal spread through submucosa
2) Lymphatic and Vascular
• Rich vascular and lymphatic supply
• Facilitates spread to regional lymph nodes
• Then to distant lymph node beds
Clinical Presentation
• Early-stage: asymptomatic or mimic GERD
• Most present with:
— Dysphagia and weight loss
• Hematemesis may be presenting symptom
• Tracheoesophageal fistula:
— Choking, coughing, aspiration
• RLN invasion:
— Hoarseness, vocal cord paralysis
• Systemic metastases (liver, bone, lung):
— Jaundice, pain, respiratory symptoms
Key Point:
Most patients present late with dysphagia and weight loss — emphasizing the importance of early screening in high-risk patients.
