Complications of GERD

Esophageal Complications
• Esophageal stricture disease
— Scarring leads to dysphagia (solids)
• Esophagitis
• Ulcer
• Bleeding
Barrett's Esophagus & Adenocarcinoma
• BE and esophageal adenocarcinoma
• Gastroscopy recommended for patients with:
— Chronic GERD
— Symptoms suggestive of complicated disease
— Anorexia, weight loss, bleeding, dysphagia
• Up to 25% of BE patients don't report GERD symptoms
Randomized Trial — NEJM 2019
1523 Patients with PPI refractory heartburn
Randomly assigned to:
• Surgical (Nissen fundoplication): 67% success
• Active medical (omeprazole + baclofen): 28%
• Control medical (omeprazole + placebo): 12%
Surgical treatment significantly greater than medical
