Barrett's — Treatment & Surveillance

Significance
• Malignant transformation: ~0.12% per year
• High-grade dysplasia: 32-59% transformation
— Over 5-8 years of surveillance
• Increased gastric acid secretion more frequent with BE
Treatment
• High-dose PPI indefinitely (or surgical fundoplication)
• Surveillance gastroscopy every 3 years if no dysplasia
High-Grade Dysplasia
• Regular and frequent surveillance
• Intensive biopsy
• Endoscopic ablation/resection or esophagectomy
• Evidence increasingly favoring endoscopic ablation
— Mucosal resection or radiofrequency ablation
Low-Grade Dysplasia
• Surveillance: every 6 months for 1 year, then annually
• Endoscopic ablation/resection also satisfactory
Endoscopic Surveillance Debate
Corley et al. findings:
• No difference in endoscopy rates
• Between BE patients who died of
• esophageal adenocarcinoma vs other diseases
Possible explanations:
• Most adenocarcinomas may not arise from BE
• Esophageal carcinoma is too rare a cause of death
• Morbidity from treatments
• Endoscopic screening not effective in real world
Analogy:
Situation analogous to disappointing value of serum PSA screening for prostate cancer
